# PMF Framework for Candidates

> Evaluate job opportunities the same way you'd evaluate product-market fit: People, Mission, Financials — settling for 2 out of 3 consistently leads to bad outcomes.

## Content

Introduced by [[adam-fishman]] (first growth hire at Lyft, former product and growth lead at Patreon), this framework applies product-market fit thinking to career decisions. Just as a product needs the right market, the right problem, and the right business model to succeed, a job opportunity needs alignment across three dimensions:

1. **People** — Will you learn from and genuinely enjoy working with these people? This includes the hiring manager, immediate team, and cross-functional partners. Culture and management quality compound over time — a toxic environment with a great mission still produces misery.

2. **Mission** — Does the problem the company is solving matter to you? Mission alignment drives discretionary effort and sustains motivation through difficult stretches. Without it, even well-compensated roles become hollow.

3. **Financials** — Is the business on a trajectory that rewards your time? This includes both current compensation and the expected value of equity, growth opportunity, and the company's funding/revenue trajectory.

Fishman's core heuristic: **settling for 2 out of 3 consistently leads to bad outcomes.** A great mission with bad people and weak financials is exhausting. Great people and money without mission feels empty. Great mission and financials with bad people is daily suffering.
