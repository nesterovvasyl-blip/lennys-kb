# Gardening vs. Building

> Builder: effort-in = value-out. Gardener: find systems with compounding potential, plant cheap seeds, water what grows — and let the system do the compounding.

## Content

This framework distinguishes two fundamentally different mental models for creating value:

### The Builder Mindset

The builder executes a plan. Work in → value out. The relationship is roughly linear: more effort produces more output. Builders are essential for execution, but the returns are capped by the effort they can personally apply.

Characteristics:
- Clear plans with defined deliverables
- Success looks like completing the plan
- Value is proportional to time and effort invested
- Predictable, but hard to scale beyond individual capacity

### The Gardener Mindset

The gardener looks for **systems with compounding potential** — situations where a small input can produce disproportionate output because the underlying system does the work. The gardener:

1. Identifies systems that could compound (network effects, content libraries, habits, platforms)
2. Plants **cheap seeds** — low-cost experiments across multiple candidates
3. Observes what grows
4. Waters what's working (invests more in proven compounders)
5. Prunes what's not (kills experiments that don't show signal)

The gardener accepts that most seeds won't grow — that's by design. The outsized returns from the ones that do justify the portfolio approach.

### Why gardening produces outsized returns

The underlying system does the compounding. The gardener's leverage is in identifying and activating those systems, not in doing the work themselves. This is the difference between linear and exponential returns.
