# MOO — Most Obvious Objection

## Overview
MOO (Most Obvious Objection) is a pre-communication mental check: before sharing anything — a Slack message, a presentation, a pitch, a recommendation — spend two minutes asking what the most obvious objections are that you're likely to receive. Anticipating the obvious objections allows you to either address them proactively or frame your communication to preempt them. It is the single tactic Wes Kao most recommends for people beginning to improve their communication.

## When to Use
Before every significant communication: a meeting where you need buy-in, an email requesting action, a strategy doc, a product recommendation, a Slack message to a large channel. Wes uses MOO "multiple times a day, every day" — it has become muscle memory.

## How to Apply

1. **Before you communicate**, ask: "What are the most obvious objections someone will have to what I'm about to say?"
2. **Spend two minutes** — even 30 seconds yields useful answers. You won't anticipate every objection, but you can absolutely anticipate the obvious ones.
3. **Choose your response**: either address the objection proactively in your communication ("you might be wondering if this will require headcount — here's why it won't") or frame your point so the objection is less likely to arise.
4. **At the end of the conversation/doc**, ask yourself what the Most Obvious Objection was that you might have missed.

## Why It Works
- Most people feel blindsided by objections in meetings. The reality is that obvious objections could usually have been predicted. The feeling of being blindsided is a sign of insufficient preparation.
- Anticipating objections forces you to understand your argument (including its weaknesses) more deeply before presenting it. This often surfaces gaps in the argument itself — a chance to strengthen it before it's said out loud.
- Objection-handling done in the moment is reactive and defensive. Objection-handling done in preparation is calm and strengthens your credibility.

## Connection to Other Tactics
- Works alongside **sales then logistics**: selling the why first reduces the most obvious objection ("why are we doing this?")
- Works alongside **preparation for concision**: the same prep that helps you anticipate objections helps you know what your core point is
