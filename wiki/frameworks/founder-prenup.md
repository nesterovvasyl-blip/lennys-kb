# Founder Prenup

> A structured questionnaire for co-founders to complete before starting a company — 65% of startups fail due to founder conflict.

## Content

The Founder Prenup is a pre-commitment exercise adapted from executive coaching practice by Alisa Cohn. Like a marital prenuptial agreement, it surfaces assumptions and incompatibilities before they become conflicts — when the cost of resolution is still low.

### The questionnaire covers

- **Core values**: What do you stand for? What would you never compromise on?
- **Vision of success**: What does winning look like in 5 years? In 10?
- **Conflict styles**: How do you fight? How do you prefer to be challenged?
- **Decision-making when disagreeing**: When you can't reach consensus, how does the tie break?
- **Desired culture**: What kind of company do you want to build? What behaviors are you unwilling to tolerate?
- **Roles and ownership**: Who owns what, and how does that change as the company scales?
- **Exit scenarios**: What would make you want to leave? What would make you want to sell?

### Why it works

The conflict that kills startups is usually not about facts — it's about **unstated assumptions** that turn out to be incompatible. The prenup makes those assumptions explicit while both founders are still aligned on wanting to build together. Revisiting these questions annually also surfaces drift in values or priorities before it becomes irreparable.

### The statistic

65% of startups fail due to co-founder conflict, not market or product failure. This makes the founder relationship the single highest-leverage risk to address before building anything else.
