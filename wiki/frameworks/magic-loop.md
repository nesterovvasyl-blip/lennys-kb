# The Magic Loop

> A five-step self-reinforcing cycle for expanding your career impact and getting promoted — from excellent work to bigger opportunities and back again.

## Content

The Magic Loop is Ethan Evans's core framework for career advancement, developed during his 12+ years rising to VP at Amazon. It explains why talented people plateau: they execute step 1 (excellent work) but skip or underdevelop the other four steps, which are equally necessary.

### The Five Steps

1. **Do Excellent Work** — Not good: excellent. The bar for the Magic Loop to work is higher than most people acknowledge. Excellent work is distinguishable, not just "met expectations." It solves important problems, not just assigned tasks.

2. **Make Your Work Visible** — Excellent work that no one knows about doesn't advance careers. Visibility is not self-promotion — it's stewardship. Write the one-pager that documents what your team shipped and why it mattered. Present the results to stakeholders. Send the update email. This step is where most engineers and introverted ICs get stuck.

3. **Get Recognition** — Formal and informal acknowledgment that your work mattered. This includes: being cited in executive reviews, winning awards, receiving public thanks from senior leaders, being mentioned in others' work. Recognition is a signal that compounds — each instance increases the probability of the next.

4. **Build a Reputation** — Reputation is the lagging indicator that compounds over years. It's what people say about you when you're not in the room. "She's the one who fixed the checkout system." "He's the person to call for platform scaling problems." A strong reputation opens doors before you apply — recruiters reach out, projects find you, sponsors advocate for you.

5. **Get New Opportunities** — Reputation attracts bigger problems. Bigger problems, executed excellently, restart the loop at a higher level. This is the compounding mechanism. Each loop cycle increases the scope of what you're trusted with.

### Why the Loop Breaks

- **Break at Step 2 (Visibility)**: Most ICs and engineers. They do great work and assume it will be noticed. It often isn't.
- **Break at Step 3 (Recognition)**: Mid-career PMs and managers. They make their work visible but don't cultivate the stakeholder relationships that convert visibility into recognition.
- **Break at Step 4 (Reputation)**: Senior individual contributors. They're recognized for specific work but haven't built a cross-functional reputation.
- **Break at Step 5 (Opportunities)**: Senior managers who are known within their domain but not sought out for problems outside it.

### The VP Jump

The biggest loop break is between senior manager/director and VP. To jump to VP, you need to run the loop at a business level, not a team level. This means:
- Your "excellent work" must move business metrics, not just team metrics
- Your "visibility" must reach business unit leaders and company-wide stakeholders
- Your "reputation" must include the phrase "thinks like a business owner"

### Invention as Loop Accelerator

Evans supplements the Magic Loop with a structured practice for generating the "excellent work" that seeds the loop: two hours per month, alone, no agenda, forced combination of two adjacent domains. This is how he generated the novel proposals at Amazon that became the promotable work.
