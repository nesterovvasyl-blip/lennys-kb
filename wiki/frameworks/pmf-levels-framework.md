# PMF Levels Framework

## Overview
A structured four-level model for understanding and progressing toward product-market fit in B2B SaaS companies, developed by Todd Jackson at First Round Capital from patterns across hundreds of early-stage startups. The key insight is that PMF is not binary but a staged journey with three key dimensions (satisfaction, demand, efficiency) that are optimized in sequence rather than simultaneously.

## Components / Steps

### The Three Dimensions
- **Satisfaction**: Customers get real value; they would be disappointed if the product disappeared
- **Demand**: There is a scalable, repeatable channel to find and acquire more customers like the satisfied ones
- **Efficiency**: The unit economics work; each new customer is becoming progressively cheaper to acquire and serve

### Level 1 — Nascent PMF
- **Target**: 3–5 paying customers
- **ARR range**: $0–$500K
- **Team size**: Under 10 people
- **Primary focus**: Satisfaction
- **Efficiency**: Deliberately ignored — Wizard-of-Oz delivery is fine
- **Demand source**: Mostly warm intros; may take 20 intros per customer
- **Signs you're stuck**: Customers wouldn't miss the product; different features used by different customers (looks like consulting); low usage despite payment

### Level 2 — Developing PMF
- **Target**: 5–25 customers
- **ARR range**: $500K–$5M
- **Team size**: Up to 20 people
- **Primary focus**: Demand — crack the first scalable channel
- **Efficiency metrics to watch** (not optimize): Gross margin >50%, burn multiple <5
- **Retention benchmarks**: NRR ≥100%, regretted churn <20%
- **Signs you're stuck**: No inbound momentum; sales cycle too long relative to ACV; churning >20% of logos; customers politely declining ("not the right time")

### Level 3 — Strong PMF
- **Target**: 25–100 customers
- **ARR range**: $5M–$25M
- **Team size**: 30–100 people
- **Stage**: ~Series B
- **Primary focus**: Efficiency — all three dimensions must be maintained simultaneously
- **Efficiency benchmarks**: Gross margin >60–70%, burn multiple <3, NRR >110%, regretted churn <10%
- **Hallmark**: The boulder is rolling downhill; 10%+ of inbound is organic/referral
- **Signs you're stuck**: Growth slowing to below 2x; first channel saturating; efficiency deteriorating

### Level 4 — Extreme PMF
- **Target**: 100+ customers
- **ARR range**: $25M+
- **Stage**: Series C+
- **Primary focus**: TAM expansion (new products, new markets)
- **Benchmarks**: Gross margin >80%, burn multiple <1, NRR >120%, regretted churn <10%, sales conversion >15% (first call to close)
- **Challenge**: Re-finding PMF with each new product/market; never assume existing PMF transfers

## The Four Ps — Pivot Navigation
When stuck at any level, four levers are available:
1. **Persona** — who you target (think market as a collection of people, not a category)
2. **Problem** — the pain being addressed
3. **Promise** — the positioning and value proposition communicated
4. **Product** — what is actually being built

Examples:
- Lattice kept the persona, changed problem + promise + product (OKR tool → performance management)
- Plaid kept the product, changed persona + problem + promise (consumer app → fintech API)
- Vanta changed all four (early failed ideas → compliance automation)
- Ironclad changed promise only (AI legal assistant → CLM category)

Most founders do "10% pivots" when they need "200% pivots" (Jack Altman's framing).

## The Marginal Customer Test
At any level, the best ongoing signal of PMF progression is whether the next incremental customer is getting easier to acquire and serve. If the marginal customer is consistently hard, PMF hasn't progressed regardless of current ARR.

## When to Use
This framework is designed for sales-led B2B SaaS founders in the first 6–12 months, though the level diagnostics apply throughout the first 4–6 years. Not well-suited for consumer product, bottom-up PLG, or hardware.

## Rough Timeline
- L1: 12–18 months (most critical level; picking the right persona and problem)
- L2: ~1 year (rapid if foundations are solid)
- L3: ~2 years (from $5M to $25M)
- L4: ~2 years (from $25M to $100M+)
Total: ~4–6 years to extreme PMF for the best enterprise companies
