# Emerging Channel Evaluation Framework

> Three ingredients for deciding whether to invest in a new acquisition channel — cap exploration at one quarter.

## Content

When a new acquisition channel emerges (a new platform, format, or distribution mechanism), teams face the decision of whether to invest. This framework provides a structured approach using three filters:

**Ingredient 1: Overlap of needs**
Is there genuine alignment between:
- Customer needs (where your audience actually is and what they want)
- Company goals (what you're trying to achieve)
- Channel strengths (what the channel is actually good at delivering)

All three must overlap. A channel that's hot but doesn't align with your customer's behavior is noise.

**Ingredient 2: Channel DNA**
Evaluate the channel itself:
- **Trajectory**: Is it growing, plateauing, or declining?
- **Risk**: Algorithmic dependency, platform risk, cost volatility
- **Monetization**: Does the channel support your conversion model?

**Ingredient 3: Company DNA**
Evaluate your own organization's fit:
- **Risk appetite**: Can you absorb a failed experiment?
- **Team capacity**: Do you have the skills to execute well?
- **Existing channel mix**: Does this complement or cannibalize what's working?

### Time constraint

Cap initial exploration at **one quarter**. If you can't generate signal within that window, the channel is not the right fit at this time. This prevents zombie experiments that drain resources without resolution.
