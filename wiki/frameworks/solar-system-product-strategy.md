# Solar System Product Strategy

> A portfolio metaphor: one core product acts as the "sun" while satellite products orbit it — used by the NYT for its bundle strategy.

## Content

The Solar System framework is a way of thinking about product portfolio strategy for companies with a dominant core product and a desire to expand.

### The Sun: Core Product

The sun is the brand-defining, audience-generating flagship. For the New York Times, this is news. The sun provides:
- **Brand heritage and trust** — the reason people pay attention
- **Audience funnel** — the top of the acquisition funnel for everything else
- **DNA** — the editorial and product values that define what "NYT quality" means

The sun is not necessarily the highest-growth product in the portfolio, but it is the gravitational center. Everything else is defined in relation to it.

### The Satellites: Adjacent Products

Products like NYT Cooking, Wordle, The Athletic, and NYT Games orbit the sun. They share the sun's DNA — quality, craft, trust — but serve distinct needs and may appeal to different segments. Key properties:
- Each satellite must be able to stand on its own editorially and financially
- Each satellite benefits from the sun's brand and distribution
- The bundle (satellite + sun) is worth more than the sum of parts

### Strategic implication

When evaluating whether to build or acquire a satellite, ask: does it share the sun's DNA? A satellite that contradicts the core brand's values creates gravitational instability — it weakens the sun rather than orbiting it.
