# Hill Climb: Local vs. Global Optimum

> The hardest part of reaching a higher peak is climbing down from the one you're on.

## Content

Ami Vora's metaphor for navigating transformative change in products, companies, and careers.

**The setup:** You're standing on a hill (your current local optimum). You can see rolling hills, sheep, grass — things are working pretty well. But in the distance, you see a mountain (a global optimum) that's even higher than the summit you can see.

**The dilemma:** To reach the mountain, you must:
1. Climb down from your current hill
2. Cross an unknown valley
3. Climb back up to the same level you started at
4. Keep climbing to reach the new summit

There's no guarantee you'll make it. You're giving up something that works for something uncertain.

**Real-world examples:**
- Companies optimized for desktop who could see the mobile mountain but had to sacrifice core desktop business to get there
- Career transitions — leaving a role where you're succeeding for one with higher potential
- Product platform migrations

**What gets you through the valley:** Remembering what the summit feels like. The feeling of being on top — "this was absolutely worth it, my life is better in these ways."

**When to use it:**
- Setting expectations with teams: "It's going to be a slog. That's by design — we're in the valley."
- Deciding whether a transformation is worth the cost (most of the time, the answer is yes)
- Personal career decisions involving short-term loss for long-term gain
