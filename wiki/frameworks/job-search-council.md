# Job Search Council

> A peer support group of 6-8 job seekers who commit to supporting each other through the process — combining accountability, emotional support, structured methodology, and shared market intelligence.

## Overview

The centerpiece of Phyl Terry's Never Search Alone methodology. Inspired by Phyl's mother, who organized a council of teachers in 1960 that met for 50 years until her death. Proven across downturns including the dot-com depression of 2000-2002.

The core psychological insight: when anxious people gather and are invited to be open and vulnerable and to ask for help, anxiety converts into hope, motivation, accountability, and confidence. This is "baked into human psychology" — it's the hack that makes group support more powerful than solo effort.

Scale as of 2024: 2,000+ councils launched, completely free, funded by Phyl's book sales and 20,000+ hours of volunteer time. Average job search completion in a council: 3 months vs. 3-6 months national average.

## Components / Steps

**Structure:**
- 6-8 job seekers per council
- **Fast seekers** (out of work): meet twice per week
- **Slow seekers** (searching while employed): meet every two weeks
- **Moderator**: a volunteer job seeker who runs the sessions; gets matched faster and receives extra training

**Process:**
1. Apply at phyl.org (free)
2. Get matched (also matched by fast/slow seeker status and willingness to moderate)
3. Attend orientation training on the methodology
4. **Meeting Zero**: open, vulnerable sharing. Members share who they are, their stories, what they're going through. Builds trust before the work begins.
5. Move into structured methodology: Mnookin two-pager → listening tour → candidate market fit statement → job mission + OKRs → interview prep → negotiation

**Materials:**
- 100-page workbook with all templates and guides
- Community Slack for ongoing connection
- Bi-weekly LinkedIn Live sessions with Q&A
- Recruiter network for candidate market fit listening tours

## Application

**Who it's for:** Anyone in a job search. Built by and for the product community, but explicitly applicable to anyone. Different configurations for fast and slow seekers.

**Why groups work better than solo search:**
- When one member feels demoralized, others provide perspective and momentum.
- Shared learning on candidate market fit: seeing others' Mnookin two-pagers surfaces things you forgot about what you want (or don't want).
- Interpretation support during the listening tour — some feedback is biased, sexist, or misleading; council members help parse it.
- Accountability: monthly update notes to your full network is something you'll actually do when your council expects you to.
- Multiplied listening posts: when five council members are each doing listening tours, market intelligence flows back to the whole group.

**The "I in village" principle:** Being in a council doesn't mean becoming passive or dependent. It means having independent support that enables you to be more accountable and responsible — the "I" in the village. The council gives you the emotional backing to take bolder, better-informed action.

**Moderator advantage:** Volunteering to moderate a council gets you faster matching and more training. The coaching that comes with the moderator role is often reported as one of the most valuable aspects of the program.
