# PMwheel

> An eight-bucket assessment framework for evaluating and developing product managers across all dimensions of the PM role — used by product leads to coach their teams and by PMs to identify development priorities.

## Overview

Petra Wille created the PMwheel to give herself (as a product coach) and product leads a structured starting point for development conversations. She found that coaches and managers often entered conversations without a clear shared map of what "good" looks like for a PM. The PMwheel provides that map.

Unlike simple skills lists, the PMwheel is designed as a self-assessment + manager assessment + team member assessment — capturing the full 360 view of how a PM operates. Each of the eight buckets contains 15+ framing questions, making it comprehensive while still being navigable.

## Components / Steps

**Bucket 1: Understand**
Can the PM identify the underlying problems that users and the business actually have — not surface symptoms, but root causes? Are they connecting to genuine user and business needs?

**Bucket 2: Find Solutions**
Are they good at generating and evaluating solutions to those problems? Do they have methods for ideation, and are the solutions genuinely address what was discovered in Bucket 1?

**Bucket 3: Plan**
Can they create roadmaps and product goals that align the team? Are they able to translate vision into actionable priorities?

**Bucket 4: Get It Done**
Can they actually ship — working with engineers, designers, and QA to deliver working product? Does the team trust them to be a good execution partner?

**Bucket 5: Listen and Learn**
After shipping, can they gather and synthesize both qualitative and quantitative data to understand what actually happened? Can they iterate based on real usage, not assumptions?

**Bucket 6: Team**
Do they understand the dynamics of working with a team rather than individuals? Do they know how to motivate, align, and communicate within a product team? Can they navigate cross-functional relationships?

**Bucket 7: Personal Growth**
Are they intentionally investing in their own development — reading, experimenting, reflecting? Petra puts this on the wheel because she wants it to be a standing conversation topic, not an afterthought.

**Bucket 8: Agile**
Do they understand the underlying values and principles of agile ways of working — not just the ceremonies (standups, sprints, retros), but the *why* behind them? Many PMs have never read the Agile Manifesto or reflected on agile values even after years of working in agile environments.

## How to Apply

**For product leads running a coaching program**:
1. Ask each PM to complete a self-assessment (rate themselves 1–5 on each question per bucket)
2. Complete your own assessment of them independently
3. Optionally: request two to three team members complete a brief assessment
4. Use the gaps and convergences as the starting point for a development conversation
5. Identify one or two buckets to focus on in the next development plan — not all eight simultaneously

**Starting from scratch**: Petra's advice is to start with a development plan conversation rather than waiting to complete a full assessment. Ask the PM what they want to improve — most have an answer. Use the PMwheel to add structure and completeness to whatever they identify.

**Customization is required**: The PMwheel is a template, not a universal prescription. A primarily technical PM org may weight Bucket 2 (solutions/technical) differently. A more enterprise-oriented org may weight Bucket 3 (planning) more heavily. Never copy-paste — adapt to your context before use.
