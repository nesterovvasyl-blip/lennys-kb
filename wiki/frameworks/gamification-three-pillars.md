# Gamification Three Pillars

> Habit-forming product design: Core Loop, Metagame, and Profile — the framework behind Duolingo's engagement engine.

## Content

Attributed to Jorge Mazal at Duolingo, the Three Pillars framework explains how to design products that build durable habits rather than one-time engagement spikes.

### Pillar 1: Core Loop

The tight, daily action-reward cycle at the heart of the experience. Every session, the user does something and gets something back — immediately. At Duolingo, this is the lesson → XP → streak feedback cycle. The core loop must be:
- Short enough to complete in a single session
- Rewarding enough to motivate the next session
- Consistent so it becomes automatic

### Pillar 2: Metagame

Long-term progression mechanics that sit on top of the core loop. Leaderboards, achievement badges, level systems, and streaks. The metagame answers "what am I building toward?" and gives the core loop stakes beyond the session.

Without the metagame, the core loop feels repetitive. Without the core loop, the metagame has nothing to build on.

### Pillar 3: Profile

A persistent record of investment — the user's history, achievements, and identity within the product. The profile makes accumulated progress visible and creates **identity commitment**: "I'm a person who does this." Quitting means abandoning an identity, not just skipping a session.

### Why all three are required

Each pillar addresses a different churn risk:
- No Core Loop → sessions aren't rewarding enough to start
- No Metagame → sessions don't build toward anything, engagement decays
- No Profile → investment isn't visible, switching costs are low
