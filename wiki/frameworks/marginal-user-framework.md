# Marginal User Framework

> Focus on the user just on the cusp of converting — they reveal the full spectrum of product problems.

## Content

The Marginal User Framework reorients product research from satisfied power users to the users who are barely making it work. These are the people on the threshold of adoption — they have the need and some motivation, but something is blocking them.

### Two research modes

**Quantitative (data)**: Identify where marginal users are. What devices, connection speeds, geographies, or segments are underrepresented in your conversion funnel? The data surfaces the population.

**Qualitative (observation)**: Once identified, watch them. Don't ask what they want — observe what they actually do. What breaks? Where do they stop? What do they misunderstand?

### The worst-case lens

A particularly powerful application: study your **worst-case marginal users** — the person on a feature phone, on a 2G connection, in a low-literacy context. Their experience surfaces friction that is invisible to your typical user and your team. Fix those problems and you improve the product for everyone.

### Why marginal users over power users

Power users have adapted to your product's quirks. They've learned the workarounds. Marginal users haven't — they experience the product as designed, which means they expose design failures that power users have long since stopped seeing.
