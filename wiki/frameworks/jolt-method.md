# JOLT Method

> A four-step sales framework for unsticking indecisive buyers, derived from analysis of 2.5 million recorded sales conversations by Matt Dixon and Ted McKinnon.

## Overview

The JOLT Method addresses the 56% of no-decision losses that stem not from status quo preference but from buyer fear of failure — what Dixon calls FOMU (Fear of Messing Up). Standard sales techniques like FOMO pressure or urgency creation backfire on this segment 87% of the time. JOLT is specifically designed for buyers who want to buy but can't commit.

## The Four Steps

### J — Judge the Level of Indecision

Determine whether the buyer is stuck in status quo preference or fear of failure — they look identical from the outside but require completely different responses.

**Technique: Pings and echoes**
- Articulate what you think is worrying the buyer and listen for confirmation or correction
- Don't ask directly ("are you feeling indecisive?") — frame it as "many customers at this stage worry about X"
- Listen for the "echo" — does the concern land with recognition?

If the buyer is stuck on status quo, use Challenger-style urgency. If they're stuck on fear of failure, use J-O-L-T.

### O — Offer a Recommendation

Shift from asking what the customer wants to recommending what they should do.

- At late-stage deals, too many options overwhelm rather than empower
- The "delegation effect": sharing the decision burden reduces buyer anxiety
- Sound like an advisor: "Here's the option I'd recommend and why" — not "here are 10 options, your choice"
- Under-promise on configuration: "We'll help you configure this step-by-step, you won't be on your own"

### L — Limit the Exploration

Help the buyer stop doing endless research. Indecisive buyers often stall because they believe one more article, one more vendor call, or one more demo will give them certainty it never will.

Two techniques:
1. **Brutal transparency early**: Proactively acknowledge what you're not good at ("we're not the best at X"). This builds trust faster than polished salesmanship and makes the buyer stop looking for the thing you're not.
2. **Demonstrate expertise**: When the salesperson becomes the most authoritative source the buyer has found, they stop researching externally and rely on the rep. The goal is to become the trusted advisor who filters the market for them.

### T — Take Risk Off the Table

Address the buyer's fear of being blamed if things go wrong.

Techniques:
- **Under-promise on ROI**: Quote minimum achievable outcomes ("we see at minimum 5X in every implementation"), not average or best-case. This sets up over-delivery and creates advocates.
- **Safety nets**: Pilot programs, customer success roadmaps, professional services add-ons, clear escalation paths — anything that shows the buyer they won't be jumping alone.
- **Reference stories**: Specific customers in similar situations who succeeded, named and available for reference calls.
- **Reversibility signals**: Where possible, make the first commitment feel smaller — a pilot, a phase, a limited rollout — so the stakes of saying yes feel lower.

## When to Use JOLT

JOLT applies specifically when:
- The buyer has expressed intent to purchase but is stalling
- The buyer goes quiet after saying they want to move forward
- Standard urgency-based techniques have failed or made things worse
- The buyer keeps asking for more information without reaching clarity

It does NOT apply to buyers who are genuinely on the fence about whether they want the solution at all. That requires a different approach (Challenger, diagnosis, reframing).
