# Backcasting

> A strategic planning technique that starts with a desired future state and works backward to identify the steps required to reach it — the opposite of forecasting.

## Content

Backcasting is the inversion of conventional strategic planning. Standard planning extrapolates from the present: given where we are and the trends we observe, where might we be in 3-5 years? Backcasting begins with a desired future state and asks: what has to be true for us to reach it?

Christopher Lochhead ([[christopher-lochhead]]) applies backcasting specifically in the context of category design. Category designers don't ask "where will the market go?" (forecasting). They ask "what market do we want to exist, and how do we design our way to it?" (backcasting).

### The Process

**Step 1: Define the desired future state**
Write a clear, concrete description of what success looks like in 3-5 years — not financial metrics but *market reality*. What category exists that doesn't exist today? Who are the customers, and what problem is solved for them? How is the world different because your company succeeded?

Lochhead recommends a "Day 1 Press Release" variant: write the announcement that a journalist would publish on the day your category is fully established. What does it say? Who quotes it? What problem did your company solve?

**Step 2: Identify the prerequisites**
Work backward from the desired future. What has to be true in year 2 for year 3 to be possible? What has to be true in year 1 for year 2 to be possible? What has to be true in Q1 for year 1 to be possible?

This step often reveals category design prerequisites: the thought leadership that needs to exist, the analyst relationships that need to be built, the customer case studies that need to be created, and the language that needs to be established in the market.

**Step 3: Map the lightning strike timing**
Category design requires coordinated market moments — "lightning strikes" — where product, thought leadership, analyst relationships, and customer evidence all appear simultaneously. Backcasting helps identify when these lightning strikes need to happen and what needs to be in place before each one.

**Step 4: Design the present**
Based on the prerequisite map, determine what the company must start doing right now to be on track. This often means investing in activities (analyst relations, book writing, community building) that don't pay off for 12-24 months.

### Backcasting vs. Forecasting

| | Forecasting | Backcasting |
|---|---|---|
| Starting point | Present reality | Desired future |
| Question | "Where will we be?" | "Where do we want to be, and how?" |
| Mindset | Extrapolation | Design |
| Risk | Bounded by current trajectory | Requires belief in a different future |
| Best for | Operational planning | Strategic vision and category creation |

### Application Beyond Category Design

While Lochhead applies backcasting to category strategy, the technique is broadly applicable:
- **Product roadmaps**: Start with the product experience you want users to have in 2 years; work backward to determine what must ship when.
- **Hiring**: Start with the team you need to execute on 3-year ambitions; work backward to determine when to hire whom.
- **Personal career planning**: Claire Vo ([[claire-vo]]) implicitly uses backcasting in her career design framework — start with a concrete 3-year career goal, then identify what has to be true at 18 months, 12 months, and 6 months.
