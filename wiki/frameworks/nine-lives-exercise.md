# Nine Lives Exercise

> A life-design exercise where you write nine completely different 5-year life scenarios to break out of incremental thinking and discover what you actually want.

## Content

Developed by [[graham-weaver]] for use in his Stanford GSB "Personal Leadership: Life and Career Design" course.

**The exercise**: Write nine different versions of your life — nine distinct 5-year scenarios. The key constraint: they must be genuinely different, not variations on a theme. Not "PM at a startup" and "PM at a FAANG" — those are the same life. Nine genuinely different trajectories might include: founder, artist, athlete, nonprofit leader, academic researcher, politician, craftsperson, investor, back-country homesteader. The wilder the range, the more useful the exercise.

**Why nine specifically**: Most people naturally generate 2-3 life scenarios when asked to brainstorm. These tend to be close variations on their current path, bounded by what they've already considered plausible. Nine forces the brain beyond the comfortable range. By the seventh or eighth scenario, you've usually had to write down something you've never seriously considered — and sometimes that's where the insight is.

**What to write for each scenario**: For each of the nine, write:
- What are you doing day-to-day?
- What's your relationship to work?
- What are you building or creating?
- What are your key relationships?
- How do you feel in this life?

The last question is the most important. You're looking for emotional data, not logical evaluation.

**What to do with them**: After completing all nine, review them and notice:
- Which ones created energy just by writing them?
- Which ones felt like relief to read (as if you gave yourself permission)?
- Which ones felt dull or obligatory?
- Are there themes across multiple scenarios — values or activities that recur?

The exercise doesn't produce a definitive answer. It produces better data for the [[genie-framework]] and other decision tools.

**Common insight**: Many people find that one or two scenarios they thought were impractical produce the most energy when they actually write them out. The act of writing gives the idea enough substance to feel real. This often reveals that what seemed impossible was mostly unfamiliar — not actually impossible.

**Relationship to other tools**: The nine lives exercise is a broad exploration tool used before the [[genie-framework]] (which narrows to a choice) and before values work. Sequence: nine lives (expand option space) → genie framework (identify preference without fear) → values check (verify alignment with inner scorecard) → incremental experiments (test the hypothesis with low-cost action).
