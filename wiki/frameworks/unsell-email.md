# Unsell Email

> A hiring practice of sending candidates a candid description of the job's hard parts before making an offer — to filter out those who will join and be disappointed.

## Content

The unsell email is a pre-offer communication technique where the hiring manager honestly describes the difficult, unglamorous, or frustrating aspects of the role before extending an offer. Popularized by Kevin Yien (former Stripe/Square PM).

### How It Works

After deciding you want to make an offer, send an email to the candidate that covers:
- The real challenges of the role (not the polished recruiter version)
- The friction or frustration they should expect
- The things that won't be obvious from interviews
- Honest context about the team, the work style, or the current state of things

Then make the offer.

### Why It Works

The logic is counterintuitive: telling candidates the hard parts reduces offer acceptance, which sounds bad. But:

1. **Best candidates lean in** — people who are genuinely motivated by the work aren't deterred by honest difficulty. They appreciate the candor and often become more interested, not less.

2. **Misaligned candidates self-select out** — candidates who back out after the unsell email would have churned within 6–12 months anyway, once they encountered the reality you described.

3. **Sets correct expectations** — candidates who accept after reading the unsell email join with clear eyes. They're less likely to feel blindsided or deceived.

4. **Signals the hiring culture** — sending an unsell email signals that the team values honesty over sales. This is itself a filter for candidates who value that culture.

### What to Include

- Specific current challenges ("we're in the middle of a platform migration that will dominate the first 6 months")
- Cultural friction points ("we have a strong engineering culture that can feel slow to product people")
- Role ambiguity or scope constraints ("you won't own the full roadmap for at least a year")
- Workload or pace expectations ("the team moves very fast and responds to messages outside business hours")

### What Not to Include

The unsell email is for honest difficulty, not for making the role sound unappealing gratuitously. Don't include problems that you're actively fixing or that are truly temporary. The goal is accurate representation, not discouragement.
