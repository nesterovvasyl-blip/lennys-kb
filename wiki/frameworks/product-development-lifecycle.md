# Product Development Lifecycle

> A four-phase framework for product development with phase-based commitments: Discovery, Solutioning, Build, Launch/Measure/Iterate.

## Content

Annie Pearl describes Calendly's product development lifecycle as four sequential phases, each with a distinct commitment level:

1. **Discovery** — Research and evaluate a problem space. Commitment: we'll complete this investigation by a target date. No commitment on whether we'll proceed.
2. **Solutioning** — If the problem is worth pursuing, explore solutions, do user testing, and land on an approach. Commitment: we'll have a solution recommendation by a target date.
3. **Build** — With a known problem and validated solution, engineering can do proper estimation and commit to a delivery date.
4. **Launch, Measure, and Iterate** — Ship, measure outcomes, and iterate based on data.

**Key insight: commit only to the phase in front of you.** Teams can commit to completing a discovery phase, but should not promise a delivery date for a project that hasn't even completed discovery. This avoids the classic failure mode of committing to a ship date before enough is known about the problem or solution.

Annie notes: "We've gotten a lot better at making commitments around the work that's right in front of us versus making a commitment around a project six months out when we haven't even done enough discovery, enough design and ideation to have a real clear understanding of estimation."

**Supporting practices at Calendly:**
- OPA (Opportunity/Problem Assessment) meetings where PMs debate and discuss problems without leadership present
- Templates embedded in Confluence that reinforce target customer, user needs, and competitive differentiation at each phase
- Quarterly milestones on annual OKRs to track progress without over-committing to distant dates
