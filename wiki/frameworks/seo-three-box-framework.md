# SEO Three-Box Framework

## Overview
A quick-check framework for deciding whether your product is positioned to win with SEO before committing significant resources to it. Check two of three boxes to proceed.

## Components / Steps

**Box 1: Unique editorial angle**
Do you have a genuinely differentiated perspective on "how to" searches in your space? Not just "we can write good content" but a point of view, unique expertise, or first-party research that no existing publisher can match. If the editorial landscape is saturated with well-resourced incumbents and you bring nothing new, SEO is an uphill battle.

**Box 2: Programmatic angle**
Can you generate long-tail search pages at scale from data, templates, integrations, or structured content? Canva's template strategy ("wedding invitation template," "instagram post template") is the canonical example. Zapier's integration pages ("connect X to Y") is another. Real estate platforms doing location pages is another. If your product can generate thousands of unique, useful, discoverable pages from your underlying data or catalog, this box applies.

**Box 3: Unique data angle**
Do you have proprietary data from user behavior that could power original insights no one else can produce? Monarch Money (personal finance) can see anonymized spending patterns. A health app can surface population-level trends. This data can become content (studies, benchmarks, trend reports) that earns organic search presence through novelty and citation.

**The decision rule**: Check two of three before committing serious SEO resources. One box is probably too thin. Three boxes is ideal.

## When to Use
- Pre-investment evaluation: before hiring an SEO agency or building an in-house SEO function
- At Series A–B when thinking about diversifying away from paid acquisition
- After getting paid acquisition under control and looking for lower-CAC channels
- Before committing to a 6-month SEO experiment, which Yuriy recommends time-boxing to 3 months for initial validation

**Additional guidance from Yuriy:**
- SEO returns nothing for 6+ months; it compounds slowly but defensibly once established
- Getting an outside audit (5–10K) from an agency is usually worth it before committing internal resources — they have templates and benchmarks you'd spend many human hours assembling yourself
- Canva and Grammarly illustrate two different archetypes: Canva's programmatic SEO was its primary early growth loop; Grammarly had no inherent SEO angle and relied heavily on performance marketing instead
