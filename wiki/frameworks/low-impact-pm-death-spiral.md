# Low Impact PM Death Spiral

> Matt LeMay's term for the self-reinforcing cycle that traps product teams in activity without outcomes — shipping features that don't move meaningful metrics.

## Overview

The Low Impact PM Death Spiral is Matt LeMay's diagnosis of a dominant dysfunction in modern product organizations. It describes how teams get trapped optimizing for busyness and delivery rather than business outcomes — and how the cycle becomes self-reinforcing.

## The Cycle

1. **No clear goals** → PMs fill the vacuum with roadmap delivery
2. **Roadmap delivery becomes the metric** → Teams optimize for shipping
3. **Shipping feels like impact** → Nobody questions whether it mattered
4. **Leaders see teams as execution machines** → They don't invest in goal-setting
5. **Loop repeats** — deeper each cycle

Each rotation makes the spiral harder to escape: the team's skills and incentives adapt to the delivery mode, leaders stop expecting strategic input from PMs, and the muscle of outcome-oriented thinking atrophies.

## Why It's Hard to See

The spiral is invisible from inside because:
- Busyness looks productive
- Shipped features are tangible evidence of work
- Metrics exist (velocity, release cadence, JIRA completion rate) — just the wrong ones
- The team is collaborative and responsive, which all feels like good PM behavior

The diagnostic question: "In the last quarter, what metric did you move by how much as a result of what you shipped?" If nobody can answer confidently, the spiral has taken hold.

## How to Break It

LeMay's "impact-first" antidote requires three steps:

1. **Set clear, measurable goals** — not "improve retention" but "increase 30-day retention from 42% to 50% by Q3"
2. **Connect daily work to those goals** — every backlog item must trace to a defined goal or it gets deprioritized
3. **Make the connection visible** — dashboards and rituals that show progress toward goals, not feature output

The hardest part: step 1. Most product organizations have never written down specific, measurable, time-bound goals at the PM/team level. Doing so forces uncomfortable prioritization conversations that the delivery mode allowed everyone to avoid.
