# Lighthouse Users Program

## Overview

The Lighthouse Users Program is a structured early-customer development framework created by [[tanguy-crusson]] at Atlassian during the development of Jira Product Discovery. It solves two failure modes of new product development:

1. **Premature scale**: Exposing a product to too many users before it's ready, causing churn that is very hard to recover from
2. **Aggregate research blindness**: Hiding behind CSAT scores and NPS rather than maintaining direct, name-level customer relationships

The name comes from the role lighthouses play: a small, fixed reference point that guides navigation for everyone else. The first 10 customers are the lighthouse — understood deeply, representative of the broader target market, guiding every product decision.

## The Three Stages

### Stage 1: The 10 (Alpha)
Work intensively with exactly 10 customers. They are in your Slack workspace. You meet on Zoom regularly. You build features specifically for their problems.

The critical discipline: **document explicitly why these 10 represent your future customer base.** They aren't arbitrary early adopters — they are a deliberate proxy for the market you intend to serve.

Success measurement is entirely qualitative at this stage: snippets of customer conversations showing the problems they have and demonstrating how the product solves them. No MAU targets, no retention metrics.

Engineering teams that participate in direct customer conversations at this stage become **product engineers** — they push back in planning with "we talked to Maria last week and she struggles with X, let's fix that." This is one of the highest-leverage outcomes of the program.

### Stage 2: 10 → 100 (Expanded Alpha / Early Beta)
Recruit more customers, but still in a curated, high-touch way. Goals:
- Test whether the core solution works across different customer profiles and use cases
- Capture subtleties and edge cases that only emerge at variety (different security needs, workflow variations, integrations)
- Still primarily qualitative; metrics start to appear but aren't the primary signal

### Stage 3: 100 → 1,000 (Self-Service Beta → GA Readiness)
The bottleneck shifts: can the product work without white-glove onboarding? Teams should be spending significant time at the 10-100 stage answering support tickets, doing Zoom calls, and explaining the product manually. The signal that you're ready to graduate: you can reliably onboard and retain without that hands-on help.

Once proven at 1,000 self-service customers, the product is ready for general availability.

## Why This Works

**The "Safety Funnel" principle**: Rather than maximizing the number of people who enter the top of the funnel, limit the number of people who have bad experiences. Premature scale with a bad product creates permanent brand damage in the segment you're trying to win — customers who tried it and churned are much harder to re-engage than customers who have never heard of you.

**The climate change analogy**: Tanguy observes that people read IPCC reports without changing behavior. But if they know someone personally affected by climate change, they act. The same is true of product teams — aggregate research data produces intellectual understanding; direct relationships with real customers produce motivation to act.

## Applying to Stakeholder Communication

The Lighthouse Users Program also provides a communication framework for internal stakeholders. Instead of defending the product against "when will this be in front of more customers?", teams can clearly state: "We're currently at Stage 1: working with our 10 lighthouse users. Here's what we're learning. Here's when we expect to move to 10→100."

The stakeholder response shifts from frustration at low numbers to curiosity about the qualitative signal.

## Relationship to Point A

At Atlassian, the Lighthouse Users Program was embedded in the **Wonder-Explore-Make-Impact** incubation framework. The Make stage explicitly used lighthouse user stages as its internal success milestones, replacing the usual MAU or revenue targets that would be meaningless at this scale.
