# Opinionated Defaults

> Use data from best-performing users to identify optimal behaviors, make those the default, and add friction when users deviate — guiding toward success while preserving choice.

## Content

Opinionated Defaults is a product design philosophy that treats defaults not as neutral starting points but as active recommendations informed by data.

### The process

1. **Identify best-performing users** — segment users who achieve the core outcome (retention, revenue, engagement) most successfully.
2. **Reverse-engineer their behavior** — what settings, configurations, or choices did they make? What do they have in common?
3. **Make those behaviors the default** — new users land in the configuration most likely to lead to success.
4. **Add friction to deviation** — when users try to move away from the recommended default, surface a warning or confirmation step. Don't block them, but make them pause.

### Why it works

Most users don't have enough context to make optimal decisions at setup. Defaults have enormous leverage — the majority of users never change them. Opinionated defaults shift the distribution toward success without removing autonomy.

### Example: Patreon pricing

At Patreon, data showed that creators with 3–5 pricing tiers and a $3–5 entry-point tier performed best. New creators were defaulted into that configuration. Deviating (e.g., setting a $50 minimum) required an extra acknowledgment step.
