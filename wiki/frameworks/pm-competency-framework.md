# PM Competency Framework (12 Competencies)

> Twelve universal product management competencies across four domains, applicable from APM to CPO — the scope changes with seniority, not the framework itself.

## Overview

Originally developed by Ravi Mehta at Tripadvisor to structure a rotational PM program that hired directly from business school and undergrad. The program needed to clearly define what product management *is* and how to grow the skill — so they created this structured decomposition. It has since become one of the most widely shared PM frameworks.

The twelve competencies remain constant regardless of seniority. An APM thinks about product execution as individual contribution; a CPO thinks about it as building systems for teams to execute. The underlying competency is the same.

## Components / Steps

### Domain 1: Product Execution (Building)
1. **Functional specification** — Defining what to build; writing PRDs and functional specs that teams can execute against
2. **Product delivery** — Working with engineering and design to ship quality product on schedule
3. **Product quality** — Ensuring the product meets a high bar technically, in design, in UX, and in business terms

### Domain 2: Customer Insight (Understanding What to Build)
4. **Data fluency** — Using quantitative data to understand users, identify opportunities, and make informed decisions
5. **Voice of the customer** — Being the customer advocate throughout the organization; gathering qualitative insight
6. **User experience design** — Thinking holistically about the experience (not just UI — includes APIs, ML systems, developer experience)

### Domain 3: Product Strategy (Connecting to the Business)
7. **Business outcome ownership** — Moving from "shipping features" to "driving business outcomes"; understanding how the product creates value
8. **Product vision and roadmapping** — Sequencing work coherently toward the product strategy over time
9. **Strategic impact** — Sequencing *outcomes* (not just features) toward company strategy; seeing the multi-year arc

### Domain 4: Leadership (Influencing and Scaling)
10. **Stakeholder inclusion** — Rallying engineering, design, data, marketing, legal, and other partners around product work
11. **Team leadership** — Developing direct reports as PMs (only relevant once you have them)
12. **Managing up** — Winning support and resources from leadership; translating product work into business impact

## How to Apply

**For self-assessment:** Rate yourself on each competency (needs focus / on track / outperforming). Ask your manager to do the same independently. Compare — the gaps and agreements spark the most productive development conversations.

**For feedback:** Use the framework to move from surface-level feedback ("your PRDs could be better") to root-cause feedback ("I think there's a gap in functional specification — here's what I'm seeing and why I think that's the underlying issue").

**Exponential feedback:** When you tie feedback to underlying competencies rather than symptoms, the person can work on the root cause. That compounds: fixing the competency gap prevents the same symptom from recurring in future contexts.

**For career planning:** Different PM archetypes cluster around different competencies. Growth PMs cluster around data fluency and business outcome ownership. Product discovery PMs cluster around voice of the customer and vision. Identifying your natural cluster helps target roles and development.
