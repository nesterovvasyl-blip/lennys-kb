# Three Questions to End Every Meeting

> Close every meeting with three questions that create alignment, accountability, and information cascade.

## Content

This is a simple facilitation habit recommended by Alisa Cohn for leaders to close every meeting — whether a 1:1, team standup, or executive review.

### The three questions

1. **What did we decide?**
Surfaces misalignment immediately. Two people can sit through the same conversation and walk away with different understandings of the outcome. Saying it out loud catches the divergence in the room, not in an email chain three days later.

2. **Who needs to do what by when?**
Converts discussion into accountable action items. "We should look into that" is not an action item. "Maria will pull the cohort data by Friday" is. The "by when" is as important as the "who" — without a deadline, the task has no gravity.

3. **Who else needs to know?**
Creates the information cascade. Most meeting follow-up failures aren't about the people in the room — they're about the stakeholders who weren't there and weren't informed. This question makes information propagation an explicit step rather than an afterthought.

### Why it works

The framework is so lightweight it takes less than two minutes. But it closes three common failure modes simultaneously:
- Decision ambiguity (question 1)
- Accountability gaps (question 2)
- Communication silos (question 3)

Applied consistently, it changes the culture of meetings from discussion forums into decision-making and action-generating machines.
