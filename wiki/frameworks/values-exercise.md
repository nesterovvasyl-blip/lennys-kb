# Values Exercise

> A 15-minute exercise to select and stack-rank your 3–5 core values — used as a filter for every major career and life decision.

## Content

Described by [[ada-chen-rekhi]] (executive coach, co-founder of Notejoy), the Values Exercise is a fast, structured tool for building a personal [[inner-scorecard-outer-scorecard]].

**How to run it:**
1. Set a 15-minute timer
2. Browse or brainstorm a list of candidate values (common ones: learning, adventure, family, integrity, impact, kindness, creativity, autonomy, stability, wealth)
3. Select the values that resonate most strongly with your lived experience — not aspirationally, but descriptively
4. Stack-rank your selections down to 3–5 core values
5. Write them down in order

The output is a personal decision filter. When facing a major choice — whether to take a job, accept a promotion, change teams — run it through the ranked values. Does this decision serve your top value? Does it actively conflict with it?

Ada recommends revisiting the exercise at career inflection points. Values can shift over a decade, and a scorecard that was accurate at 28 may not reflect who you are at 38.

The exercise complements the [[curiosity-loop]]: external input from trusted advisors + internal clarity from your values scorecard together provide a reliable signal when a decision feels stuck.
