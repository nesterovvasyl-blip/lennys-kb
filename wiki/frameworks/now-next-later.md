# Now/Next/Later Roadmap
> A roadmap format that replaces Gantt-style timelines with three horizon buckets — Now, Next, Later — acknowledging the cone of uncertainty while preserving strategic direction.

## Content

Invented by Janna Bastow and her co-founder Simon Cast at ProdPad, the Now/Next/Later framework emerged from a very practical problem: every ProdPad user kept requesting a "move all these items forward by a month" button. This signaled that the real issue wasn't tooling — it was that nobody was delivering timeline roadmaps on time, and the format was forcing false precision onto inherently uncertain future work.

**The three buckets:**

- **Now** — Work currently in progress or starting imminently. Items here typically have specific scope, defined success metrics, and rough delivery expectations (weeks, not months). Dates are acceptable here if truly needed.
- **Next** — Problems or opportunities likely to be tackled in the next horizon (typically the next quarter or two). Less specific than Now; defined more by the problem to solve than the solution.
- **Later** — Directional priorities further out. Intentionally vague. These are bets and direction, not commitments.

**Why the format works:**

The underlying principle is the *cone of uncertainty*: the further out you plan, the less certainty you have. A Gantt chart applies equal date-commitment pressure to all items regardless of how far out they are, which is epistemically dishonest and creates coordination overhead when dates slip (which they always do). Now/Next/Later encodes honest uncertainty into the format itself.

It does not mean no dates. Items with genuine hard dates (regulatory deadlines, seasonal launches) still get dates — but only those items. The insight: by not requiring dates on everything, you prevent the planning overhead of assigning, tracking, and re-estimating dates for work you can't actually estimate.

**Roadmap as prototype for strategy:**

Bastow's framing: a roadmap is not a plan, it's a *prototype for your strategy*. Just as a design prototype lets you test assumptions about a feature, a roadmap lets you test assumptions about your strategic priorities. You share it, get feedback, revise it. The value is in the roadmapping process, not the roadmap artifact.

**Integration with soft/hard launch cycle:**

In practice, Now/Next/Later solves the marketing-engineering date coordination problem by decoupling:
- *Soft launch*: when engineering ships (on their schedule)
- *Hard launch*: when marketing announces (on their schedule, with a working product to work from)

This removes the stress of trying to align two fundamentally different project types against the same date.

## Alternatives and Context

- Gantt-based roadmaps are appropriate when the work is truly fixed-scope and fixed-date (e.g., contracted deliverables, regulatory compliance).
- Some companies use quarterly OKRs as a roadmap substitute — the Now/Next/Later fits naturally alongside OKRs as a problem-discovery layer above the key results.
