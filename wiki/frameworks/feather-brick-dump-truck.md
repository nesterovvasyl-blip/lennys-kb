# Feather-Brick-Dump Truck Framework

> An early warning system for burnout: your body signals stress in progressively louder ways — feather, brick, dump truck. The goal is to notice and respond at the feather stage, not the dump truck.

## Overview

Developed by [[jonny-miller]], founder of Nervous System Mastery. The framework names three escalating stages of burnout signals to help people catch burnout early, before it becomes a health or career crisis.

## The Three Stages

### Feather (Early Warning)
Subtle, easy-to-dismiss signals:
- Waking up feeling slightly tired or less rested than usual
- Mild irritability or shorter fuse than normal
- Slightly lower quality sleep
- Minor reduction in enthusiasm for work you normally enjoy

The feather is easy to ignore or rationalize ("I just had a bad week"). This is exactly when it's most important to pay attention.

### Brick (Middle Stage)
Clearer, harder-to-ignore signals:
- Losing your cool in situations that wouldn't normally trigger you
- Arguments or strained relationships — especially in close relationships
- Feeling frustrated and terrible without clear cause
- Disproportionate emotional reactions

By the brick stage, significant emotional debt has accumulated. Course correction is still possible but requires real intervention.

### Dump Truck (Crisis)
Full-blown consequences:
- Health crisis (illness, injury, chronic condition)
- Complete inability to function (unable to get out of bed)
- Relationship breakdown
- Being fired, forced resignation, or burnout collapse

At the dump truck stage, recovery is measured in months or years, not weeks. The damage is done.

## Why People Miss the Feather

### Low Interoceptive Capacity
Many high-achievers in tech have spent years in their heads — solving problems cognitively, disconnected from body signals. Miller describes this as being "numb from the neck down." Without body awareness (interoception), feather-level signals simply don't register.

### Normalization
In high-performance tech culture, sustained stress is normalized. "I've felt like this for months" becomes the baseline. Once you stop comparing to a well-rested baseline, the feather disappears.

### Misattribution
Feather signals are often attributed to external circumstances ("it's just a hard quarter") rather than a growing deficit in the nervous system.

## How to Catch the Feather

1. **Build interoception**: regular body check-ins (the APE framework: Awareness, Posture, Emotions) create the sensitivity to notice subtle signals
2. **Track your baseline**: know what "fully rested" feels like so you can detect deviation
3. **Monitor these early metrics**: sleep quality, emotional reactivity, enthusiasm for work you normally enjoy, quality of close relationships
4. **Use breathwork practices morning and evening**: they create a downshifting habit that prevents debt accumulation

## Relationship to Emotional Debt

The feather-brick-dump truck sequence maps directly to emotional debt accumulation. Each unit of stress that doesn't complete its mobilization cycle (doesn't get to full relaxation on the other side) adds to allostatic load. The feather is the moment when debt is small and manageable. The dump truck is when the debt becomes unpayable without a major intervention.
