# AI Monetization Two-by-Two

> A framework for choosing the right AI pricing model based on two axes: how well you can attribute value to your AI (Attribution) and how autonomously the AI acts without humans (Autonomy).

## Content

Madhavan Ramanujam introduced this framework in 2025 to help AI founders navigate the proliferation of pricing models. The core insight: **how you charge matters more than how much you charge** for AI companies, because the business model has shifted from "pay for software access" to "pay for work delivered."

### The Two Axes

- **Attribution**: Can you credibly prove that your AI produced specific, measurable value (revenue gained, costs saved, KPIs moved)?
- **Autonomy**: Does your AI act independently, without humans in the loop, to complete tasks?

### The Four Quadrants

| | Low Autonomy (copilot mode) | High Autonomy (agentic) |
|---|---|---|
| **Low Attribution** | **Seat/Subscription** — classic SaaS; value exists but can't be measured or attributed. Example: Slack in its early days. Appropriate when AI is a productivity enhancer with diffuse benefits. | **Usage-Based** — AI acts autonomously but value is hard to attribute to specific outcomes. Common in infrastructure/backend AI. More you use it, more you pay. Consumption is a proxy for value. |
| **High Attribution** | **Hybrid** (Seat + Consumption) — AI improves measurable outcomes (e.g., coding speed, reduced ticket time) but humans are still in the loop. Example: Cursor (attribution: coding time saved; autonomy: still copilot). Best approach: baseline seat + AI credit/token layer. | **Outcome-Based** ← The Golden Quadrant. AI completes work autonomously AND you can prove the value. Example: Intercom Fin ($0.99 per resolved ticket), Chargeflow (25% of recovered chargebacks). Vendors can capture 25–50% of value created. |

### How to Use the Framework

1. **Identify your current quadrant** based on your product's actual attribution and autonomy levels today.
2. **Don't rush to outcome-based** if you can't prove attribution — you'll fail to defend the pricing.
3. **Plan a roadmap** to move toward the golden quadrant: build dashboards and value audits that demonstrate attribution; build agentic capabilities to increase autonomy.
4. **Coding as a category example**: GitHub Copilot → seat-based → Cursor moves to hybrid → future AI developer agents move toward outcome-based (attributable, autonomous code delivery).

### Why This Matters in 2025

As of recording: ~5% of AI companies are in true outcome-based pricing. Madhavan predicts 25% within 3 years. The companies that get there first and prove the attribution+autonomy model will have:
- 2–5x the pricing power of hybrid or seat-based competitors
- Built-in customer alignment (you only pay when it works)
- A durable moat (the attribution data and agentic capabilities are hard to replicate)
