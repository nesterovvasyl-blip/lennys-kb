# Curiosity Loop

> A structured method for gathering contextual advice — user research for your life.

## Content

The Curiosity Loop is a five-step framework for soliciting high-quality, personalized advice from a curated set of people:

1. **Craft a specific, unbiased question** — avoid leading questions; be precise about the context and what you actually want to learn.
2. **Curate 5–10 respondents** — mix domain experts (people with relevant experience) with people who know you well (who can contextualize advice to your situation).
3. **Make the ask lightweight** — reduce friction so respondents can reply quickly; a short email or voice note beats a lengthy survey.
4. **Process responses looking for surprises** — patterns are useful, but outliers and unexpected angles are where the real insight lives.
5. **Close the loop** — follow up with respondents on what you learned and what you did. This builds goodwill and encourages future candor.

The framework treats personal decision-making with the same rigor as product user research — structured, iterative, and insight-driven rather than relying on a single mentor or gut feel.
