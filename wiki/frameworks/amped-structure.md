# AMPED Structure

## Overview
Miro's cross-functional product organization structure: Analytics, Marketing, Product, Engineering, and Design — all embedded together within each product stream. The key innovation is that product marketing is a core member of every product team, not a downstream handoff function.

## Components / Steps

**A — Analytics**: Data and insights embedded in the team from the start. Every shipped feature is measured against a pre-defined metric commitment.

**M — Marketing (Product Marketing)**: Product marketing embedded inside product teams, not in a separate marketing department. Responsible for positioning, competitive differentiation, packaging, and ensuring internal and external stakeholders can articulate the value of what's being built.

**P — Product**: Product management, setting direction, writing the problem definition (P0) and solution framing (P1).

**E — Engineering**: Engineers embedded in product discovery from the beginning — joining user research sessions, design jams, and editorial planning. Enables faster feasibility assessment and deeper understanding of the why.

**D — Design**: Designers co-creating from discovery, not just executing on specs.

## When to Use
- Teams building products in competitive markets where positioning is as important as the product itself
- Organizations where product marketing has historically been disconnected from product development
- Any team where features ship but customers don't understand or adopt them (often a positioning problem, not a product problem)

## Why It Matters

Most product organizations have product marketing downstream: the team builds, then hands off to marketing to position and communicate. By the time marketing is involved, positioning is constrained by the product decisions already made. AMPED inverts this: product marketing is in the room when the product is being defined, so competitive differentiation and positioning shape the product from the start.

Varun Parmar's observation: "PMs are more acting as product owners or more focused on core problem and solution but not thinking about positioning." In markets with strong competition (Miro competes with Microsoft, Figma, and others), everything built must be looked through the lens of "why is this better than the alternative the customer is already using?"

At Miro, with ~450 PMs and 1,800 total employees, AMPED is the unit of the entire product org — streams (persona-based teams) are AMPED units. The product org meeting is all five functions together, not PMs separately.
