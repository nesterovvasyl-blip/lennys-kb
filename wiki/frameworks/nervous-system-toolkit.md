# Nervous System Toolkit

> A collection of body-based practices for managing nervous system state — calming anxiety, building energy, preventing burnout, and making better decisions.

## Overview

Developed by [[jonny-miller]], founder of Nervous System Mastery (nsmastery.com). The toolkit is based on the foundational principle: **State over story** — change your physiological state first, and thoughts/feelings update downstream. There are 4x more neural pathways from body to brain than from brain to body. Working bottom-up (body to brain) is dramatically faster and more effective than working top-down (thought to body).

## Calming Practices (Downregulation)

### 4-4-8 Breathing
Most accessible and versatile calming practice.
- Inhale through nose: 4 counts
- Hold: 4 counts
- Exhale through nose: 8 counts (long exhale activates parasympathetic)
- Repeat for 1–2 minutes
- Scale down if needed: 3-3-6 or 2-2-4

**When to use**: Before a presentation, in a tense meeting, as a morning practice, when feeling anxious.

### Humming
- Take a full breath in, then hum through the nose until the end of the exhale
- Let out a sigh if it comes naturally
- Releases nitric oxide (vasodilator), tones the vagus nerve, reduces eye tension

**When to use**: Before Zoom calls, when looking at a screen too long, to reset after stressful interaction.

### Expanding Awareness
- Become aware of the space behind you, above you, to your sides, below you
- Relax your gaze (defocus slightly) rather than narrowing focus
- Expanding spatial awareness triggers parasympathetic activation

**When to use**: In a busy room, during social anxiety, anytime narrowly focused attention is creating stress.

### NSDR (Non-Sleep Deep Rest / Yoga Nidra)
- Lie down with eye mask
- Follow guided audio (Miller has recorded versions)
- 14–20 minutes
- Includes guided body scan (also trains interoception)
- Optimal window: 1–3 PM (afternoon energy dip)
- Reported effect: equivalent recovery to a 2-hour nap

**When to use**: Afternoon recovery, days when feeling depleted, as a way to pay down emotional debt.

## Activating Practices (Upregulation)

### Espresso Breath
- Series of rapid exhales through the nose from the lower belly
- 2–3 rounds of 30 pumps
- More gentle than Wim Hof; use if Wim Hof feels overwhelming

**When to use**: Lethargic in the afternoon, before needing intense focus, instead of coffee.

### Physiological Sigh (Huberman)
- Full inhale, then a short second inhale on top
- Instant acute reset

**When to use**: 5-second reset in a stressful moment; immediate state change.

## Body Awareness (Interoception)

### APE Check (Awareness, Posture, Emotions)
A structured body check-in:
- **Awareness**: Am I narrowly focused (activating) or expanded (calming)?
- **Posture**: How is my body positioned?
- **Emotions**: What sensations and moods am I noticing in my body?

**When to use**: Morning body scan with tea, before important meetings, when something feels "off" but you can't name why.

### Email Apnea
Many people unconsciously hold their breath while checking email (term coined by Linda Stone). Noticing and correcting breathing patterns during email is a practical entry point to interoception practice.

## "If This, Then Breathe" System
Build a personal recipe map:
- If overwhelmed → humming
- If anxious before presentation → 4-4-8 breathing
- If lethargic in afternoon → espresso breath
- If can't sleep → NSDR

Having the recipe pre-decided means you're more likely to remember and use it when you need it most.

## Practice Protocol

**Daily minimum (7–10 days to build the habit)**:
1. 2 minutes of 4-4-8 breathing in the morning before work
2. APE check during morning tea or before first meeting
3. NSDR 1–2x per week in the afternoon

The goal of morning practice: train your nervous system to remember these tools exist, so you'll reach for them in the moment rather than defaulting to anxiety or cognitive reframing.
