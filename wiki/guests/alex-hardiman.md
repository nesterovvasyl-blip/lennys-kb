# Alex Hardiman

> Chief Product Officer at the New York Times, former CPO at The Atlantic and news product lead at Facebook.

## Content

Alex Hardiman leads product at the NYT across news, cooking, games, audio, and advertising. Previously CPO at The Atlantic and spent two years at Facebook leading news product during the turbulent post-2016 period.

### Key Ideas

- **Essential Subscription bundle**: The NYT is building a bundle of news, games, cooking, sports, audio, and shopping targeting ~135M English-speaking readers, aiming for 15M subscribers by 2027.
- **Solar System Product Strategy**: News is the "sun" (heritage, trust, audience funnel) with satellite products (Cooking, Games, Wirecutter, The Athletic, audio) orbiting it, sharing the same DNA of trusted journalism.
- **Full-Stack Product Ownership**: PMs at the NYT own journalism, distribution, AND product — unlike tech platforms that only own software/distribution.
- **Editorial-Trained Algorithms**: Training recommendations on editorial judgment signals (importance scores from journalists) rather than pure engagement metrics.
- **Wartime Product Management**: During COVID, the NYT blew up roadmaps, built a public COVID database from scratch, made coverage free — half the country visited the site at the pandemic's height.
- **Mission over metrics**: Business goals serve the mission (seek truth, help people understand the world), not the other way around.

### Notable Quotes

> "Our impact and our business goals are in service of our mission, which is to seek the truth and help people understand the world, not the other way around."

> "Product managers are becoming very editorially minded and we're also getting editors to become more product minded."
