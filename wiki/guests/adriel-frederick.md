# Adriel Frederick

> VP Product at Reddit X, former Director of Product at Lyft and early PM at Facebook leading user acquisition.

## Content

Adriel Frederick is VP of Product Management at Reddit X, where he incubates and scales new products. Previously Director of Product at Lyft (marketplace and pricing) and an early PM at Facebook (user acquisition for four years).

### Key Ideas

- **Diversity as product velocity**: Teams that mirror global user demographics resolve design debates faster and catch blind spots homogeneous teams miss entirely.
- **Sustainable growth > growth hacking**: Growth comes from grinding on core user actions (find product, enter product, find friends) rather than stacking tricks.
- **Marginal User Framework**: Study users just on the cusp of converting — especially worst-case users — to reveal the full list of product problems. Data shows where they are; qualitative observation shows what's actually wrong.
- **Human-Algorithm Interface**: Machines optimize well but can't handle strategic constraints or long-term effects. The PM's job on ML products is designing the boundary between human judgment and algorithmic execution.
- **Lead Bullets and Cannonballs**: Portfolio approach — few large foundational bets (cannonballs) plus many iterative improvements (lead bullets). Ratio shifts with product maturity.
