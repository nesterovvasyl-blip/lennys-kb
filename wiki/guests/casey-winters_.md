# Casey Winters

> One of the most prolific growth and product advisors in tech; former CPO at Eventbrite; Reforge partner.

## Content

Casey Winters built his career in growth at Grubhub (where he grew the company dramatically through SEO and organic), then as an early growth hire at Pinterest, then as CPO at Eventbrite for three years. He is one of the founding partners at Reforge, the product and growth education company. His advisory roster includes Pinterest, Reddit, Canva, Airbnb, Tinder, Thumbtack, Fair, and Hipcamp, making him one of the most cross-company-experienced practitioners in growth.

His 2022 return appearance on Lenny's podcast focused on two main ideas: the "zero interest rate phenomenon PM" and network effects taxonomy.

### Key Ideas

- **The Zero Interest Rate Phenomenon (ZIRP) PM**: During the ZIRP era, every startup operated like a well-resourced public company. PMs who came up in this environment learned to follow processes and frameworks rather than exercise judgment. When asked to solve a problem without data, they defaulted to "I can't begin to answer this without research." Casey's diagnosis: PMs forgot that the job is to add value to customers, not to follow a framework. The fastest way to learn is often to ship, not to research. Frameworks are tools in a toolkit, not a coloring book to stay inside.

- **How to interview PMs**: Casey is deliberately contrarian — he doesn't ask about work history or offer prepared behavioral questions. He gives real scenarios from the role and watches how candidates reason without data or analyst support. Red flags: inability to generate solutions under uncertainty, suggesting solutions that would take months to get signal from, and inability to think holistically about what metrics to move vs. track.

- **Founder intuition vs. team expertise**: When founders find PMF, they accumulate subconscious intuition about customers and business that's very hard to transfer. New hires — even senior ones — need to be directed more than founders expect, not delegated to immediately. The transition to trusting team expertise should be gradual and earned. Casey's framework: track the signals of when founder intuition wanes (founder has too many domains to stay deep in any) and when team expertise surpasses it — employees should proactively signal "I've got this" OR "I need direction."

- **CPO survival**: The CPO is a company executive first and product executive second. You must create the genuine perception that you care about sales, marketing, legal — not just product. Share a transparent diagnosis of product strengths and weaknesses with peers and a credible timeline to improve. Product is a confusing discipline for non-product executives; transparency builds the trust that gives you room to operate.

- **Network effects taxonomy**: Three types: (1) **Direct** — every new user makes the product better for all existing users (WhatsApp). (2) **Cross-side** — two distinct user types, adding one side helps the other (Grubhub restaurants/diners). (3) **Data** — quality improves as more data accumulates (Pinterest recommendations). Social networks look like direct NE businesses but must evolve to cross-side (ads, creators) and data NE (personalization) to be monetizable. Every marketplace that reaches scale needs to evolve its NE strategy.

- **Grubhub's near-miss**: Casey initially opposed building a delivery driver app because he couldn't see how to drive adoption among restaurant-owned drivers. The founders overruled him. In hindsight, this was right — DoorDash and Uber Eats with their own driver networks made delivery tracking table stakes, and Grubhub needed the infrastructure. He missed the long-term horizon.

### Actionable Advice

- When a founder or employee is confident they know the answer, they should say so clearly rather than giving performative delegation. Founders should tell teams what to do when they know the answer, not pretend they don't.
- Use research as a scarce resource: in consumer at scale, ship to learn and bring in research only when impact is confusing or leverage is unusually high. In enterprise, talk directly to customers and sales.
- When you can't persuade a founder, figure out what kind of evidence they respond to (data, peer CEO conversations, customer demos) and build that case rather than repeating the same argument.

### Notable Quotes

> "Every new person on the product team is acting like they work at Google and have these infinite resources and infinite time to make sure everything is perfect. It became such this focus on the right way of doing product management that no one's taken any risk."

> "At Reforge, we're building frameworks that are tools in a toolkit. You pull them out when relevant. They're not a coloring book to stay inside the lines of."

> "The job is not to follow the process. The job is to figure out how to add value to customers that translates into value to the business."

> "A network effect is when a product or a business gets better with more users or customers using it."
