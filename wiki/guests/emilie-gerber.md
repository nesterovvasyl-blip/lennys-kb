# Emilie Gerber

> Founder of Six Eastern, a PR and communications firm specializing in tech startups — the practical guide to getting media coverage that actually moves the needle.

## Content

Emilie Gerber founded Six Eastern after working in PR at major tech companies. Her episode is one of the most actionable guides to startup PR in the Lenny's Podcast canon — no fluff, just a clear framework for how to pitch reporters, which publications matter for what goals, and how to think about PR ROI for early-stage companies.

### Key Ideas

- **Publication-Goal Fit**: Not all press is equal and not all publications serve the same purpose. Gerber provides a clear mapping:
  - **TechCrunch**: Fundraising announcements — best for signaling momentum to other investors
  - **Axios**: B2B deals, fintech, partnerships — business development credibility
  - **Business Insider**: Founder journey stories, personal narrative
  - **Forbes**: Awards (30 Under 30, etc.), credibility building
  - **VentureBeat / The Information**: AI/deep tech — technical credibility
  - **Fast Company**: Future of work, culture, progressive business practices

- **Warm Intros Are Not Required**: Contrary to popular belief, cold outreach to reporters can work. The key is extreme relevance — you need to show the reporter immediately why their audience cares. Gerber gives a concrete structure: one sentence on what happened, one sentence on why it's news (trend, timing, conflict, data), one sentence on why this reporter specifically.

- **Reference Incumbents, Not Category Creation**: Startup PR often makes the mistake of leading with "we're creating a new category." Reporters hate this because their readers don't have the context. Instead, reference the incumbent you're displacing or improving on — "we're doing X like [known company] but for [specific use case]." This creates immediate comprehension.

- **Morning Brew Coworking Series**: Gerber highlighted a specific tactic: Morning Brew's series on coworking spaces and the future of work is an underused vehicle for founders trying to reach operators and investors. Pitching this type of ongoing series is different from pitching news — you're pitching yourself as a recurring source, not a one-time story.

- **PR is a Multiplier, Not a Channel**: Gerber's framing — PR alone doesn't drive signups or revenue. It works as a multiplier when someone already knows about you and needs a credibility signal to convert. The typical path: investor sees TechCrunch article → pings a connection → investment conversation starts. Not: random reader sees article → signs up. Founders who measure PR by direct traffic will always be disappointed.

- **When to Hire a PR Agency**: Not at seed. At Series A or later, when you have a recurring story to tell and product-market fit to defend. Before that, founders should do their own PR — the founder's authentic voice is more compelling than agency-polished pitches, and it builds relationships that pay off later.

### Actionable Advice

- Map your PR goals to the right publications before pitching — don't spray the same pitch to every outlet.
- Lead pitches with the story, not the product. What's happening in the world that makes your company relevant right now?
- For cold pitches: follow the reporter for 2-3 weeks first, understand their beat, then reference a specific recent piece in your pitch.
- Avoid buzzwords like "revolutionary," "game-changer," or "disrupting." Every pitch says this.
- Track your press as a sourcing asset — a good press clipping file is a recruiting and fundraising tool, not just a marketing asset.

### Notable Quotes

> "A reporter's inbox is full of founders who think they're doing something unprecedented. If you want to stand out, explain who you're beating."

> "PR is not a customer acquisition channel. It's a credibility infrastructure layer."
