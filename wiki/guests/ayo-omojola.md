# Ayo Omojola

> CPO at Carbon Health; co-creator and scaler of Square's Cash Card and Cash App; angel investor; former founder.

## Content

Ayo Omojola co-created the Cash Card at Square and helped scale Cash App from sub-50K actives to 50M+ monthly actives. He is currently CPO at Carbon Health (health tech), an angel investor in companies like Mercury and Modern Treasury, and board member at Pinwheel. He is also the co-founder of Mailform (print and mail API).

### Cash App's Success Was 10 Things, Not One
Ayo resists single-cause explanations of Cash App's success. The compounding of multiple advantages created the moat: exceptional talent density, extreme focus (consumer over merchants always), fraud sophistication, great design, and one crucial differentiator: **instant availability of money**. Instant was the cut-through: "Try and send me a dollar that I can use now" — for years there was only one app you could do it with.

### Differentiation Must Be Better AND Different AND Matter to Users
"Different" is easy (just don't do what others do). "Better" is easy (charge more for a marginal improvement). The hard part: being better in a way that **matters to the end user** in a domain that has real economics behind it. All four conditions need to be true.

### Going Deep: The Art of Real Understanding
Before Cash App launched the Cash Card, Ayo spent months visiting card manufacturing factories to understand laser engraving — going through 1,000+ combinations of settings before finding the right formula. His broader principle: the people product builders typically treat as "experts" are often not the most expert people in the world on a topic — you have to go deeper. This shows up in regulatory work too (sitting with the regs and asking "what does this actually mean?").

### Startups Within Companies: Small, Senior, Trusted Teams
The one thing Ayo would tell founders building within a company: small, tightly-knit, **senior** teams. Smallness reduces miscommunication; seniority creates trust and the ability to navigate a large organization. Cash App stayed small and senior relative to its GMV scale for a long time. Headcount should track success, not hope.

### Hiring Founders (and Its Costs)
Ayo deliberately hires people who have started companies (regardless of outcome) because they are exceptional — and because they see organizational waste immediately and call it out. The trade-offs: (1) they will cut through all bullshit visibly, which creates friction; (2) they leave in ~2 years. Result: high-output, high-attrition team. Worth it.

### Long Relationship Cycles in Hiring
Ayo's meta-philosophy: **you pick the people, but they pick when.** Build relationships early, add value without an agenda (make intros, share knowledge), and when the timing aligns, the hire is easy. He made ~600 introductions in one year. The enabling belief: if someone wants something you can provide at no cost, it's almost immoral not to provide it.

### Switching Domains via Deep Regulatory Skills
Moving from fintech (Cash App) to health tech (Carbon Health): Ayo repositioned as someone who gets really good at regulated industries — where going deep into complex regulations creates durable product moats — rather than just being good at money specifically.
