# Ray Cao

> Global Head of Monetization Product Strategy & Operations at TikTok, former Google Shopping lead, who built TikTok's go-to-market organization from 2 people to a global operation.

## Bio

Ray Cao is the Global Head of Monetization Product Strategy & Operations at TikTok, where he has spent over four years building out their advertising and monetization business. Before TikTok, he spent six years at Google helping scale Google Shopping globally. He brings a unique cross-cultural perspective on how tech organizations operate at global scale.

Note: The transcript file for this episode is labeled as "Marty Cagan" in the title but the actual interview content is with Ray Cao.

## Key Ideas

### Context, No Control

TikTok's most important cultural operating principle. Rather than confining people to narrow job descriptions, TikTok encourages every employee to see themselves as a business owner with full visibility into the company's problems. "Context, no control" means giving people all the information they need and then trusting them to think beyond their immediate responsibilities. Ray contrasts this with the "black and white box" approach — telling people exactly what they can and cannot do — which he found actually slows things down by creating human-made silos.

The principle has a practical formula: proactive thinking + reactive doing. Employees should think broadly about business problems (proactive), but when they actually take action, they must collaborate with the right owners (reactive). This prevents chaos while still breaking down silos.

### Always Day One

TikTok's borrowed startup mentality — explicitly drawn from Amazon's culture — holds that the company is always a young, hungry startup regardless of its market position. Ray emphasizes that this isn't mere slogan; it shows up in open access to leadership (he can message co-founders directly), relentless focus on growth, and a "how can I run my second half of the marathon faster than the first half?" mentality.

### Cross-Functional Collaboration as Secret Sauce

One of TikTok's unique organizational structures is the extreme closeness between engineering, product, sales, and go-to-market teams. Ray had never seen engineering and product teams join client-facing "immersion trips" before TikTok. The company runs large quarterly all-hands (~180 people) using an Amazon-style reading/doc format that includes ICs from multiple functions — not just leaders — ensuring everyone has shared context. Taking PMs and engineers to meet clients directly, feeling "the heat" of real customer problems, is standard practice.

### Globalization Through Local Fine-Tuning

TikTok's algorithm does heavy lifting globally, but Ray argues that true globalization requires boots on the ground. The algorithm must be "fine-tuned" with local talent who understand cultural nuances — e.g., food content drives virality in Japan, while the US started with lip-syncing. Ray's early market research experience (studying ink cartridge preferences in Thailand) taught him that consumer behavior is rooted in local culture, not universal preferences.

### TikTok as Advertising Platform

Ray positions TikTok as a "content graph" distinct from Google (intent graph) and Meta (people graph). This means the ad platform works differently: broader targeting outperforms narrow targeting in early stages, creative testing is more important (run at least 10 different ad creatives per week), and success requires a "TikTok-first" mindset rather than repurposing Instagram or Google content. TikTok's explore-and-exploit algorithm gives content multiple chances to find its audience — unlike Instagram, which mostly shows content to existing followers.

## Frameworks

### Context + Proactive Thinking + Reactive Doing

Ray's operational framework for autonomous high-performance teams:
1. **Context**: Share all relevant business information freely
2. **Proactive thinking**: Encourage employees to think beyond their role boundaries
3. **Reactive doing**: When taking action, always collaborate with the actual owners

This prevents two failure modes: siloed execution (too little context) and chaotic overreach (no reactive constraint).

## Actionable Advice

- **For advertisers on TikTok**: Start with an organic business account to feel the platform, then layer in paid ads. Give it at least one month.
- **Run at least 10 ad creatives per week** — TikTok's algorithm rewards creative diversity and testing; the "best" creative is almost impossible to predict in advance.
- **Avoid narrow targeting early on** — Broader targeting outperforms niche targeting for new advertisers; let the algorithm find your audience.
- **Hire for curiosity and discipline** — curiosity to think broadly, discipline to know when to collaborate vs. act.
- **Never compromise quality for speed when hiring** — Wrong hires slow a fast-moving company down more than understaffing does.
- **Leaders must stay close to the market** — Managers who distance themselves from customers lose their competitive edge and their ability to guide teams correctly.
