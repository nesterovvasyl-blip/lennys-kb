## Content

Alisa Cohn coaches C-suite executives at startups like Venmo, Etsy, Wirecutter, and DraftKings, plus Fortune 500 companies including Microsoft, Google, and Pfizer. Named top 50 coach in the world by Thinkers50.

### Key Ideas

- **Name the fear beneath the discomfort**: Difficult conversations become manageable when you identify the specific fear driving avoidance — often it's about the meaning you attach to the other person's reaction.
- **Observable Facts Feedback**: Anchor performance feedback in specific observable behaviors ("what I've observed is...") rather than judgments or vague feelings.
- **Winning Culture > Happy Culture**: A leader's job is not to make employees happy but to drive results through clear expectations and accountability. Avoiding hard conversations leads to underperformance.
- **Three Questions to End Every Meeting**: (1) What did we decide? (2) Who needs to do what by when? (3) Who else needs to know?
- **Founder Prenup**: Structured questions on values, vision, conflict style, and culture that co-founders should complete before starting a company. 65% of startup failures stem from founder conflict.
- **Personal Operating Manual**: Document your working style, communication preferences, delegation style, and pet peeves to reduce team friction.
