# Jonathan Becker

> Co-founder of Thrive Digital, a performance marketing agency — the person who built Uber's early paid marketing engine and one of the most systematic thinkers on performance advertising.

## Bio

Jonathan Becker is co-founder of Thrive Digital, a performance marketing and paid growth agency. He built and ran paid acquisition for Uber during its explosive early growth phase — a role where he pioneered many of the creative testing and channel diversification practices that are now standard. He's worked with companies at every stage from seed-funded startups to major enterprises, and is one of the clearest thinkers on attribution, creative testing, and the intersection of brand and performance marketing.

## Key Ideas

### The Wanamaker Problem
The famous John Wanamaker quote: "Half the money I spend on advertising is wasted; the trouble is I don't know which half." Becker's view: this remains true today despite decades of digital attribution. The dirty secret of performance marketing is that **there is no single source of truth**. Every attribution tool (last-click, multi-touch, media mix modeling) is a model with assumptions and blind spots. Sophisticated teams triangulate across multiple methodologies rather than picking one "correct" answer.

### Creative Testing Framework: Isolate One Variable
The most common creative testing mistake is changing multiple things simultaneously (headline, image, CTA, audience) and then not knowing what drove the result. Becker's framework:
- **Isolate a single variable** per test
- **Run until statistical significance** before concluding
- **Build a library of learnings**, not just winning ads — understanding *why* something worked is more valuable than the winning creative

### UGC > Polished Brand Ads
One of the most durable findings in performance marketing: User Generated Content (UGC) — authentic videos from real users — consistently outperforms polished, produced brand advertising. The reason: perceived authenticity. Social media users have pattern-matched polished ads as "ads" and tune them out. UGC reads as peer recommendation.

### The Uber Taxi Arbitrage Origin Story
Becker describes the early Uber paid marketing strategy: Google was full of people searching "taxi [city name]" with essentially no competition. Uber bid on taxi keywords and converted taxi-searchers into Uber users at incredibly low cost. This arbitrage lasted longer than it should have because taxi companies didn't recognize Uber as a competitor until much later. The lesson: the best paid marketing opportunities often come from seeing substitution before the market does.

### Channel Diversification and the TikTok Window
Every paid channel has a lifecycle: discovery (cheap, high ROI) → maturation (competitive, rising CPMs) → saturation (expensive, low ROI). Smart performance marketers move early into emerging channels before they mature. Becker's consistent advice: when a new platform opens up paid inventory (he cited TikTok specifically), move aggressively early, even if the audience isn't perfect yet. The CPMs will never be lower.

### The Snap RFP Story
Becker describes being one of the first agencies to get access to Snap's advertising API by responding aggressively to their early RFP. Companies that were first movers got years of favorable pricing and algorithmic advantages that latecomers could never catch up to. The pattern repeats with every major new platform.

### Attribution: No Single Source of Truth
Becker's nuanced view on attribution:
- **Last-click**: easy to measure, heavily biased toward bottom-of-funnel channels (search, retargeting)
- **Multi-touch**: more honest but hard to implement and interpret
- **Media mix modeling (MMM)**: good for upper-funnel and channel-level decisions but slow to react
- **Incrementality testing** (holdout experiments): the gold standard but expensive and requires scale

Best practice: use MMM for quarterly strategy decisions, incrementality for validating big bets, and last-click as a real-time operational dashboard (while knowing its biases).

### AI and the Shift to Strategic Roles
Becker is sanguine about AI changing performance marketing: the routine work of ad operations, bid management, and basic copywriting will be automated. What remains valuable: strategic judgment about channel selection, creative direction, and understanding business context that tools can't infer. The performance marketer's role shifts from executing tactics to setting strategy and directing AI execution.

## Actionable Advice

- Build a creative testing discipline: one variable at a time, document your learnings
- Triangulate across attribution methods — don't pick one "true" measurement
- When a new ad platform opens up, move early: CPMs will only rise
- Use UGC alongside polished creative — authentic voice often outperforms production value
