# Claire Vo

> CPO of LaunchDarkly and creator of ChatPRD — a practitioner who built a career by treating it as something she could actively design, not just receive.

## Content

Claire Vo is Chief Product Officer at LaunchDarkly, a feature flagging and progressive delivery platform. She is also the creator of ChatPRD, a popular AI tool that helps product managers write product requirement documents — one of the early examples of a domain-specific AI tool built by a practitioner for practitioners. Claire has had a non-linear career: she's been a PM, a CPTO (combined Chief Product and Technology Officer), a founder, and an operator.

Her most distinctive contribution to the Lenny's community is her philosophy of career agency — the belief that careers are not things that happen to you but systems you can deliberately design and steer. She approaches her own career the way a product manager approaches a product: by setting explicit goals, running experiments, and adjusting based on feedback.

### Key Ideas

- **Bend the universe**: Vo's phrase for the posture of active career design. Most people wait for opportunities; Vo believes in creating them. This means being explicit about what you want, asking for it directly, and engineering the conditions that make it possible. The universe doesn't bend itself — you have to bend it.
- **Know what you want and ask for it**: Vo's observation is that most career stagnation comes not from lack of opportunity but from lack of clarity. People don't know specifically what they want, so they can't ask for it, so they don't get it. She coaches PM leaders to write down their 1-year and 3-year career goals in specific, concrete terms before any career conversation.
- **Clock speed mentality**: Vo values environments where things move fast — where you can make decisions, ship product, and get feedback quickly. She deliberately chooses companies and roles that operate at high clock speed, even when slower, more stable options would pay more. Fast environments compound learning faster.
- **The CPTO role**: Vo has served as CPTO (Chief Product and Technology Officer) — a combined role that owns both product and engineering. She argues this combination is underutilized: it eliminates the classic product-engineering handoff dysfunction, forces product leaders to understand technical tradeoffs, and forces engineering leaders to understand user outcomes. The CPTO is accountable for the full delivery system.
- **AI for PMs**: Vo built ChatPRD because she found herself spending 40% of her time on document generation — writing PRDs, briefs, and frameworks that followed predictable templates. She automated that layer, freeing herself and other PMs for the thinking work that couldn't be templated. Her broader view: AI won't replace PMs, but PMs who use AI will replace PMs who don't.
- **Career experiments**: Vo treats career moves as experiments with hypotheses. Before taking a new role, she writes down what she expects to learn, what success looks like in 12 months, and what she'd do if it doesn't work out. This reduces career anxiety and improves decision quality.

### Actionable Advice

- Write your career goals down in concrete terms before any conversation with a manager or recruiter. Vague goals produce vague feedback and vague opportunities.
- Ask for what you want explicitly and early. Don't hint; don't hope it's noticed. State it directly: "I want to be considered for VP Product within 18 months. Here's what I'm doing to get there. What else do I need?"
- Choose your next role based on clock speed as much as title or comp. A fast company with a smaller title will teach you more than a slow company with a bigger one.
- If you're a PM, automate your most formulaic work with AI tools. Identify the 20% of your time spent on templated outputs (status updates, PRDs, meeting summaries) and systematically reduce it.
- Before accepting a leadership role, ask: what does the existing team look like, and what's their relationship with product? The technical-product relationship is the biggest determinant of your success.

### Notable Quotes

> "You have to bend the universe. It doesn't bend itself. Know what you want, say it out loud, and then create the conditions that make it possible."

> "Most PM career stagnation is a clarity problem, not an opportunity problem. People don't know what they want, so they can't ask, so they don't get it."

> "I chose clock speed. Fast environments compound. You make more bets, you get more feedback, you learn faster. Slow environments feel safer but they cost you years."
