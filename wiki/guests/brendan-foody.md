# Brendan Foody

> At 22, the youngest American unicorn founder; built Mercor from 0 to $500M ARR in 17 months — the fastest revenue ascent in history.

## Content

Brendan Foody co-founded Mercor with friends he met at age 14. They started the company at 19 in January 2023 — initially a general recruiting platform using AI to automate resume review, interviews, and hiring decisions. They bootstrapped to $1M ARR before dropping out of college. The pivot to AI training data came when they met the xAI founding team, realized the market was shifting from low-skilled crowdsourcing labor to highly credentialed domain experts (lawyers, doctors, engineers, scientists) needed to write evals and post-training data for frontier models.

From May 2024 onward, Mercor grew from $1M to $400M+ in revenue run rate in 16 months — working with 6 of the Magnificent 7, all top-5 AI labs, and most application-layer AI companies. They raised $100M at a $2B valuation. Net revenue retention exceeds 1,600% and they have never had a customer churn.

### Key Ideas

- **The era of evals**: "If the model is the product, the eval is the PRD." Labs need a systematic way to measure success across every domain before they can improve it. The primary bottleneck to better models is building high-quality evals — which requires deep domain experts, not generalist labor. Evals are also the sales collateral for models.

- **Expert labor as the new frontier**: The human data market shifted from crowdsourced, low-skilled data generation (RLHF with Amazon Mechanical Turk-style workers) to sourcing and vetting domain experts — Goldman bankers, McKinsey analysts, Harvard Lampoon comedy writers, Emmy-winning screenwriters. Top 10% of contributors drive the majority of model improvement, making quality of sourcing the core moat.

- **Elastic demand jobs**: The jobs that will thrive with AI are those with elastic demand — where 10x productivity creates 10x demand. Software engineering, product management, and consulting have nearly unlimited demand. Accounting and other fixed-demand work does not.

- **Finding PMF through pull**: The key to Mercor's breakthrough was recognizing genuine market pull. "If it's extremely difficult to sell the marginal customer, you're not going to build a huge business. What you actually need to find is the customer that's surprisingly easy to sell into." Be stubborn about your thesis but open-minded about the exact form.

- **Culture tenets for hypergrowth**: Three values drove Mercor's success: (1) Can-do attitude — setting ridiculously ambitious goals and somehow hitting them; (2) High standards — first 10 hires included former head of growth at Scale, successful consumer app founders; (3) Intensity — output-oriented, not hours-oriented, but genuinely high ownership.

### Actionable Advice

- Focus on leading indicators in fast-moving markets to find pockets of demand before they become obvious to everyone.
- For the first 10 hires, be patient and disciplined — initial talent density shapes the entire org. After finding clear PMF, optimize for speed in scaling from 10 to 100.
- Apply AI proficiency in your actual domain rather than trying to fight AI adoption — the people who win are those who leverage AI to do 10x more of what they're already good at.
- Prioritize 100% of company resources on building great experiences for customers before scaling sales and marketing.
