# Gustaf Alstromer

> Y Combinator Group Partner who built Airbnb's growth team from scratch, and one of the most systematic thinkers on what separates startups that find product-market fit from those that don't.

## Content

Gustaf Alstromer (Gustaf Alströmer) is a Group Partner at Y Combinator, where he has been for several years after a career that included founding his own startup and leading Airbnb's growth team. At Airbnb, he was among the first people to formalize growth as a discipline at the company. At YC, he works with hundreds of founders per year in the early stages of their companies, and he has become one of YC's most vocal advocates for climate tech investment.

### Key Ideas

- **Talk to customers — it's the single most important thing**: Across every YC office hours session, the advice Gustaf gives most often is the same: talk to your customers. Not surveys, not analytics — actual conversations. The companies that find PMF fastest are invariably the ones with the highest frequency of direct customer conversations. The companies that plateau are usually the ones who stopped talking to customers after the initial research phase. He uses this as a proxy question in interviews: "What have you learned from customers in the last two weeks?"

- **YC office hours process**: The mechanism Gustaf uses in office hours is simple and high-leverage: (1) ask "what's holding you back?" — founders often know the bottleneck but haven't named it clearly, (2) set a specific goal for the next two weeks, (3) hold them accountable to it at the next session. The structure forces concreteness and prevents the diffuse, everything-at-once problem-solving that kills early-stage momentum.

- **Technical co-founders matter more than people admit**: Gustaf's consistent observation across YC batches: companies with at least one highly technical co-founder move faster, ship more, and are harder to block. The technical co-founder isn't just about building the product — it's about the velocity of experimentation, the ability to prototype quickly, and the credibility to hire great engineers. Non-technical founders often underestimate how much they slow down their own growth when every product iteration requires external help.

- **Most startup failures are PMF failures, not execution failures**: Gustaf's blunt observation from seeing hundreds of companies: most startups fail because they never found something people genuinely wanted, not because they ran out of money or executed poorly. This has implications for how founders should spend their time — the vast majority of early-stage energy should go into PMF work (understanding customers, rapidly testing hypotheses), not building infrastructure for scale.

- **Climate tech as the largest investment opportunity of our era**: Gustaf has been one of YC's most prominent climate tech advocates, personally involved in writing YC's request for startups focused on carbon removal. His argument: trillions of dollars will flow from carbon-emitting activities to clean alternatives. That transition has been decided at the government and corporate level. Software founders with standard startup skills are uniquely positioned because many climate tech opportunities are as much software/coordination problems as they are hardware problems. He cites companies like Pachama, Unravel Carbon, Enode, and others as examples.

- **Founders don't need to know YC partners**: YC's application process is designed to work without knowing anyone inside. Gustaf explicitly discourages the belief that networking with partners improves your odds. The application is the channel; the batch experience is what matters. Applicants should focus on demonstrating progress and learning since their application.

### Actionable Advice

- In every two-week sprint, set one specific goal and one metric that will tell you if you achieved it. Review at the start of the next sprint.
- If you haven't talked to customers in the last 7 days, that's your next task, not building the next feature.
- When hiring technical co-founders: the bar is "could I imagine hiring this person into a senior engineering role at any great company?" Not "can they code" but "are they exceptional?"
- For applying to YC: watch the YC YouTube channel's application advice video before writing a word of your application.

### Notable Quotes

> "The companies that find product-market fit fastest are the ones with the highest frequency of real customer conversations. Not surveys. Conversations."

> "What's holding you back? I ask that in every office hours. Founders usually know. They just haven't said it out loud."

> "This transition to clean energy is going to be bigger than software. It's already been decided. The question is who builds the companies that make it happen."
