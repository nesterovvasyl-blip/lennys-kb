# Kayvon Beykpour

> Co-founder of Periscope (acquired by Twitter), Twitter's Head of Product until 2022. Now focused on consumer product building.

## Bio

Kayvon Beykpour co-founded Periscope, the live video streaming app acquired by Twitter in 2015. He served as Head of Product at Twitter until Elon Musk's acquisition in 2022. He was part of the small group that stayed on to help with the transition before eventually departing. He speaks candidly about the realities of culture change, the acquisition process, and what he believes makes great consumer products.

## Key Ideas

### Sacred Cows Are Your Roadmap

The most powerful signal for where to direct culture change: identify what nobody will talk about changing. "The sacred cows are like their own roadmap." When you're new to an organization or trying to change its direction, the things people defend most reflexively — the untouchable assumptions — are precisely where leverage lives. Challenging sacred cows requires credibility first: earn trust by succeeding at smaller things before taking on the most defended assumptions.

### Portfolio of Bets

Consumer product building requires maintaining a portfolio of experiments simultaneously. You can't bet the company on one new direction — you run multiple bets in parallel, accept that most will fail, and let results guide where to double down. This is especially important at large platforms like Twitter, where product surface area is vast and user needs are diverse.

### Culture Change Requires Top Alignment

Culture change is impossible without genuine alignment at the top. It doesn't matter how much individual contributors want something different — if senior leadership isn't genuinely committed (not just rhetorically), the culture reverts. Kayvon's experience at Twitter showed him that culture reflects the values of those who hold actual power, not stated values on walls.

### The "When You Got Nothing to Do, Sweep" Principle

A reflection on ownership mindset: leaders who are truly bought in don't wait for permission or a perfect mandate. They find work, they improve things without being asked. This principle came from a mentor and became a core lens for evaluating whether someone is truly an owner vs. a hired hand. 

### Periscope and the Live Video Competitive Landscape

Periscope was acquired by Twitter for ~$100M before it launched. The live video wars (2015–2016) involved Periscope, Meerkat, Facebook Live. Periscope won the early battle but struggled to break out of a niche audience. The key learning: distribution is everything in consumer. Twitter had the distribution, but failed to fully integrate Periscope's live video vision into the core product.

### The Elon Acquisition Experience

Kayvon was inside the process during the Twitter acquisition. His view: Elon's arrival was genuinely disruptive and unpredictable, but not uniformly negative. The experience reinforced that company culture is determined by whoever makes the hard calls at the top — and those calls changed immediately with new ownership.

## Memorable Quotes

- "The sacred cows are like their own roadmap."
- "When you got nothing to do, sweep."
- "Culture change is really, really hard and it takes a long time — and the people at the top have to genuinely want it."
- "Consumer product building is humbling. You throw things at the wall and you have to be okay with most of them not working."

## Actionable Advice

- When entering a new organization, inventory the sacred cows before choosing your battles
- Build credibility with small wins before challenging protected assumptions
- Run a portfolio of bets rather than sequential big swings on consumer products
- Evaluate team members by their ownership mindset: do they find work, or wait for it?
