# Garrett Lord

> Co-founder and CEO of Handshake — the career network for college students (LinkedIn for new grads), used by 100% of Fortune 500 companies, 1,500+ colleges, 20M+ students and alumni, and 1M+ companies; built a second business (AI training data) on top of the first, reaching $50M ARR in four months and on track to exceed $100M ARR in year one.

## Content

Garrett Lord built Handshake over a decade into the dominant platform for early career hiring, with ~$200M in annual revenue from the core business. In early 2025, he recognized that Handshake's unique asset — a deeply trusted network of 500,000 PhDs, 3 million master's students, and 18 million professionals — was exactly what frontier AI labs desperately needed and couldn't easily acquire. He launched a data labeling business for AI post-training in January 2025. By May, it had generated $50M ARR. The story is a model of incubating a zero-to-one business inside an established company using an existing audience as the unfair advantage.

### Key Ideas

- **Pre-Training vs. Post-Training**: AI model improvement has two phases. Pre-training ingests the full corpus of human text and video from the internet. Post-training — where most gains now come from — augments model capabilities in specific domains through expert-generated data. Models have exhausted the public internet; now they need data that has never been digitized: advanced STEM research, domain-specific expertise, professional workflows, step-by-step reasoning traces in specialized fields.

- **The Shift from Generalists to Experts**: Early AI data labeling used low-cost international workers for generic tasks. As models improved, generalist tasks became trivially automatable. What labs now need is experts — PhDs, domain specialists, credentialed professionals — who can break the latest models, identify where they fail, provide ground-truth answers, and articulate step-by-step reasoning. A physics PhD at the top of their class can break cutting-edge models in ways no generalist can; and they can make $100-200/hour doing so.

- **The Only Moat in Human Data Is Audience Access**: Competitors in the data labeling space spend hundreds of recruiters sending LinkedIn messages and millions of dollars on performance advertising to find qualified experts. They achieve low conversion and low retention because they have no brand relationship with these people. Handshake had a decade of trust with exactly the audience labs need, with rich academic profile data (GPA, field of study, degree level) enabling hyper-targeting. Zero customer acquisition cost and dramatically higher conversion and retention than any competitor.

- **Types of Post-Training Data**:
  - **RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback)**: Preference ranking — which of two responses is better?
  - **SFT (Supervised Fine-Tuning)**: Prompt-response pairs where the expert provides the correct answer.
  - **Trajectories**: Screen recordings + mouse movement + voice narration of how a knowledge worker solves a problem end-to-end. Labs want to understand how humans think through multi-step tool use.
  - **Rubrics**: Model-as-judge frameworks with human-defined criteria for what "good" looks like in subjective domains (educational design, legal writing, medical diagnosis).

- **Quality, Volume, Speed**: The three things labs actually care about. Quality first and foremost — wrong training data is extremely hard to overcome. Volume — can you generate thousands of units of high-quality data in advanced domains? Speed — labs run parallel experiments, and the team that can spin up and scale a data collection pipeline in days wins the race.

- **Zero-to-One Inside an Established Company**: Garrett launched this as a new business inside Handshake. Key insight: the way you build zero-to-one is fundamentally different from how you run a mature business. You must give a small team permission to operate like a startup — fast decisions, customer-obsessed, no process, clear metrics. He hired a team with deep domain expertise in AI post-training (including a former Meta post-training lead), built proprietary tools to assess data quality, and treated the fellow network as a premium community (instructional design, cohort onboarding, expert progression paths) rather than a commodity gig marketplace.

- **AI and Jobs**: Garrett's position on AI displacement is optimistic. Handshake powers early career hiring for every Fortune 500 company. What he hears from employers: AI is enabling one person to do what previously required a team. Entry-level roles are evolving, not disappearing. Being "AI-native" is a skill advantage for young workers the same way being "Google-native" once was.

### Actionable Advice

- Look at what you've built over years and ask: what unique asset do I have that an emerging technology desperately needs? Handshake had a trusted expert network; the timing of AI data demand created a match.
- Incubating zero-to-one inside a larger company requires organizational permission to operate differently. Give the new team startup-like structure and metrics, not the parent company's processes.
- The only moat in data businesses is access to the supply of that data. Build the audience relationship first; monetization follows.
- As an early career person: being AI-native is a hiring advantage. Use these tools fluently, and don't wait to be asked.
