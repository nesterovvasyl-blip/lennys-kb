# Emily Kramer

> Co-founder of MKT1, the marketing community and newsletter for B2B SaaS — the definitive framework for when and how to hire your first marketer.

## Content

Emily Kramer has led marketing at Asana, Carta, and Amplitude, and co-founded MKT1 to help startups navigate marketing hiring. Her episode is the most comprehensive answer to "when do I hire a marketer and what kind?" in the Lenny's Podcast canon. She introduces the Fuel vs. Engine distinction as the organizing framework for thinking about marketing types.

### Key Ideas

- **Fuel vs. Engine Marketing**: The most important framework from this episode. Fuel is campaigns, content, and one-off initiatives that create short-term momentum (paid ads, a launch, a PR push, an event). Engine is systematic infrastructure that compounds over time (SEO, community, email list, product-led growth loops). Most early marketing hires are fuel-focused when companies actually need engine builders. The mistake: hiring someone great at campaigns when you need someone who can build an acquisition engine. They are different skills and different people.

- **Pi-Shaped Marketers**: Kramer argues against the conventional "T-shaped marketer" (broad generalist with one deep skill). The ideal first marketing hire is π-shaped: broad generalist knowledge with TWO deep specializations. The two deepest skills should map to the two biggest levers in the company's go-to-market. If your biggest lever is content + PLG, hire someone who's deep in both. This prevents the common failure mode of hiring a "full-stack marketer" who's actually shallow everywhere.

- **Product-Marketing Handoff**: The biggest coordination failure between product and marketing teams is the handoff timing. Marketing should be involved at the feature definition stage — not just at launch. By the time a product manager brings marketing in for launch planning, the decisions that affect marketability (naming, positioning, packaging) are already locked. Kramer's rule: marketing joins the roadmap conversation when items move from "maybe" to "probable."

- **DRI System from Asana**: At Asana, Kramer ran marketing with a system directly borrowed from their product: every significant initiative has a single Directly Responsible Individual (DRI). Not an owner of the output, but an owner of the process — who ensures cross-functional alignment, who tracks dependencies, who calls the meeting when things slip. Without DRIs, marketing initiatives drift in committee.

- **GACCS Brief Framework**: For any major marketing campaign, Kramer uses a five-element brief: Goal, Audience, Channel, Content, Success metrics. The most commonly skipped is Goal (articulated specifically) and Success metrics (defined before launch, not after). She's adamant that "increase awareness" is not a goal — it needs a number and a date.

- **Impact Goals Not Activity Goals**: The most common marketing planning failure is setting activity goals ("publish 12 blog posts this quarter") instead of impact goals ("generate 500 MQL from organic content this quarter"). Activity goals measure effort. Impact goals measure whether the effort worked. Only impact goals create accountability.

### Actionable Advice

- Before hiring your first marketer, classify your biggest growth problem as fuel or engine. Match the hire type to the problem type.
- The right first marketing hire for most B2B startups at Series A is a demand gen or growth marketer, not a content marketer or brand marketer.
- Invite your marketing hire to product roadmap reviews — give them context on what's coming 6+ months out.
- Run every campaign with a GACCS brief. If you can't fill in the goal and success metrics before you start, don't start.
- Avoid the "Swiss Army knife" marketing hire — the person who claims to be world-class at everything is excellent at nothing.

### Notable Quotes

> "Fuel is what gets you to this quarter's number. Engine is what gets you to next year's number. Most companies are 90% fuel when they should be 50/50."

> "The T-shaped marketer is a myth. The best first marketer you can hire is π-shaped — generalist base, two deep specializations that match your two biggest GTM levers."
