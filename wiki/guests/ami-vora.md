# Ami Vora

> CPO at Faire, ex-Meta (WhatsApp, Facebook Ads, Instagram) — champions curiosity, authenticity, and execution over strategy.

## Content

**Background**
Ami Vora is Chief Product Officer at Faire, the largest B2B marketplace connecting independent retailers and brands. Before Faire, she spent 15+ years at Meta, starting as employee #150 (originally hired as a temp in PR). She launched the first Facebook developer platform, led product for the $55B global Facebook Ads business, oversaw introduction of ads on Instagram, and most recently led product and design for WhatsApp (2B+ users). She joined Facebook in 2007 after sleeping on couches in California to get her foot in the door.

**Key Ideas**

1. **Curiosity over being right.** Ami's signature move — responding to disagreement with "Fascinating, tell me more" — was highlighted by Meta CTO Boz as transformative. She learned to sublimate her ego and prioritize getting to the right outcome over being the person who was right. The shift: reinterpret visceral disagreement as a chance to learn, not a threat to shut down. Take a pause before responding.

2. **Execution eats strategy for breakfast.** Great strategy + poor execution = no learning (you don't know which was wrong). Good-enough strategy + great execution = rapid learning and iteration. She recommends spending ~20% of time on strategy and 80% on confirming it with customers and building the execution machine. Even senior leaders should stay connected to execution — just at a different level.

3. **Metaphors and narrative as alignment tools.** Learned from manager Eric Antino. When teams agree on a feeling or metaphor (e.g., "WhatsApp should feel like face-to-face communication"), individuals naturally make consistent decisions without being told what to do. The WhatsApp metaphor drove design choices like lightweight joinable calls, typing indicators as "taking a breath," and read receipts as "face lighting up."

4. **The dinosaur brain and product reviews.** Executives have limited bandwidth — a "little dinosaur brain" that can hold ~3 facts. The best service a PM can provide is a clear recommendation, not a data dump. Reviews should calibrate on principles, not approve individual decisions. Walk away with decision-making principles so you don't need to come back.

5. **The hill climb metaphor (local vs. global optimum).** You're on a hill (local optimum), you see a mountain in the distance (global optimum). Getting there means descending into an unknown valley first. Most of the time it's worth it. What gets you through the valley: remembering what the summit feels like.

6. **Toddler soccer and goal-setting.** When all teams chase the same high-level metric (e.g., GMV), you get "toddler soccer" — everyone running to the same ball with no coordination. Instead, decompose the metric into input metrics mapped to the customer journey and assign each team a different lane that ladders up to the overall goal.

**On senior leadership:**
- As you get more senior, you only see unsolvable problems (otherwise someone would have solved them before they reached you). All you do is choose the least-bad option and articulate the principles behind it.
- "You can either have more energy or less ambition" (advice from Chamath).
- No org is optimal — you optimize for one axis (people, products, customers, technology) and trade off the rest.

**On being a woman in tech:**
- Received feedback to be more likable, which she acted on by shrinking herself (wearing earth tones, being less opinionated). It worked short-term but backfired when she needed to lead. Now reframes that feedback as "expand your toolset" rather than "make yourself smaller."
- Challenges the mentor myth — telling women they must find a mentor adds another burden. Most of her growth came from generous leaders, not formal mentorship.

**On joining Faire:**
- Used "hot takes" documents with CEO Max — quarterly provocative proposals for fundamental changes. Max engaged seriously with them, building trust and a complementary relationship.
- Advises CPO candidates: spend a day with the CEO to ensure mind meld before accepting. Advises founders: make sure you actually need the seniority level you're hiring for.

**Memorable Quotes**
- "Fascinating, you have to tell me more why you think that."
- "It's more important to get to the outcome than to be right."
- "For strategy to be useful, it actually has to change our behavior as a team to create better customer outcomes."
- "Don't dampen who you are and your strengths — just continue expanding."
