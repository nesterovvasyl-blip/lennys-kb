# Jason M. Lemkin

> Founder of SaaStr, the world's largest SaaS community, and veteran investor who has deployed over $200M into B2B startups.

## Content

Jason Lemkin built and sold EchoSign (acquired by Adobe, grew to $100M+ ARR), then founded SaaStr — the world's biggest community for B2B software founders. He runs two major annual SaaStr conferences (15,000+ attendees in the Bay Area) and is one of the most prolific voices on all things SaaS growth, sales, and hiring. His Twitter/LinkedIn is required reading for anyone building a B2B company.

### Key Ideas

- **Founder-Led Sales Is Non-Negotiable Early**: Even founders who hate sales must close the first 10 customers themselves. Founders are "A+ middlers" — exceptional in the middle of a sales conversation even without formal training. The key move: learn to ask "what would it take to get going?" to convert middle conversations into closes.

- **Hire Two Sales Reps, Not One**: You need an A/B test with humans. The cheat code for the first two hires: you must feel you would buy your own product from them. Quirky backgrounds are fine — what matters is that they love your product, understand the customer's problem, and have enough maturity you can trust them with a precious lead.

- **Never Hire VP of Sales Before Two Reps Hit Quota**: A VP of sales can't find product-market fit, be the first rep, be the second rep, and scale all at once. Always get two reps crushing it first. Then hire the VP to take you from rep 3 to 300. Critical test: make your VP of sales candidate sell you the product in an interview. If they won't demo it, don't hire them.

- **VP of Sales Must Want to Actually Sell**: After three years of burnout across the industry (2021–2024), many veteran sales leaders no longer want to sell. First screening question: "Do you want to do sales?" They should want to be in deals 20–30 hours/week when they start. If they talk process before customers in the first 30 days, walk away.

- **B2B Sales Is Problem-Solving, Not Pitching**: The best reps solve problems, not sell features. They tell customers when the product isn't the right fit. They study the customer's environment (charging network near your house, etc.) and tailor the conversation. When leads are scarce, this expert consultative approach is the competitive moat.

- **Comp Mechanics**: Standard is 50% base / 50% bonus (OTE). In the first three months, let reps keep 100% of what they close. Don't negotiate down the OTE — what matters is they close 4–5× their take-home. Two great reps closing $1M each beats 20 reps struggling to close $100K.

- **AI in Sales**: The episode title references replacing sales teams with AI agents — Lemkin notes AI is changing the top-of-funnel dramatically, but human experts remain essential for consultative problem-solving and complex enterprise deals.

### Actionable Advice

- Interview at least 30 sales candidates before hiring your first two reps. Twenty will be terrible, eight will be okay, and 1–2 will be the ones you'd genuinely buy your product from.
- Ask every VP-level candidate: "What would you do in your first two weeks?" If the answer doesn't include "talk to customers," pass.
- When a VP candidate talks about territory planning and process before customers and calls, that's a red flag — they don't want to sell.
- Test candidates by asking them to demo your product. Give them a few days to prep. If they won't do it, move on.
- Don't stress about OTE dollar amounts. Focus on: can this person close 4–5× their salary? If yes, they'll be accretive regardless of exact compensation.
- Founder advice for closing: end every discovery conversation with "what would it take to get going?" or "I've answered all your questions — when should we get started?"
