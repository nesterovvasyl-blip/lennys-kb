# Dmitry Zlokazov

> Global Head of Product at Revolut ($45B+ fintech operating in 50+ countries with 50M+ customers), consistently ranked as one of the top companies for developing world-class product managers.

## Content

Dmitry Zlokazov leads product across Revolut, a fintech super app that started as a multi-currency travel card and grew into mortgages, stock trading, crypto, savings, and dozens of products across 50 countries. When Lenny's research into which companies produce the best product managers triangulated data on alumni promotions, CPO appointments, and startup founders, Revolut sat at the top alongside Palantir and Intercom. Dmitry came to Revolut as an industry outsider (never worked in FinTech before) and found that his "fresh view" was actually an advantage.

Revolut's approach is radically ownership-first and founder-led: the founders (Nick and Vlad) review every single screen that ships, conduct weekly product reviews with every team, and personally dive into code-level details on the 7-10 most critical projects. Despite having 150+ product owners running ~100 simultaneous projects, the structure enables a credit division of 300 people to ship credit products in 50 countries — compared to 2,000-3,000 people for a single country at an incumbent bank.

### Key Ideas

- **Local CEO Model**: Revolut's product owners are "local CEOs" — they own the product end-to-end, hire their team, define the roadmap, and are ultimately accountable for business metrics. They're not just PMs defining specs; they run fully cross-functional pods with line management over engineers, designers, data analysts, and operations. The PM calls it "product owner" to emphasize ownership, not Scrum terminology.

- **Three Types of Product Owners**: UX product owners (consumer-facing experience, taste-driven), Technical product owners (former engineers who go deep into systems), and Data Science product owners (former data scientists managing metrics-heavy products). But 85-90% of what makes a great product owner is common across all three types.

- **Raw Intellect Over Experience**: Revolut explicitly hires for "raw intellect and unquenched hunger" over experience. Experienced PMs from large companies often "rest on their laurels" and don't have the urgency to change the status quo. The best profiles: tech co-founders, startup veterans, and internal transfers from engineering and operations who love building things.

- **Wow Product as Non-Negotiable**: Revolut never ships a "scrappy MVP." They cut features but never compromise on quality, UX, or aesthetics. The reason: if you launch a poor product that gets no traction, you can't tell if the idea is wrong or the product is bad. By forcing wow quality from the start, you remove that uncertainty. If it doesn't get traction, the idea itself is the problem.

- **99% Done is Closer to 0%**: A product feature that's built but not integrated into go-to-market, customer success, and marketing is essentially 0% done. Product owners must ensure the full launch — not just code completion.

- **Deep Dive + Helicopter View**: Dmitry picks 7-10 of 100 simultaneous projects to go deep on personally — code-level deep. This creates discipline across all teams because they know which ones are being scrutinized this cycle, and they want to be ready. It's not micromanagement; it's a last line of accountability that enables the rest of the org to be fully autonomous.

- **New Bets Framework**: Anyone can propose a new product idea. Key requirements: market exists, business case exists, Revolut can do it better than competitors, clear customer problem. Then: assemble a small team, build a first version fast, iterate, and only scale after retention metrics are verified. Good news: first successful version instantly reaches 50M+ customers.

### Actionable Advice

- In hiring, look for candidates with a startup or co-founder background even if they have fewer years of experience; they've had to build, sell, and own everything.
- Never cut quality to ship faster. Cut scope instead. The wow bar is the brand.
- When managing 100 projects, pick 7-10 to go deep on per cycle — this creates accountability across all of them, not just the ones you scrutinize.
- Measure product success before scaling. A million users from 50M is easy; a failed product in front of 50M is expensive.

### Notable Quotes

> "If something is 99% done, it's closer to 0% rather than 100%."

> "By forcing everyone to build a product that people will love, we kind of cut out this part of uncertainty."

> "Everyone is striving for talented, skillful, smart people. Revolut values way more raw intellect and this unquenched hunger to build things rather than experience."
