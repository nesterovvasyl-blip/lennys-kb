# Anton Osika

> Co-founder and CEO of Lovable, the fastest-growing startup in Europe, building "the last piece of software" — an AI engineer that turns natural language prompts into working products.

## Content

Anton Osika is co-founder and CEO of Lovable, an AI-powered tool that lets anyone describe an idea in plain English and get a fully working software product. Previously, he was CTO at a YC startup and created GPT Engineer, the most popular open-source tool for LLM-based code generation (50K+ GitHub stars). Based in Stockholm, Sweden.

**Key Ideas**

1. **The last piece of software.** Lovable's vision: building the tool that makes all future software. The goal is to enable the 99% of people who don't write code to turn their ideas into real products. This will create a "Cambrian explosion of entrepreneurship."

2. **Minimum lovable product.** Anton reframes MVP as MLP — minimum lovable product. The progression is: minimum lovable product > lovable product > absolutely lovable product (ALP). The company name itself embodies this philosophy.

3. **Scaling laws for AI products.** When AI gets stuck (introduces a bug it can't resolve), the system fails. Lovable's key technical insight: painstakingly identify where AI gets stuck, tune the system quantitatively, and maintain a very fast feedback loop for improvement. They prioritized the highest-impact stuck points first: login, data persistence, Stripe payments.

4. **Generalists over specialists.** For modern product teams, Anton would "obsess about getting as many skill sets as possible for each person I hire." Everyone should understand architecture, design, product taste, and how to talk to users. The bottleneck is shifting from engineering to taste and user intuition.

5. **Clarity in prompting mirrors PM skills.** Being specific about what you expect and what's not working is even more important with AI than with humans. Don't say "it doesn't work" — explain exactly what you expect and which parts are and aren't working. This is fundamentally a PM skill.

6. **Explosive growth through product love.** Lovable hit $4M ARR in 4 weeks, $10M ARR in 2 months, with just 15 people. Growth driver: "People love the product." Awareness came primarily from posting what they shipped on social media (building in public).

**Memorable Quotes**

- "The best word for a great product is that it's lovable."
- "People love the product. That's the driver of the growth."
- "Being a generalist is much more important than it used to be."
- "If you spend a full week trying to reach an outcome using AI, you are in the top 1% of the global population."

**Frameworks and Practices**

- Weekly planning cadence with FigJam board of ranked problems
- Weekly demo for shipping accountability
- Work trials (1 day to 1 week) as a core hiring practice
- In-office work for high-bandwidth communication; lunch together as a cross-pollination tool
- Shackleton-inspired job posting to self-select for high-ambition candidates

**Actionable Advice**

1. To master AI tools: spend a full week end-to-end solving a real problem. That alone puts you in the top 1% globally.
2. When using AI coding tools, use chat mode to understand how things work before prompting for changes.
3. Be extremely specific when prompting AI — explain exactly what you expect and what's not working.
4. For hiring: look for people who care deeply (obsession), have a generalist brain, and demonstrate raw cognitive capability plus a startup mindset.
