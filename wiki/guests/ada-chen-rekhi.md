# Ada Chen Rekhi

> Executive coach and co-founder of Notejoy, former SVP Marketing at SurveyMonkey and growth marketing lead at LinkedIn.

## Content

Ada Chen Rekhi is an executive coach who helps founders scale themselves. She co-founded Notejoy (a collaborative notes app), was SVP of Marketing at SurveyMonkey, and led growth marketing at LinkedIn after her startup Connected was acquired.

### Key Ideas

- **Curiosity Loop**: A structured method for gathering advice — craft a specific unbiased question, send it to 5–10 curated respondents (mix of domain experts and people who know you), then close the loop with gratitude. It's "user research for your life."
- **Explore and Exploit career strategy**: Early career should be deliberate exploration of roles and environments with hypotheses. Once you find what you love, shift to exploit mode — go deep.
- **Inner vs. Outer Scorecard**: Use Warren Buffett's framework to distinguish internal values (how you spend your days, kindness, adventure) from external metrics (titles, wealth, status). Build a personal scorecard of 3–5 core values.
- **Boiling Frog Trap**: Career inertia prevents people from noticing deteriorating situations. The key signal to act is when you stop learning, not when things feel uncomfortable.
- **Eating Your Vegetables**: Like children needing 10–12 exposures to a new food, professionals need repeated practice in uncomfortable skills before developing competence. Distinguish discomfort from novelty vs. genuine dislike.

### Actionable Advice

- Before seeking a coach, run a Curiosity Loop: email 5–10 trusted people a specific question, process the responses, close the loop.
- Do a 15-minute values exercise: select and stack-rank 3–5 core values. Use them as a filter on every major decision.
- When evaluating whether to leave a job, focus on learning trajectory — are you growing? If not, talk to your manager first.
