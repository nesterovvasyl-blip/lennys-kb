# Aishwarya Naresh Reganti & Kiriti Badam

> AI researchers and builders with 50+ AI deployments across OpenAI, Google, Amazon, and enterprises.

## Content

Aishwarya Naresh Reganti is an AI researcher (Alexa, Microsoft, 35+ papers) who runs an AI consulting practice. Kiriti Badam works on Codex at OpenAI and spent a decade building ML infrastructure at Google. Together they've led 50+ AI product deployments.

### Key Ideas

- **Non-determinism is the core challenge**: AI products face unpredictability on both sides — unbounded natural language inputs and probabilistic model outputs. Design for behavior calibration, not specification.
- **Agency-Control Trade-off Ladder**: Start with high human control and low AI agency. Gradually increase autonomy as the system earns trust (e.g., ticket routing → draft generation → autonomous resolution).
- **CC/CD (Continuous Calibration / Continuous Development)**: An iterative lifecycle with a dev loop (scope, curate data, build, evaluate) and a calibration loop (deploy, monitor, spot errors, fix). Analogous to CI/CD but for non-deterministic systems.
- **Pain is the new moat**: Hard-won institutional knowledge from iterating through real failures and messy data is the durable competitive advantage.
- **AI Success Triangle**: (1) Hands-on humble leaders, (2) empowering culture (not fear of replacement), (3) technical obsession with understanding workflows deeply.

### Notable Quotes

> "It's not about being the first company to have an agent among your competitors. It's about have you built the right flywheels in place."

> "Pain is the new moat. Successful companies right now building in any new area, they are going through the pain of learning this."
