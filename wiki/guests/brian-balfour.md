# Brian Balfour

> Founder of Reforge, former VP of Growth at HubSpot, and one of the clearest thinkers on distribution strategy in tech.

## Content

Brian Balfour spent his early career in growth, including a founding role at a social gaming company during the Facebook platform boom and later as VP of Growth at HubSpot. He founded Reforge, which has become the premier resource for product and growth education, and more recently has been building new product tools including Reforge Insights. His career has given him a front-row seat to every major platform shift — Facebook, Google SEO, mobile, and now AI.

His central insight is that while building a great product is necessary, it is not sufficient. The true differentiator for startups is distribution — specifically, the ability to find and ride new distribution platforms before competitors do. He articulates this through a four-step cycle that every major platform follows, and applies it to predict ChatGPT as the next great distribution platform.

### Key Ideas

- **Distribution beats product**: "Building a great product is one of those things that's necessary, but not sufficient. The separation is between those that build really great distribution." Quoting Alex Rampell of a16z: startups are a game of getting distribution before the incumbent can copy. That escape window is shrinking.

- **The four-step platform cycle**: Every new distribution platform follows: (0) Fierce competition with no clear winner; (1) One competitor identifies its moat and opens a third-party platform to accelerate gathering it; (2) The platform is open — this is the gold rush window for third-party developers; (3) The platform closes through ads monetization, first-party feature absorption, or organic suppression. This happened with Facebook (2007–2012), Google SEO, mobile App Store, and LinkedIn. Cycles are getting shorter.

- **ChatGPT as the next distribution platform**: His prediction for the next major channel is ChatGPT. Evidence: (1) It has the best retention among AI platforms, showing the smile curve — retention that increases over time, a rare indicator of escape velocity; (2) The moat they're building is context and memory — the more you use it, the more personalized it becomes; (3) Signals (hiring, partnerships) indicate a third-party agent platform is imminent.

- **There is no opting out**: It's a prisoner's dilemma. If you don't build on the emerging platform, your competitors will, and customer expectations will shift toward them. "You have to play the game. It's better to be early." The key skill is anticipating when the platform begins to close and sequencing away before that happens.

- **Multiple distribution channels will emerge**: It won't be just one platform. Cursor, Notion, Airtable — each vertical tool may become its own distribution channel. Smaller but still following the same four-step cycle.

### Actionable Advice

- Watch retention curves of AI platforms for the "smile curve" — retention increasing over time. This is the early indicator of which platform will win.
- Don't wait to evaluate the emerging channel. If your competitors are in and you're not, that's already a problem.
- Build on the new platform while it's open, but plan the exit strategy before the platform closes (monetization squeeze, ads, first-party copies of your features).
- For startups specifically: go all-in on one platform. For late-stage companies: place multiple bets.
