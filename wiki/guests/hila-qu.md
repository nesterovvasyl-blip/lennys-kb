# Hila Qu

> One of the clearest thinkers on product-led growth — her two-part series on adding a PLG motion became two of Lenny's top 25 most-read newsletter posts, and this episode is described as worth "tens of thousands of dollars in value."

## Content

Hila Qu has led growth at GitLab and Acorns, advises numerous startups through Reforge and independently, and has published one of the top-selling Chinese business books on growth. Her core contribution is operationalizing how B2B companies actually add a PLG motion when they historically relied on sales. She's not a PLG purist — she argues that most mature companies need both PLG and sales motions, and that the harder problem is adding PLG to a sales-led company, not the reverse.

Her framing: PLG is fundamentally DLG — data-led growth. Giving away a free product without the data infrastructure to understand usage is "giving away your product for nothing."

### Key Ideas

- **PLG funnel vs. SLG funnel**: The core difference is that in sales-led growth, interest is measured by marketing engagement (emails opened, white papers read, webinars attended). In PLG, product usage is the leading indicator. Instead of marketing-qualified leads (MQLs), you generate product-qualified leads (PQLs) and product-qualified accounts (PQAs).

- **Two conversion paths in PLG**: (1) Self-serve checkout — the user loves the product, hits a paywall or limit, clicks to upgrade, pays with a credit card. No salesperson needed. Typically works below ~$10K ACV. (2) PQL/PQA sales-assist — usage data signals a high-intent account that also fits your ICP. Sales reaches out with context. The same customer who would've gone through a 6-month enterprise sales cycle now shows up already warm.

- **PLG prerequisites checklist**:
  1. A free vehicle (free plan, free trial, open source, or realistic interactive demo)
  2. Short time to value — help users reach an aha moment quickly
  3. Self-serve checkout flow
  4. Data foundation to understand usage
  5. Simple, comprehensible pricing

- **The PLG funnel audit**: Before investing in PLG, personally walk through the funnel as a new user: Is the landing page compelling? Can you sign up and start without friction? Do you reach the aha moment quickly? Can you actually buy? Most companies find multiple low-hanging improvements through this exercise.

- **GitLab's aha moment discovery**: Rather than guessing, ran correlation analysis across ~10 high-value actions. Checked which actions predicted higher 90-day conversion and higher 30-day retention. The winner: two users using two or more features within 14 days. Why two users? Because that combination captures both individual value perception and the collaborative/team nature of the product. Why 14 days? Long enough to be realistic for a complex tool, short enough to be meaningful.

- **Finding leverage**: The PLG funnel has four primary buckets: Acquisition (PLG top of funnel), Activation (helping users reach aha moments), Conversion (self-serve checkout), and Retention/Expansion. Most B2B software has its biggest opportunity in **activation** — historically these products weren't designed for users to experience value quickly. Miro is Hila's gold standard: sign up → answer a few questions about use case → land in a relevant template → doing something useful in 5 minutes.

- **Retention is the messy middle**: Unlike activation and conversion (relatively fast feedback loops), retention unfolds over months. Two principles: (1) Build habitual frequency into the product itself, not just reminders — if people only need to visit once a month, retention levers are weak. (2) Expansion is part of retention — think in three buckets: upgrade to a higher tier, more seats, or more consumption (e.g., GitLab CI minutes).

- **Data infrastructure stack**: Data hub (Segment), product analytics (Amplitude/Mixpanel/PostHog), experimentation tool (Optimizely/Eppo), lifecycle marketing tool (not a lead-nurturing tool, but behavior-triggered in-product and email). Data enrichment (Clearbit/ZoomInfo) for B2B enrichment. PQL tools (Pocus, Endgame, Pace) for the sales-assist layer. Before picking tools, do a data dictionary audit — ensure key product actions are instrumented and consistently named.

### Actionable Advice

- Walk through your product as a new user every quarter. You'll spot embarrassing friction you've become blind to.
- If you can't decide where to start on PLG improvement, start with activation. That's almost always where the biggest opportunity is.
- Make sure your checkout flow is as frictionless as Lululemon.com. Any confusion there is leaking revenue from people who already decided to pay.
- PLG requires commitment of 1-2 years minimum, a dedicated team, and conviction in strategic fit (not just because PLG is trendy). Red flag: thinking "add a free trial" is the whole PLG strategy.

### Notable Quotes

> "PLG is actually fundamentally DLG — data-led growth. If you don't have a foundation of data and understanding of how to analyze those data, you are giving away a free product for nothing."
