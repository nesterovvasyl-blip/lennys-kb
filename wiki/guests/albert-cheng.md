# Albert Cheng

> Consumer growth and monetization leader at Duolingo, Grammarly, and Chess.com.

## Content

Albert Cheng has led growth at three of the world's most successful consumer subscription companies: Duolingo, Grammarly, and Chess.com. Earlier he was a PM at Google/YouTube and worked at startups including Chariot.

### Key Ideas

- **Growth = connecting users to value**: Not metrics hacking. Teams should be structured around each stage of the user journey (acquisition, activation, engagement, retention).
- **Explore and Exploit**: Find the right mountain to climb (explore), then spread the proven insight across the entire organization to multiply impact 10x (exploit). Attributed to Brian Balfour.
- **Reverse Free Trial**: Instead of time-limited trials, give free users capped real-time tastes of premium features within their natural workflow. Nearly doubled Grammarly's upgrade rates.
- **User Retention is gold**: For mature products, most DAUs are existing or resurrected users, not new ones. Resurrection experiences are often higher ROI than new acquisition.
- **Gamification Three Pillars**: Core Loop (daily action-reward), Metagame (long-term progression like leaderboards), Profile (persistent record of investment creating identity).
- **High Agency Hiring**: In the AI era, learning velocity and agency beat domain experience. Learned habits may need to be intentionally discarded.

### Notable Quotes

> "Growth as the job is to connect users to the value of your product. Growth sometimes gets this reputation that it's just pure metrics hacking."

> "What if we actually sampled a number of different paid suggestions and interspersed them to free users across their writing? Our upgrade rates nearly doubled."
