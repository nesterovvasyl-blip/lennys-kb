# Upasna Gautam

## Background
Upasna Gautam is a product manager at CNN Digital, where she leads the team responsible for the content management system that journalists and editors use to write and publish stories. She spent a decade in tech working in data and search infrastructure before transitioning to product management at CNN. She is also a longtime meditation and mindfulness teacher and is active in the News Product Alliance, a community dedicated to advancing product thinking in newsrooms globally.

## Key Ideas
- **Equanimity as a core PM skill**: The ability to remain unrattled in the highest highs and lowest lows — pausing before reacting — is the foundational skill for working in chaotic environments. Mindfulness practice is the primary way to develop it.
- **Reactivity is the root cause of PM dysfunction**: Most stakeholder conflicts, communication failures, and bad product decisions trace back to reactive behavior. The pause before reacting unlocks better outcomes in all directions.
- **Building bridges between product and editorial**: The most impactful change Upasna made was creating real trust between a product team and editorial staff that historically operated in silos. Direct, consistent, and respectful customer dialogue is the mechanism.
- **High agency under high pressure**: Working in breaking news conditions — where anything can change in seconds — develops the superpower of making clear, rapid decisions without perfect information. This skill transfers to any product environment.
- **Buffers are essential, not optional**: In environments with volatile schedules (journalists covering breaking news), planning without buffers creates constant failure. Systematically building buffer time is a process discipline, not a soft suggestion.
- **Listening is more important than speaking**: Mindful communication means being fully present with the person in front of you. The ability to converse in the language of the listener — journalists' vocabulary, not technical vocabulary — is a core trust-building mechanism.

## Frameworks & Mental Models

### Four Editorial Engagement Touch Points
The system Upasna built to maintain constant, structured dialogue with journalist-customers:
1. **Weekly Demo Days** — open forum to show existing platform features and preview new ones; also used to evangelize the product across the broader business.
2. **Working Sessions** — breakout groups with journalists to recreate their specific workflows in the new platform. Three to six sessions per team before onboarding. Previously called "user testing sessions"; renamed to reflect collaboration over evaluation.
3. **Breaking News Dress Rehearsals** — scripted simulations of breaking news scenarios to stress-test the platform at the speed of real events. Time-boxed, multi-team, engineer-observed.
4. **Office Hours** — open blocks weekly (or twice weekly during intense onboarding phases) for troubleshooting and ad-hoc feedback.

### Breaking News Dress Rehearsal
A deliberate practice ritual where the team scripts a full breaking news scenario — from the first email from the news-gathering team through to publishing — and runs it as a simulation. Engineers and editorial support observe in real time. Collects more feedback in minutes than weeks of ordinary testing, because breaking news is the hardest load the platform faces.

## Actionable Advice
- Build buffer time into every plan that involves busy stakeholders — the question is only whether the buffer is a day, a week, or a month.
- Involve engineers in product discovery from the beginning (user sessions, design jams, editorial conversations). When they understand the why, they define the how with far greater precision.
- Rename "user testing sessions" to "working sessions" — the framing shift from evaluation to collaboration changes the dynamic with customers.
- Practice mindful communication: be fully present in each conversation. Put away distractions, listen actively, and respond in the listener's language.
- Start meditating with something you already do every day — brushing teeth — and just be fully present for those two minutes. That's the entry point.
- Use a "breaking news dress rehearsal" model for any high-stakes platform launch: script the most extreme use case and run it as a simulation before going live.
- Favorite interview question to test for high agency: "What's something that would not exist without your initiative?" Specific, tangible answers are a strong hiring signal.
