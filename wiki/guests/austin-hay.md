# Austin Hay

> Head of Marketing Technology at Ramp; former VP Growth at mParticle, VP Business Operations at Runway; 4th employee at Branch Metrics; Reforge instructor on MarTech.

## Content

Austin Hay is one of the foremost experts on Marketing Technology (MarTech) — the systems, tools, and infrastructure that power growth and marketing functions. He has advised companies including Notion, Airbnb, Walmart, Postmates, Robinhood, and Pete's Coffee on their MarTech strategy.

### What Is MarTech?
MarTech is the discipline at the crossroads of product, engineering, growth, and marketing that manages both the people/process and the systems/platforms side of growth infrastructure. It encompasses:
- Third-party tools (CDP, attribution, email/push, ad networks)
- First-party homegrown solutions built on top of those tools
- Contract management and procurement governance
- Data schemas, identity resolution, and permissions

At ~30–40 employees, MarTech is a "village" effort — everyone chips in. At 100–150 employees, you need someone to own it. B2C companies typically embed MarTech within the growth/marketing org; B2B companies often put it in Revenue Operations.

### Build AND Buy (Not Build vs. Buy)
Austin's key principle: buy the third-party tool to get 90% of the way there, then build the first-party layer on top for differentiation. The velocity comes from not starting from scratch.

### How MarTech Fits the Growth Stack
MarTech sits between user acquisition and product. In a typical setup:
- **Growth marketing** — paid channels, email, SEO, affiliate bring traffic
- **MarTech** — ensures data flows correctly from acquisition all the way through to product and back to ad networks for optimization (e.g., sending bottom-of-funnel opportunity data as synthetic events back to Facebook for better targeting)
- **Product analytics** — CDP as source of truth for behavioral data

### Deterministic to Probabilistic Attribution
From 2010–2020, deterministic matching (IDFA) made attribution precise. Post-ATT (Apple's App Tracking Transparency), most attribution is now probabilistic. This means marketers must build models from partial signals and extrapolate — a fundamentally different skill set.

### When to Hire Your First MarTech Person
Signs: accumulating too many disconnected tools, no one knows how the data flows, a key system can't be changed without risk, contracts are becoming significant cost centers, email sends are a liability risk. A good first MarTech hire is either a software engineer who understands marketing, or someone with an engineering background who learned marketing technology from operations work.

### What a MarTech Person Does Day-to-Day
- Designing and managing a 1–2 year systems roadmap
- Managing contracts, permissions, and compliance
- Building financial models to justify consolidation/migration
- Serving as technical bridge between growth, product, and engineering
- Piloting and evaluating new tools

### Key Principle: Think About the Future State
Even when constrained, always ask: "What happens a year from now if I don't change anything?" Set up SSO early. Choose tools whose pricing scales reasonably. Document schemas. These small early decisions compound massively in either pain or leverage.
