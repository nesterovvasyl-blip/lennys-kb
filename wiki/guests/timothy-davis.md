# Timothy Davis

## Background
Timothy Davis leads performance marketing for all of Shopify and has spent 2.5 years in that role. Previously he consulted for companies including Pinterest, LinkedIn, Redfin, and Eventbrite, helping them kickstart and scale performance marketing teams. He's also worked in-house at IBM and several other large technology companies.

## Key Ideas
- **Paid is for everyone**: Even companies that don't primarily grow through paid channels should at minimum run paid search. Google has pushed organic listings far down the page and Meta has become pay-to-play, so no business can completely ignore paid.
- **Signs of life testing**: Before committing to a platform, run a minimal-budget experiment using your own customer data to build lookalike audiences (starting at 1% match tightness). A positive signal from a 1% lookalike justifies building a full campaign; don't rush to scale before validating creative, targeting, and messaging.
- **Signal vs. noise**: The hardest skill in performance marketing is identifying the right metrics to optimize. Click-through rate tells you targeting; conversion rate tells you product-channel fit; impression share tells you reach. The goal is to serve the right person as often as possible — not to chase vanity reach or "ego marketing."
- **Attribution: prefer time-decay multi-touch**: Users forget where they first encountered a brand, so first-touch attribution over-credits early touchpoints. Multi-touch time-decay is more defensible. Incrementality tests (GeoX, Conversion Lift) are the gold standard but only worthwhile at 50K+/month spend per platform.
- **Creative is massively underrated**: Most companies underestimate how much creative drives performance. If the creative causes an emotional reaction — comedy, happiness, surprise — it drives recall and favorable action. Dollar Shave Club's original video is the canonical example.
- **Forward thinking, backwards planning**: Start with the desired end-state (e.g., all platforms running, 1,000 monthly conversions on emerging channels), then plan backwards to immediate milestones. This makes complex multi-platform programs tractable.
- **Hire for signal-not-noise thinking**: The first performance marketing hire should be a data-driven generalist ("growth marketing specialist/manager") who can find signal in noisy platform data. Creative and dedicated data scientist come next as spend scales. The interview test: throw multiple metrics at candidates and ask what they'd optimize — the best answer is "what's the campaign goal?"

## Frameworks & Mental Models

### The Ops Cadence
Timothy runs his team on a structured spreadsheet cadence with weekly, bi-weekly, and monthly checkpoints covering: finance, performance, structure, keywords, ad copy, quality score, targeting, and more. Each bucket has sub-tasks with defined cadence. This creates accountability and easy cross-functional reporting — answering "how often do you update ad copy?" takes seconds.

### Signs of Life Testing
A minimal-budget test on a new platform using first-party data to build lookalike audiences. Start at 1% match tightness (most correlated to existing customers), expand to 2–4%, 5–7%, and 8%+ as budget allows. The test answers: does this platform have potential for our product at all? Not: is our full campaign ready to scale?

### Bandwidth/Headcount Calculator
Timothy's team uses a quarterly calculator: sum meeting time, PTO, summits, and optimization/reporting work. If total days exceed available days in a quarter, the team is "in the red." First response is to cut scope; only if red persists across quarters does it justify headcount justification.

### The True Competition Metric (via PPC Hero)
Combines position-above-rate and impression-share metrics to identify genuine competitive threats — distinguishing actual conquest bidding from close-variant accidents. Far more reliable than ego-checking impression share.

## Actionable Advice
- Start paid experiments with your own customer data (1% lookalike on Meta or equivalent) before testing cold audiences.
- Ask your platform reps for anonymized competitive benchmarks on CPC, CTR, and conversion rate — they have the data and it's the only reliable industry benchmark.
- Build a living ops-cadence spreadsheet: column = bucket (keywords, ad copy, targeting…), row = subtask, cell = cadence (weekly/biweekly/monthly). Use it for team accountability and easy stakeholder answers.
- When starting video ads, lead with emotion — comedy, happiness, warmth. Something that makes the viewer feel something before asking them to act.
- LinkedIn advertising requires 3x the CPCs of other channels but offers unmatched B2B targeting (job title + company + location). Worth it for high-ACV enterprise sales; not for early-stage or low-LTV products.
- Don't run incrementality tests until you're spending at least $50K/month on a given platform — the signal won't be statistically meaningful below that.
- Accelerate new hire ramp time by giving ownership of a real campaign in the first 30 days, not just handbook reading.
