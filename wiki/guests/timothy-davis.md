# Timothy Davis

> Performance marketing leader who built paid growth programs at Shopify and has consulted for Pinterest, LinkedIn, Redfin, and Eventbrite.

## Content

Timothy Davis has led performance marketing for all of Shopify for over two years. Before that, he consulted for major tech companies — including Pinterest, LinkedIn, Redfin, and Eventbrite — helping them build and scale their paid growth teams. His background is in data-driven marketing, strongly influenced by his love of baseball statistics and the "signal vs. noise" mindset.

### Paid Is For Everyone

Timothy's hot take: every company should do paid search, at minimum. Google Search is user-driven — ads only show when someone types a relevant keyword. As platforms like Google and Meta increasingly require payment for visibility, paid becomes a baseline rather than an optional channel. That said, he distinguishes between paid search (always appropriate) and disruptive media like Meta or YouTube (depends on audience and creative readiness).

### Signs of Life Tests

Before scaling, Timothy recommends running small "signs of life" tests to validate whether a channel is worth investing in. Key steps:
1. Upload your existing customer base to the platform to build lookalike audiences
2. Start with a 1% lookalike match (most correlated to your current customers) to limit budget risk
3. Read the signal: are impressions converting? Is click-through rate healthy? Are you talking to the right people?
4. Only scale after seeing a positive signal — don't go from "signs of life" to full throttle without first ensuring you have the right creative, campaign structure, and messaging
5. Give yourself grace to fail: "We're either winning or we're learning"

### Platform Priorities

Recommended order for most companies:
1. **Google Search** — user-driven, always on
2. **YouTube** — invest if you have video creative; video performs well when measured correctly
3. **Meta (Facebook/Instagram)** — disruptive media, requires good creative and audience fit
4. **LinkedIn** — expensive (3x Meta CPCs), but powerful for B2B account-based targeting (e.g., targeting specific decision-makers at a named account)
5. **TikTok/Snap/Connected TV** — emerging; test if you have organic traffic from there

LinkedIn requires higher LTV/ACV products to justify cost. Timothy shared an example of geo-fencing Coca-Cola executives in LA and running targeted LinkedIn ads addressing their two specific objections (security and recency) — the sales team immediately began hearing those exact concerns resolved in calls.

### Signal vs. Noise: The Hiring Filter

For his first hire, Timothy looks for someone who can find signal in a sea of data. He gives candidates a data dump and asks: "Given your campaign goal is conversions, what do you focus on?" The wrong answer focuses on impressions, reach, and frequency. The right answer zeros in on click-through rate and conversion rate against the goal. This mindset — borrowed from Nate Silver's *The Signal and the Noise* — is his core filter.

### Performance Marketing Team Build

First 3 hires:
1. **Growth marketing specialist** (data-driven, can navigate platforms, finds signal in noise)
2. **Creative person** (in-house brand/design, able to make ads quickly and iterate vs. agency caps)
3. **Data scientist** (incrementality tests, attribution models, building reporting infrastructure)

At ~$50K/month in spend, start the conversation about bringing this in-house.

### Key Metrics and Reports

Timothy focuses on ad strength metrics within Google Ads: expected click-through rate, landing page experience, and ad relevance. He visualizes these across brand vs. non-brand campaigns to pinpoint where to improve quality score. His key insight: **improving ad strength by eliminating poor-quality ads — even a single bad ad — can improve the entire account's quality score and reduce CPC**.

On attribution: he favors multi-touch with time decay weighting. He cautions that attribution does not answer incrementality — whether the user would have converted without the ad. For incrementality, use GeoX or Conversion Lift tests (available through platform reps at ~$50K+ monthly spend).

### Agencies: When to Use, When to Move On

Agencies are good to start — they reduce cognitive load for founders who are also doing sales, HR, and everything else. However, agencies use playbooks and spread attention across many clients. They rarely go deep on keyword granularity, landing page testing, or conversion funnel analysis. Timothy's approach: use agencies to launch, set a milestone (e.g., "when we're at $50K/month"), and then hire an in-house expert when you hit it.

### Ops Cadence

Timothy runs a spreadsheet-based ops cadence listing every performance marketing activity (keywords, ad copy, quality score, targeting, finance, structure) along with frequency (weekly, biweekly, monthly). This enables the team to hold themselves accountable and gives cross-functional partners easy answers like "we update ad copy every two weeks."

### Forward Thinking, Backwards Planning

His meta-framework for all strategy: start with the end goal (e.g., "be on all channels with 1,000 conversions/month at X spend"), then backwards plan the milestones: what creative do you need? Which platform first? What tests to run? This applies from channel strategy down to individual campaign decisions.

### Actionable Advice
- Always start paid search before any other channel — it is user-driven and lowest-risk
- Load your existing customer data first before spending money on lookalikes
- Use your platform rep for benchmarking — they can anonymize competitor data for CPCs, CTRs, and conversion rates
- Don't run incrementality tests until you're spending $50K+/month; the signal-to-noise is too low
- Never let one agency or person be stretched too thin — monitor their capacity to prevent quality decay
- "We're either winning or we're learning" — create an environment where experiments that fail are valuable
