# Cam Adams

> Co-founder and CPO of Canva, one of the world's most valuable private software companies, with $2.3B ARR and 60% YoY growth — profitable for 7 years.

## Content

Cameron Adams was a designer at Google (on Google Wave) when he co-founded Canva with Melanie Perkins and Cliff Obrecht. Canva is now larger than Figma, Miro, and Webflow combined in both valuation and revenue. Used by 95% of Fortune 500 companies, Canva built its dominance through a product-first culture, homegrown leadership, and a radical approach to management: coaches instead of managers.

Adams leads product at Canva, where the philosophy has been to build from first principles rather than copy how Google or Silicon Valley companies run. This extends to how they define product management (connective tissue rather than command-and-control), how they evaluate people (coaches and 360 feedback), and how they think about culture (visual thinking, giving away your Legos, profitability as independence).

### Key Ideas

- **Coaches, not managers**: Everyone at Canva has a coach from their same specialty (product coaches for PMs, engineering coaches for engineers). Coaches focus on skill development and career growth. A separate collegial circle handles 360 feedback and performance. Canva has ~800–1,000 coaches. This preserves the identity and expertise of technical contributors while still providing mentorship scaffolding.

- **Giving away your Legos**: Adapted from Molly Graham's First Round Review article. As the company scales, you must constantly hand off the things you're doing to others in order to operate at higher leverage. The challenge is finding joy in building teams and passing on expertise, not just in the craft itself. Canva operationalizes this through coaching that identifies pivot points where someone is ready to level up.

- **Product management as connection**: Canva didn't have people with the PM title for years. Adams defines the role as connective: "They connect the team, ideas, data, a whole bunch of different things. It's very messy." Great PMs at Canva work closely with teams, navigate constraints, reorient around obstacles, and move the team toward a vision.

- **Visual product thinking**: Canva is a visual product company — everything is communicated through mock-ups and prototypes, not just verbal discussion. This is a cultural screen: people who don't communicate visually tend to struggle there. "We need to be able to see it. We need mock-ups. We need prototypes."

- **Profitability as a moat**: Being profitable for 7 years was a deliberate choice to never be in a position where an investor can cut their valuation by 50% on the eve of a funding round (which happened to them). "We never want to be put in the situation where we have to go to someone for money to ensure the survival of the business."

- **Board meetings as product reviews**: Canva spends one slide on financials and the rest on product updates. The logic: if the product is what drives the business, the board should be focused on product decisions, not just metrics.

### Actionable Advice

- Introduce the "giving away your Lego" concept explicitly in cultural onboarding. Help people find joy in growing and teaching, not just in doing their original craft.
- Implement coaching (same-specialty mentors) separate from performance review infrastructure. The coach focuses on growth, not evaluation.
- For product leaders: define what great product management looks like in the context of your company's culture and product type, not by copying Google or Airbnb. Your product type shapes how people think.
- Build profitability as a strategic moat against investor coercion, not just as a financial metric.
