# Bangaly Kaba

> Director of Product at YouTube; former Head of Growth at Instagram (1B+ users), early growth PM at Facebook, VP Product at Instacart; growth advisor to Twitter and many startups.

## Content

Bangaly Kaba has one of the most storied growth careers in tech. He was responsible for the friend recommendations algorithm at Facebook, helped scale Instagram from hundreds of millions to over 1 billion users as Head of Growth, was VP of Product at Instacart, advised Twitter on growth, and is now Director of Product Management at YouTube where he has already had significant impact.

### The Adjacent User Theory
One of Bangaly's most influential concepts (written about separately at Instagram). The growth insight is to study users who are **on the edge of activating** — not your current engaged users, not the totally unaware. Who is almost there but not quite? What's blocking them? This reveals the real product gaps. At Instagram, studying adjacent users revealed specific connection/content quality problems that were invisible from looking at core users.

### How to Choose Where to Work: Impact = Environment × Skills
Bangaly's framework for career decisions, born from personal struggle at Facebook:

**Impact** is the north star — not compensation or scope, which are outputs of impact.

**Environment** has 6 variables (each scored 0–2, total = up to 12):
1. Manager
2. Resources (team staffing, budget)
3. Scope (what's in your remit)
4. Team skills
5. Compensation (a proxy for feeling valued)
6. Company culture

**Skills** has 4 dimensions (communication > strategic thinking > execution > influence):
- Communication is the most important — and the most visible lever

Do this audit annually. Be honest about what is limiting your impact and whether it can change. Manager is the most critical variable: a great manager can fix most of the others.

### Understand Work (Anti-Pattern: Identify → Justify → Execute)
The most common failure mode: identify an idea, pull data to justify it, spend months building it, launch, metrics are flat. Why? You didn't understand the problem deeply.

The right pattern (from Facebook's DNA): **Understand → Identify → Execute**

**Understand work** is an intentional affordance in the roadmap — explicitly budgeted time for:
- Instrumenting the funnel to know where drops happen
- Data science analysis to identify root causes
- PM strategy documents
- Engineering tech investigations
- Cross-functional partnership mapping

At YouTube, Bangaly started with 60% understand / 40% execute; matured to 80–85% execute / 15–20% understand. The counter-intuitive result: by slowing down to understand, teams ship fewer things but with a higher win rate and ultimately move faster. At Instagram, 15 teams were running 12–20 experiments per team per quarter with a 60–70% positive/shippable rate.

### Managing Complex Change Framework
Five components needed for sustainable team or culture change (Bangaly carries this from company to company):
1. **Vision** — does the team have a shared picture of where they're going?
2. **Skills** — do people have the skills to execute?
3. **Incentives** — are people rewarded for the right behaviors?
4. **Resources** — is the team appropriately staffed and funded?
5. **Action plan** — is there a clear, specific roadmap?

Missing any one component produces a specific failure mode (confusion without vision, anxiety without skills, slow change without incentives, frustration without resources, false starts without an action plan).

### Mentorship: Have a Stable, Not a Single Mentor
Rather than one mentor, maintain 3–4 mentors and meet with each once a month (each on a different Friday). If one cancels, you still have others. Find mentors through referrals tied to specific challenges ("I'm trying to solve X — do you know someone who's done this?"), not cold outreach asking for generic mentorship.

### Watching and Stealing from Great Practitioners
Don't just read theory — watch how talented people actually run meetings, communicate, and lead. Take notes and steal techniques. Example: watching how an early Instagram PM named Georgia Lee built trust in meetings by naming individuals' contributions and crystallizing them into shared action — Bangaly made this a personal technique.
