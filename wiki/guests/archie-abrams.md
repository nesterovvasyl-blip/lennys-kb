# Archie Abrams

> VP of Product and Head of Growth at Shopify, leading 600+ people across product, engineering, data, and growth marketing.

## Content

Archie Abrams leads the Growth org at Shopify — a 600+ person team covering growth product, growth marketing, experimentation infrastructure, and customer support tooling. Shopify processes about 10% of US e-commerce (everything outside Amazon and Walmart), with $235B GMV in 2023.

### Shopify's Counterintuitive Growth Philosophy: Optimize for Churn
Unlike almost every SaaS company, Shopify intentionally lowers barriers to starting — even knowing most merchants will churn. The insight: Shopify monetizes primarily on payments (% of GMV), not just subscriptions. So the winning strategy is to maximize the total GMV produced by a cohort over 3–5 years. A few merchants who go on to become big businesses (Allbirds, FIGS) make the entire cohort economically successful. This is analogous to angel investing — most investments fail, but the winners make the portfolio work.

### Long-Term Holdout Experiments
Shopify runs two layers of holdouts:
1. **Global 5% holdout** — 5% of users never see any new changes in a quarter; used to measure compound effects
2. **Cohort holdouts** — for new-merchant experiments, ship the winner to 100% but track the original experiment cohort for 12+ months

Every experiment is automatically "re-reviewed" at 3, 6, 9, and 12 months after shipping. The key finding: **30–40% of short-term experiment "wins" show no meaningful long-term lift** on GMV. The most common failure mode is pull-forward: the experiment caused someone to activate sooner, not incrementally better outcomes.

### Absolutes Over Rates
Shopify's growth team tracks **absolute numbers** (e.g., total merchants who reached X GMV), not conversion rates. Why: optimizing conversion rates locally creates perverse incentives to constrict the upstream funnel. If you make it harder to sign up, your sign-up → activation rate goes up — but you've created fewer total activated merchants. The team's goal is always "more people at the end of the funnel," not "a higher percentage surviving each step."

### Monetary Friction
A key growth lever: reducing the cost/risk/friction of starting. Examples include trial length, trial pricing, incentive credits. The counterintuitive finding: free or cheaper periods attract lower-quality merchants on average, but can causally change outcomes — giving marginal entrepreneurs just enough runway to find their first sale and become successful long-term.

### Three Product Groups at Shopify
- **Core Product** — building for the 100-year vision (Tobi's direction), no short-term KPIs, driven by taste
- **Merchant Services** — payments, shipping, tools for merchant success (medium-term horizon)
- **Growth** — end-to-end customer journey: acquisition through support

### Onboarding as the Highest-Leverage Growth Work
Despite not optimizing for retention, onboarding is Shopify's most consistent source of long-term GMV lift. The formula: collect more information than you think you need at signup, use it to personalize the guidance from day one. This has consistently shown long-term GMV impact in holdout analysis.
