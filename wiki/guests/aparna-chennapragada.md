# Aparna Chennapragada

> CPO at Microsoft (Experiences & Devices), former CPO at Robinhood, former VP at Google (Lens, Search, AR, AI Assistant).

## Content

Aparna Chennapragada is Chief Product Officer of Experiences & Devices at Microsoft, where she oversees AI product strategy for productivity tools and agent work. Previously she was CPO at Robinhood, VP at Google (led Google Lens, shopping, augmented reality, AI assistant), engineering leader at Akamai, and board member at eBay and Capital One. She is also a practising stand-up comedian.

### NLX Is the New UX
Aparna coined **NLX (Natural Language Experience)** as the design paradigm for conversational AI products. Just as GUIs required explicit design (menus, dropdowns, affordances), natural-language interfaces require explicit design — just with invisible, elastic constructs. Key NLX design elements she identifies:
- **Prompts** as a new UI primitive (like a dropdown was)
- **Plans** — editable plans returned by agents as a key interaction pattern
- **Showing work / progress** — calibrating how much thinking to expose (too verbose = cron-job feel; too terse = loss of confidence)
- **Follow-ups** — proactively surfacing the right next prompts without overwhelming

### Agents: Three Defining Properties
Aparna defines agents through three dimensions:
1. **Autonomy** — a spectrum of delegation, from copilot to fully autonomous
2. **Complexity** — multi-step tasks ("build me a prototype") not one-shot ("summarize this doc")
3. **Natural interaction** — asynchronous collaboration in meetings, chat, voice, not just text

### Demos Before Memos
For the AI era, Aparna insists that if you are not prototyping to see what you want to build, you are doing it wrong. She calls **prompt sets the new PRDs**. The key insight: time-to-first-demo is radically shorter, but time-to-full-deployment will lengthen because the bar for scale is higher (supply of ideas/prototypes goes up, so standing out is harder).

### Solve Before Scale
A contrarian principle: when building zero-to-one, be comfortable with wide lurches in direction — what looks like chaos is exploration. Avoid premature metrics ("grownup metrics") like CTR or retention when you only have 1000 users. Look for qualitative signal and nail 1–2 use cases (timer / music / trivia) before claiming breadth.

### Three Inflection Points Framework (Why Now?)
When evaluating a zero-to-one product, look for at least 2 of 3 inflection points:
1. **Tech inflection** — step function in underlying technology (deep learning for Lens, LLMs today)
2. **Consumer behavior shift** — camera as keyboard (Lens), phone-first finance (Robinhood)
3. **Business model shift** — new monetization models unlocked by the technology

### Living One Year in the Future
Aparna's operating philosophy: institutionalize the mindset of living 12 months ahead via Microsoft's **Frontier program** — a fake internal company with cutting-edge AI tools, designed to let willing early adopters experience the future now, without forcing everyone to change at the same rate. She also keeps a Chrome extension that shows "How can you use AI to do what you're going to do right now?" on every new tab.

### WWXD (What Would X Do?)
A personal AI use pattern: use AI with relevant context about a stakeholder to simulate their reaction before a big meeting or pitch.

### On the PM Role in the AI Era
PMs won't become obsolete, but the "TPS report" process PMs will. The editing function — deciding what to build from an infinite supply of prototypes — becomes more valuable. PMs must earn their gatekeeping role rather than having it conferred by title.
