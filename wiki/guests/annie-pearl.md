# Annie Pearl

> Chief Product Officer at Calendly who led the company's transition from a horizontal PLG product to a focused, multi-motion enterprise business.

## Content

Annie Pearl is CPO at Calendly, previously CPO at Glassdoor and Director of Product Management at Box. She transitioned into product management from a law background, joining a startup founding team after law school. She was named one of the most influential women in tech.

**Key Ideas**

1. **Strategy as integrated choices.** Inspired by the book *Playing to Win*, Annie defines strategy as "an integrated set of choices that outline how you're going to win in whatever marketplace you choose." A good product strategy answers: where will you play (markets, segments, personas) and how will you win with that target audience?

2. **Focus as a cultural discipline.** Calendly's core principle "focus wisely" enables saying no. Annie argues most companies believe they have clarity on their target persona but when you go into the weeds, PMs and designers are often unclear. The ability to say no allows you to build something amazing for the people that matter most, rather than something average for everyone.

3. **Horizon-based resource allocation.** Calendly allocates resources across three strategic horizons, shifting over time: Year 1 was 70/30 on H1/H2, Year 2 was 50/50, Year 3 shifted to 30/60/10 across H1/H2/H3. No investment in far-out horizons until near-term ones are mature.

4. **PLG-to-sales transition.** When Annie joined, 99% of ARR came from PLG. Two years later, a sales-led motion represented 20% of ARR and was the fastest-growing segment. Key insight: early sales hires for PLG-to-SLG transition should be inbound/grower profiles (not outbound hunters), and the target buyer starts as the department head, not the CIO.

5. **Product development lifecycle with phase-based commitments.** Four phases: Discovery, Solutioning, Build, Launch/Measure/Iterate. Teams commit to dates only for the phase right in front of them, not for projects six months out where discovery hasn't been completed.

6. **Viral growth loop.** 70% of Calendly signups come through the viral loop (someone receives a Calendly link, signs up themselves). Teams form organically, then department heads either inbound or become PQLs (product-qualified leads).

**Memorable Quotes**

- "Strategy is really just an integrated set of choices that outline how you're going to win in whatever marketplace you choose."
- "The ability to say no is going to allow you to make sure you're building something that's going to be amazing for the people that matter most and not something that's going to be average or okay for a lot of different people."
- "The first 10 users were actually customer success agents at a company that contracted with the same firm Tope was using to build Calendly."
- "Sales and the go-to-market teams in general could be your biggest asset to helping you get your job done well."

**Frameworks and Practices**

- [[playing-to-win-strategy]]: Where to play, how to win, winning aspiration
- [[product-development-lifecycle]]: Discovery > Solutioning > Build > Launch/Measure/Iterate
- OPA (Opportunity/Problem Assessment): Regular PM meetings for debating problem spaces without leadership present
- Competitive War Gaming: Quarterly exercises where teams own a competitor and present SWOT analyses

**Actionable Advice**

1. To transition into PM: shadow PMs, take on product work, become a subject matter expert (SME), or join an early-stage startup.
2. Use Glassdoor to search for "associate product manager" to find APM roles across companies.
3. When sending a Calendly link, first offer the recipient the chance to share their availability before sending your link.
4. When layering sales onto PLG, match sales rep profiles to the motion (inbound vs outbound) and the buyer (department head vs CIO).
