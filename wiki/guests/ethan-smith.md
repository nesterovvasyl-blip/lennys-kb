# Ethan Smith

> CEO of Graphite (graphite.io) — the definitive practical guide to Answer Engine Optimization: how to show up when people ask LLMs about your product.

## Content

Ethan Smith runs Graphite, an SEO and AEO consultancy with clients including Webflow, MasterClass, and others. He has an academic background in research methodology (PhD-adjacent), which informs his emphasis on rigorous experimentation over received wisdom. His episode is the most comprehensive and data-grounded treatment of LLM search optimization in the Lenny's Podcast canon.

### Key Ideas

- **AEO = LLM + RAG**: Answer Engine Optimization is not training model weights — it's optimizing for Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). LLMs like ChatGPT use web search to find recent information, then summarize those search results. AEO means getting your content into those search results and getting cited in the summary. This is fundamentally similar to SEO, not different from it.

- **6X Conversion Rate from LLM Traffic**: Webflow case study — when users arrive at Webflow via an LLM citation (clicking through from a ChatGPT or Perplexity answer), they convert at approximately 6X the rate of Google organic traffic. The reason: these users are further along in the decision process. They've asked a specific question and gotten a recommendation — they arrive with intent, not curiosity.

- **LLM Traffic Now 8% of Webflow Signups**: Webflow was getting approximately 8% of all new signups from LLM sources at time of recording. This is now a top acquisition channel, comparable in size to major paid channels. The channel is growing faster than any other. Smith's prediction: within 2-3 years, LLM traffic will be a primary channel for most B2B SaaS companies.

- **The Long Tail Is Larger in Chat**: Average search query on Google: 4-6 words. Average question in ChatGPT/Perplexity: 20-25 words. This means the tail of questions in LLMs is vastly larger than in traditional search. Most of the opportunity in AEO is capturing questions that have never been typed into a search box because they're too specific or conversational.

- **Citations-Based Ranking**: LLMs cite based on frequency of mention across sources and authoritative signal from each source. A page that's cited by 3 different authoritative sources matters more than one that's mentioned once. The implication: getting cited in Reddit threads, YouTube videos, and affiliate review sites is often more valuable than optimizing your own landing page for that topic.

- **Citation Strategy by Source Type**: Smith breaks AEO offsite strategy into tiers:
  - **Affiliate publishers** (Dotdash Meredith, Forbes Advisor): expensive but reliable and controllable
  - **YouTube/Vimeo**: underserved for B2B topics — huge opportunity for specific, nonglamorous keywords
  - **Reddit**: community-driven, harder to control, but authentic and highly cited
  - **Your own landing pages**: target topic-clusters of questions, not single keywords
  - **Help centers**: underused for AEO — subdirectory placement + cross-linking dramatically improves citation rate

- **AI-Generated Content Doesn't Work**: Smith ran a rigorous study. Methodology: thousands of queries in Google and ChatGPT, AI detection on all cited pages (validated detector with ~8% false positive rate). Finding: only 10-12% of cited content is AI-generated. Pure AI content (no human in the loop) is systematically excluded by both Google and LLMs. AI-assisted content (human-edited) works fine. His warning: if AI content DID work, it would create an infinite derivative loop — LLMs summarizing LLM-generated summaries — leading to model collapse.

- **Experiment Design is Critical**: Smith's research background surfaces here. Most SEO and AEO "best practices" are not empirically validated — they're repeated assertions. His protocol: take 200 questions, assign 100 to control (no changes) and 100 to test, make a specific intervention, measure share-of-voice change after 2+ weeks, require reproducibility before declaring success.

- **SEO Is Not Dying**: Google traffic to publishers is slightly UP, not down. LLMs are an additive channel, like TikTok search or YouTube search — they expand the pie rather than slice from Google's portion. Startups that stop investing in SEO to focus on AEO are making a mistake; the two are complementary.

### Actionable Advice

- Set up an AEO tracker (answer tracking tool) to measure your current share of voice across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini before investing in optimization.
- Prioritize YouTube videos for B2B AEO — there's almost no competition for specific, high-LTV B2B topics in video format.
- Start with Reddit: find 5-10 relevant threads, create an account with your real name and company, and post genuinely useful answers. This is low-cost, authentic, and highly cited.
- For early-stage companies: skip mid-funnel SEO; focus only on citation optimization and answering very specific long-tail questions.
- Move your help center from a subdomain (help.yourcompany.com) to a subdirectory (yourcompany.com/help) — subdomains perform worse in both SEO and AEO.
- Question your assumptions: before acting on any SEO/AEO advice (including from this episode), design an experiment to validate it for your specific product and audience.
