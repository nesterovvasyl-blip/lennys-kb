# Jason Shah

> PM with experience across Airbnb, Amazon, Microsoft, and Alchemy who focuses on building meaningful careers rather than merely successful ones.

## Content

Jason Shah has held PM roles at some of the most respected companies in tech — Amazon, Microsoft, Airbnb, and crypto startup Alchemy. He's thought deeply about what distinguishes a meaningful career from a merely successful one, and about the qualities that make leaders truly great.

### Key Ideas

- **Ladder vs. Map Career Framework**: Most people approach their careers like climbing a ladder — always pushing to the next rung, never pausing to look around. Shah advocates thinking of your career as a map instead: you can go in any direction, and the goal is to navigate toward meaning and fulfillment, not just upward mobility. The question isn't "how do I get to the next level?" but "where do I actually want to go?"

- **Three Qualities of Great Leaders**: Shah identifies three characteristics he's seen consistently in the best leaders he's worked with:
  1. *Nothing is above them* — great leaders will unblock any bottleneck, even if that means doing "junior" work themselves. They don't consider any task beneath their station.
  2. *In the details* — they have genuine command of the specifics. Not micromanagers, but deeply informed. They can engage substantively with the work, not just the summary.
  3. *They adapt* — they update their mental models when new information arrives. They don't rigidly defend their prior positions.

- **Amazon Working Backwards / PRFAQ**: Shah absorbed Amazon's discipline of working backwards from the customer. The PRFAQ (Press Release + FAQ) forces you to articulate what you're building from the customer's perspective *before* writing a line of code. The press release describes the product as if it has shipped; the FAQ anticipates real objections. This creates alignment and surfaces hidden assumptions early.

- **Reframing Pushback**: When stakeholders push back on a decision, Shah's approach is to reframe rather than defend. Instead of "here's why you're wrong," he asks "what would need to be true for my proposal to be the right call?" This transforms an adversarial debate into a collaborative exploration of assumptions.

- **Marketing + Sales + Product approach to hiring**: Shah recommends treating a job search like a go-to-market motion — using marketing (content, reputation building), sales (direct outreach, relationship-building), and product (your actual skills, portfolio) in combination, rather than relying on any one channel.

- **Meaningful Career Markers**: Shah encourages PMs to track not just impact metrics but meaning metrics — are they learning? Working with people they respect? Doing work that matters to them personally? The external signals of success (title, comp, company brand) often diverge from the internal experience of meaning.

### Memorable Quotes

> "Most people think about their career like a ladder. But what if it's a map? You can go in any direction — not just up."

> "The best leaders I've seen won't let anything stay above them. They'll do whatever it takes to unblock the team."
