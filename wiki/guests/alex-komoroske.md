# Alex Komoroske

> Strategic thinker, 13 years at Google (Chrome, Maps, strategy), former Head of Corporate Strategy at Stripe.

## Content

Alex Komoroske spent 13 years at Google leading Chrome's Open Web Platform, AR in Google Maps, and company-wide strategy. He served as Head of Corporate Strategy at Stripe and is currently founding a startup reimagining the web for the AI era.

### Key Ideas

- **LLMs as magical duct tape**: A disruptive technology that upends the assumption that software is expensive to write and cheap to run. Makes taste the most important differentiator.
- **Gardening vs. Building**: Instead of executing a predetermined plan (builder), the gardener looks for systems with compounding potential, plants cheap seeds, and waters what grows.
- **Organizational Kayfabe**: Layers of optimistic spin compound upward until leadership is orders of magnitude removed from ground truth, causing zombie-like persistence on failing strategies.
- **Slime Mold Framework**: Coordination costs scale with the square of team size. Large orgs behave more like decentralized slime molds than top-down machines — embracing this unlocks anti-fragile problem-solving.
- **Adjacent Possible**: At any moment only a small set of actions are within reach. Take the best step toward a North Star, let the world reconfigure, repeat.
- **Always rules beat sometimes rules**: Black-and-white commitments are far more sustainable than conditional ones for self-discipline.

### Notable Quotes

> "LLMs are magical duct tape. They're formed principally by the distilled intuition of all of society."

> "I think taste is the most important thing. You have a perspective that is different from the background noise."

> "A community with zero people speaking is dead and a community with one person speaking doesn't yet realize it's dead."
