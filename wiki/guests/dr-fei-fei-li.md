# Dr. Fei-Fei Li

> Known as the "Godmother of AI"; created ImageNet, the dataset that sparked the deep learning revolution; former Chief AI Scientist at Google Cloud; Director of Stanford's AI Lab; co-founder of World Labs building spatial intelligence and world models.

## Content

Dr. Fei-Fei Li spent two and a half decades at the center of AI research before the public knew AI was anything. She created ImageNet starting in 2006-2007 — a dataset of 15 million carefully curated and labeled images across 22,000 concepts — and the ImageNet Challenge. In 2012, a Toronto team (led by Geoff Hinton) used ImageNet data with two gaming GPUs to demonstrate the first neural network that could make dramatic progress on object recognition. This combination — big data, neural networks, GPUs — became the "golden recipe" for all modern AI including ChatGPT. She was chief AI scientist at Google Cloud, director of Stanford's AI Lab (SAIL), and co-creator of Stanford's Human-Centered AI Institute. She's on the UN AI Advisory Board, named to TIME's 100 Most Influential People in AI.

In late 2025, she co-founded World Labs to build spatial intelligence and world models — a fundamental capability she believes is as important as language models and currently missing from AI.

### Key Ideas

- **The Big Data Insight**: The most critically overlooked ingredient of modern AI was large-scale labeled data. Rule-based programs and models alone couldn't generalize. Human learning is a big-data learning process — children and animals experience the world at massive scale. ImageNet was built on this insight a decade before it was obvious.

- **The Three-Ingredient Recipe**: Modern AI (including ChatGPT) runs on three ingredients that were first combined in 2012: (1) large-scale labeled data, (2) neural networks, (3) GPUs. These three remain at the core of all current AI, scaled enormously.

- **AI as Humanist Technology**: "There's nothing artificial about AI. It's inspired by people, created by people, and most importantly, impacts people." AI is a net positive for humanity, but it's a double-edged sword — responsible development is not optional. The direction AI takes is fundamentally up to us.

- **AGI is a Marketing Term**: As a scientist, Fei-Fei considers "AGI" undefined and essentially a marketing term rather than a scientific concept. The north star of the field has always been the same question Alan Turing asked in the 1940s: can machines think and do things the way humans can? AI has achieved some of this, but far from all of it.

- **What AI Still Cannot Do**: Current AI cannot count chairs in a room by watching a video (a toddler can). Current AI cannot look at celestial body data and derive Newton's equations. Current AI cannot have the emotionally intelligent conversation of a mentor with a student. Language models are powerful but cover only a fraction of human intelligence.

- **World Models and Spatial Intelligence**: Humans are deeply visual, spatial animals — we interact with the world at the object level, in 3D, through movement and manipulation. Language models are a breakthrough for the language dimension of intelligence. But a whole other dimension — spatial intelligence, understanding of the 3D world — is currently missing from AI. World models are foundation models that can generate navigable, interactive 3D worlds from text or image prompts. Applications include robotics, VFX/virtual production (40X production time reduction shown in launch), game development, and scientific discovery.

- **The Bitter Lesson and Robots**: Richard Sutton's "bitter lesson" says simpler models with more data always win in the long run. But robotics is different from language: training data for robots requires actions in 3D worlds, not just text or images. And robots are physical systems, more like self-driving cars than LLMs — a 20-year journey even with deep learning accelerating the "brain" side.

- **Human-AI Collaboration**: The most under-appreciated thing is that humans, as embodied agents, can be dramatically augmented by spatial intelligence AI — just as language models augment human language tasks. It's not just robots; it's humans with better tools for perceiving and interacting with the physical world.

### Actionable Advice

- In AI development and deployment: care deeply about responsibility — you are participating in a civilizational-level technology.
- Don't dismiss things that look like toys. World models currently look like playful 3D world generators; the same pattern (ChatGPT seemed like a toy) has repeatedly preceded transformative technologies.
- In scientific and creative work: spatial intelligence and world models will matter more than we currently appreciate, as they augment human ability to reason in 3D.
