# Onboarding

> The most powerful product lever because 100% of users experience it.

## Content

Onboarding is where user expectations meet product reality. It is the highest-leverage surface in any product — not because it's glamorous, but because every user touches it. Small improvements compound across the entire user base.

**Optimize for retention, not conversion**
Adam Fishman's core onboarding principle: conversion rate is a vanity metric if it brings in users who don't stick. Teams should optimize onboarding for long-term retention and activation quality, even if it means lower raw sign-up numbers. Opinionated defaults — pre-configured settings that guide new users toward behaviors known to drive retention — are a powerful tool. Patreon used this approach to improve creator revenue by 25%, by steering new creators through a proven setup flow rather than leaving configuration open-ended. Strategic friction (adding deliberate steps that filter out poor-fit users) can improve downstream cohort quality.

**Activation as a funnel**
Albert Cheng frames onboarding as the front end of the activation funnel. Getting a user to their first "aha moment" — the point where they understand why they should care — is the primary goal. He also highlights user resurrection as a form of re-onboarding: lapsed users who return need a path back into the product's value loop. At mature products with large user bases, resurrection campaigns often outperform new-user acquisition on a cost-per-activated-user basis.

**Study the marginal user**
Adriel Frederick's marginal user framework inverts normal onboarding research: instead of studying your best users to understand what makes onboarding work, study your worst-case users — those who barely completed onboarding or churned immediately after. These users reveal the full failure mode space. Onboarding improvements designed to retain marginal users tend to be more durable than improvements targeting already-engaged cohorts.

**Onboarding as highest-leverage GMV driver (Shopify)**
Archie Abrams identifies personalized onboarding as the single highest-leverage GMV driver at Shopify. The key insight: the right first actions depend entirely on who the merchant is. A Shopify merchant migrating from a competitor needs a different first experience than a first-time entrepreneur who just had a product idea. Shopify's onboarding team experiments relentlessly on which path to route each merchant segment to, with success measured not by completion rate but by GMV generated over a 3–5 year cohort window. Short-term completion metrics can mislead — a frictionless onboarding that brings in uncommitted merchants looks great at 30 days and terrible at 12 months.

**Consistent positioning through onboarding**
Anuj Rathi argues that the biggest onboarding failure in emerging markets is when the positioning promise made in marketing doesn't match the onboarding experience. Users arrive expecting the value proposition they saw in ads; if the onboarding experience doesn't deliver on that specific promise within the first few interactions, trust is broken immediately. His rule: pick one positioning hook and carry it consistently from first ad impression through first action in the product.
