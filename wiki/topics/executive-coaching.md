# Executive Coaching

> Coaching is a tool for scaling leaders — but it is not the only tool, and often not the best one.

## Content

Executive coaching has grown from a niche intervention to a mainstream leadership resource, but the conditions under which it creates the most value are more specific than its popularity suggests.

**When coaching is — and isn't — the right tool**
Ada Chen Rekhi offers a practical calibration: most people don't need a full-time coach. The same developmental goals can often be achieved through mentors (experienced practitioners who share their playbook), curiosity loops (structured learning habits — reading, courses, experimentation), and peer communities (cohorts of people navigating similar challenges). Coaches are most valuable in two specific scenarios: hyper-growth environments where the pace of change outstrips a leader's existing frameworks, and sensitive interpersonal challenges where the coach's neutrality and confidentiality are themselves part of the value. Outside these scenarios, the cost-benefit of coaching is less clear.

**The coach's role in leadership development**
Alisa Cohn frames a coach's primary job as helping leaders navigate difficult conversations they've been avoiding, build self-awareness about how their behaviors land on others, and shift from managing individuals to building cultures. She distinguishes between "happy culture" coaching (helping leaders feel comfortable) and "winning culture" coaching (helping leaders set high standards, deliver hard feedback, and drive results). The latter is harder and more valuable.

**Coaching as feedback infrastructure**
Both guests converge on a shared insight: coaching creates conditions where high-quality, direct feedback can flow to people who otherwise wouldn't receive it — either because of organizational hierarchy (leaders rarely get candid feedback from direct reports) or because of systemic bias (underrepresented founders receive less direct feedback from advisors). In this sense, coaching is less about delivering insights and more about building the feedback infrastructure a leader needs to grow.
