# Diversity as Product Velocity

> Diverse teams directly accelerate product development for global audiences by embodying diverse user perspectives and reducing external research needs.

## Content

The standard business case for diversity focuses on ethics, representation, or long-run innovation. Adriel Frederick offers a more immediate, operational argument: for products serving global or multicultural audiences, team diversity is a **direct input to product velocity**.

The mechanism:
- When your team embodies the diversity of your user base (ethnicity, culture, geography, language), you have built-in user insight at the table during every design and prioritization discussion
- This reduces — though doesn't eliminate — the need for external research to answer basic cultural questions
- Fewer research cycles means faster iteration; fewer blind spots means fewer costly post-launch corrections

**Example framing**: A product team building for Southeast Asian markets that includes engineers and PMs from those markets will catch localization issues, interaction pattern mismatches, and trust/privacy sensitivities earlier in the process than a homogeneous team conducting quarterly user research.

**Important caveats:**
- This is not a substitute for rigorous user research — it's an accelerant
- Team members should not be expected to represent their entire demographic; the value is in pattern recognition and instinct, not as a research proxy
- The effect is strongest when diverse perspectives are integrated into core product decisions, not siloed in "localization" or "international" teams
