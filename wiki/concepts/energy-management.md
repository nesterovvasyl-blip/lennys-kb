# Energy Management

> Manage your energy, not just your time -- schedule tasks to match your personal energy patterns.

## Content

Anneka Gupta argues that productivity is less about time management and more about energy management. Knowing your personal energy rhythms and scheduling accordingly can dramatically affect decision quality and mindset.

**Practical tactics:**
- Identify your worst time of day (for Gupta, it's 5-6 PM) and never schedule difficult meetings, strategy writing, or high-stakes conversations during that window.
- Don't skip meals -- something as simple as grabbing a protein bar instead of a real lunch measurably decreases your ability to bring your full self to work.
- Architect your day so that the hardest problems get your best energy, not your leftover energy.

**Connection to mindset:**
Energy management is a prerequisite for maintaining a positive, abundant mindset. When energy is depleted, people default to scarcity thinking and struggle to approach problems creatively. Gupta links energy management directly to her ability to "have fun" even in the most difficult situations.
