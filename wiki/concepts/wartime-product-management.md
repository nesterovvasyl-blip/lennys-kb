# Wartime Product Management

> Mode of leadership during acute crisis: blow up roadmaps, rapidly reallocate resources, establish shared context and a single goal, ship at speed.

## Content

Wartime product management is a distinct operational mode triggered by sudden, high-stakes external events — platform shifts, regulatory changes, public crises, or market shocks. It contrasts sharply with "peacetime" product management, which optimizes incrementally within a stable strategy.

Key characteristics:

- **Roadmap suspension**: Normal prioritization frameworks are set aside; the crisis defines the backlog.
- **Rapid resource reallocation**: Teams are reassigned across org boundaries without normal approval cycles.
- **Shared context at speed**: Leaders over-communicate the situation so every team member understands why speed matters and what success looks like.
- **Single unifying goal**: Fragmented objectives are collapsed into one clear mission for the duration.
- **Ship fast, iterate**: Bias toward imperfect-but-deployed over polished-but-delayed.

Real-world examples:
- **Facebook post-2016 election**: Reorganized product and integrity teams around the singular goal of addressing election misinformation — standard roadmaps deprioritized entirely.
- **NYT during COVID**: Product teams rapidly stood up new data journalism, subscriber communications, and remote infrastructure under acute time pressure.

The risk of wartime mode is burnout and loss of strategic direction if it persists too long. The best leaders know when to transition back to peacetime.
