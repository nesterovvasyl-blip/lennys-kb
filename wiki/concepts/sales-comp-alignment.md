# Sales Comp Alignment

> The mismatch between how SaaS sales reps are compensated (rewarding new deals closed) and what actually makes a SaaS business healthy (customer retention, renewals, upsells) — and how to fix it.

## Overview

Standard SaaS sales compensation is designed around a single metric: new ARR. A typical structure is 50/50 base-to-variable with a quota of 5x OTE. Accelerators kick in above quota — rewarding volume aggressively. But customer health after the signature (renewals, churn, upsells, referenceability) has zero effect on AE compensation in the vast majority of SaaS companies.

Sahil Mansuri argues this structure was tolerable when growth-at-all-costs was the dominant strategy and cheap capital meant you could always acquire more customers to offset churn. When retention and net dollar retention become the primary investor metrics — as happened sharply in 2022 — the comp structure becomes actively destructive.

## The Structural Problem

Mansuri's illustrative example: consider two reps with the same quota ($1M).

**Rep A** closes 15 deals ($1.5M, 150% attainment). Gets $400K total comp, goes to President's Club, celebrated by the org. 10 of 15 customers churn next year. Comp: unaffected.

**Rep B** closes 12 deals ($1.2M, 120% attainment). Gets $250K. Doesn't go to Cabo. But all 12 renew, 4 upsell, 6 become references, and they source warm intros for the pipeline team. Comp: unaffected by any of this.

Under current norms, Rep A is the hero. Under a retention-focused business model, Rep B created far more enterprise value.

## The Fix

Mansuri's prescription for modernizing comp plans:
1. **Renewal kickers:** AEs receive a bonus when their customers renew (tying their comp to a longer feedback loop)
2. **Renewal rate benchmarking:** Track renewal rates by rep and compare to company average — reward reps whose customers renew at above-average rates
3. **Multi-year and upsell incentives:** Give explicit recognition and compensation for multi-year contracts and upsells driven by AEs
4. **Reference customer bonuses:** Reward AEs when their customers become referenceable — this is a lagging indicator of deal quality

## The Base-Variable Split

Mansuri also challenges the 50/50 split itself. The split is what creates the "coin-operated" salesperson dynamic — when half your comp is variable, you optimize for the variable. Most other executives (CMO, CFO, COO) are on 90/10 or better. He argues the VP of Sales and CRO should be closer to other executives in base-to-variable ratio, which would also change how they think about long-term business health.

## Systemic Causes

Why hasn't comp been modernized? Mansuri identifies:
- Founders often don't understand sales deeply and inherit structures from their first VP of Sales
- The "this is how sales comp works" sludge is self-reinforcing — VPs of Sales bring the same comp plans from company to company
- Short-term incentives are seductive — everyone was willing to spike revenue now and worry about churn later when capital was cheap

## Actionable Takeaways
- Audit your comp plan: does it reward the behaviors that actually make your business healthy, or just new ARR?
- Add at least one lagging retention metric into AE comp (renewal kicker, annual renewal rate bonus)
- When capital is cheap, comp misalignment is tolerable. When retention matters, it becomes a strategic liability.
