# Head of AI Operations

> A new organizational role responsible for mapping company workflows, identifying where AI can replace human work, and redesigning the remaining human roles accordingly.

## Content

Dan Shipper ([[dan-shipper]]), CEO of Every, argues that the most important new role in any AI-forward company is what he calls the "head of AI operations." This is not a technical role — it's an operational design role, closer to a chief of staff or VP of operations than to a CTO or AI engineer.

### What the Role Does

The head of AI operations owns three activities:

1. **Workflow mapping**: Systematically documenting every significant workflow in the company — how work flows from inception to completion in each function. This creates visibility into where human time is actually going.

2. **AI applicability analysis**: For each workflow, assessing which steps AI can handle autonomously, which require human judgment, and which require human judgment but can be AI-assisted. This is not a one-time analysis; it needs to be repeated as AI capabilities improve.

3. **Organizational redesign**: Restructuring roles and teams around the AI/human division of labor. This often means fewer people doing a given type of work, but those people doing higher-leverage tasks. It also means redesigning processes so AI can hand off to humans cleanly.

### Why This Role Is Distinct From Existing Roles

- **Not IT/engineering**: This role doesn't build AI systems — it figures out where to deploy them.
- **Not operations/COO**: Traditional operations optimizes existing human processes. This role assumes those processes will be fundamentally restructured.
- **Not a PM**: Product managers apply AI to products. This role applies AI to internal operations.

### At Every

Shipper describes Every as a company of 15 people running 5 products — possible only because AI handles execution at a volume that would require 50+ people in a traditional company. The implicit head of AI operations function at Every is one of the primary reasons for this leverage. The role need not be a dedicated hire at small companies — it can be a significant part of a COO or chief of staff role.

### Broader Implications

Shipper's prediction is that as AI capabilities improve, the companies that move fastest will be those that have systematically mapped and redesigned their operational workflows. The bottleneck will not be AI capability — it will be organizational design capability.
