# Community Notes

> Twitter/X's crowdsourced fact-checking system that uses cross-partisan agreement — rather than majority vote — to determine which notes become visible on posts.

## Content

Community Notes (originally called Birdwatch) is a product built by Twitter/X to add contextual information to misleading posts. Its core innovation is algorithmic: rather than surfacing notes that most people rate as helpful, it only surfaces notes that receive helpful ratings from contributors who typically disagree with each other.

### The Bridging-Based Algorithm

The algorithm works by:
1. Mapping each contributor's political/ideological position based on their rating history
2. Requiring that a note receive "helpful" ratings from contributors across the political spectrum
3. Only surfacing notes that achieve this cross-partisan consensus

This approach was explicitly designed to prevent the most obvious failure mode of majority-vote systems: partisan groups using "fact-checking" to suppress accurate corrections to content that their side spreads.

### Why Cross-Partisan Agreement Works

Misinformation often spreads along partisan lines. Majority-vote systems would make whichever group is larger on the platform the de facto arbiter of truth. Cross-partisan agreement is a more durable signal for accuracy because:
- It requires both sides to accept the correction as legitimate
- It filters out notes that are technically accurate but framed to score political points
- It gives the system legitimacy across political divides

### Scale

- 950,000 contributors globally (as of 2025)
- 30 billion note views in 2024
- Open source algorithm — anyone can download the data and run it themselves

### Adoption

Meta announced it would adopt the Community Notes model to replace professional fact-checkers on Facebook and Instagram — a significant industry signal that bridging-based approaches are becoming standard.

### Limitations

Community Notes does not catch all misinformation. It works best for:
- Specific verifiable factual claims
- Posts where a note can be written that achieves cross-partisan consensus

It struggles with:
- Fast-moving breaking news (notes require time to be written and rated)
- Nuanced contextual claims without clear factual resolution
- Areas of genuine expert disagreement
