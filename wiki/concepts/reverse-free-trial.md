# Reverse Free Trial

> Instead of time-limited trials, free users get capped real-time tastes of premium features within their natural workflow.

## Content

The reverse free trial flips the traditional freemium model. Rather than giving users full access for a limited time (then cutting them off), it integrates premium feature previews directly into the free experience — users encounter the value of paid features in context, at the moment of relevance, but hit a cap before getting the full benefit.

Grammarly implemented this by interspersing paid suggestions within free users' writing sessions. The result was nearly doubled upgrade rates compared to prior conversion approaches. The key insight: exposure to premium value inside the user's actual workflow is far more persuasive than a countdown timer.

This pattern is especially effective for writing, productivity, and AI-assisted tools where the premium value is qualitative and immediately perceptible.
