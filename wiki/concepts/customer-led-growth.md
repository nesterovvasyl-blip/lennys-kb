# Customer-Led Growth

> A growth methodology that replaces guesswork with systematically studying your best customers to understand what they value, why they chose you, and what journey brought them to success — then replicating that journey for everyone.

## Content

Customer-Led Growth (CLG) was developed and popularized by [[gia-laudi]] and Claire Suellentrop of Forget The Funnel. It is a response to the problem of "random acts of marketing" — growth teams running endless A/B tests and channel experiments without a coherent foundation in what actually drives customer value.

The core CLG argument: most growth teams are building on sand because they don't deeply understand their best customers. Without that foundation, every experiment is a guess. With it, experiments become targeted, messaging resonates, and onboarding reflects actual customer needs.

**The CLG process:**
1. **Identify your best customers** — not all customers, not churned customers, not average customers. Best customers: high LTV, low churn, vocal advocates. They've already completed the full journey successfully.
2. **Interview them** — 7-10 interviews using [[jobs-to-be-done]] methodology. Ask: What was happening in your life when you decided to look for a solution? What did you try before? What did you hope would be different?
3. **Map the customer journey** — identify 6 milestones (see [[customer-journey-milestone-framework]]): struggling moment → consideration → first value → committed use → continued engagement → advocacy. For each: what does the customer need to do? What friction prevents it? What metric signals it? Who owns improving it?
4. **Act on the insights** — rewrite onboarding copy in customer language, restructure trial flows to accelerate time-to-first-value, address the specific friction points your best customers named.

**Why best customers specifically**: Best customers have completed the full journey. Their stories reveal what *works* — the job the product successfully fulfilled, the moment the product "clicked," the language they use to describe value. Average customers or churned customers reveal what doesn't work, which is useful for different problems. For growth strategy, you want the success signal.

**Organic growth as the PMF test**: Gia argues that for early-stage companies, organic word-of-mouth is the most reliable PMF signal. If best customers aren't spontaneously recommending the product, something is wrong upstream — either the core value delivery or the job the product is solving. No acquisition budget fixes a product people don't love enough to recommend.

**CLG vs. product-led growth**: PLG is often about mechanics (free trials, self-serve, viral loops). CLG is about the foundational understanding that should inform which PLG mechanics to build. They're complementary — CLG answers "what creates value," PLG answers "how do I distribute and scale it."
