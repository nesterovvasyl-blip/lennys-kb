# Adjacent User Theory

> Growth comes not from optimizing for your current core users but from understanding and converting the users just outside your current product experience.

## Content

Adjacent User Theory, associated with [[bangaly-kaba]] (former Head of Growth at Instagram), reframes where growth actually comes from in a maturing product. The "adjacent user" is someone who is aware of the product, has probably tried it, but hasn't yet converted to habitual use — they sit just outside the current product experience.

The core insight: core users are already well-served, so optimizing for them produces diminishing returns. Adjacent users represent the next growth frontier, but they have different mental models, different use cases, and often encounter the product through different contexts than core users do. Building for them requires understanding *why* the product doesn't yet work for them — which is often a different problem than why the product works for core users.

At Instagram, this framework guided the expansion from a photo-sharing app for enthusiast photographers toward a platform that worked for a much broader audience — understanding what the adjacent user needed (simpler, more social, less artistic) shaped key product decisions.

The theory connects to [[impact-environment-skills]]: being in the right growth environment means working on products that still have substantial adjacent user populations to convert.
