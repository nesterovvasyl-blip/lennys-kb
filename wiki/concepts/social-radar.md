# Social Radar

> The calibrated ability to quickly and accurately read people — their authenticity, competence, commitment, and potential — developed through deliberate practice.

## Content

Jessica Livingston, co-founder of Y Combinator, describes Social Radar as her primary contribution to YC's investment process. Over thousands of founder interviews and application reviews, she developed a finely calibrated ability to quickly assess people that she calls "Social Radar."

Unlike intuition, Social Radar is trained. It improves through deliberate practice — reading people, testing your predictions, updating your model. Livingston believes it can be developed by anyone willing to pay attention systematically.

### What Social Radar Reads For

In the context of founder evaluation:
- **Earnestness**: Is this person genuinely passionate about the problem, or performing passion? Authentic belief has different texture than rehearsed enthusiasm.
- **Defensiveness signal**: When pushed back on, do they engage thoughtfully or shut down? Defensiveness under pressure predicts inability to work through hard feedback.
- **Domain depth**: Do they know the problem at multiple levels of detail, or only have a surface story? Real domain knowledge shows up in specific examples.
- **Commitment markers**: Have they done something that demonstrates actual commitment before asking for resources?
- **Relationship dynamics**: For co-founding teams, can you sense genuine trust and respect, or is there underlying tension?

### The Earnestness Dimension
Livingston places special emphasis on earnestness — a quality she describes as genuine belief combined with willingness to look "uncool" to do the right thing. Earnest founders:
- Actually believe the problem matters beyond just the business opportunity
- Are honest about what they don't know
- Don't posture or try to seem impressive
- Will do embarrassing things (like making cereal boxes) to survive

### Application Beyond Investing
Social Radar is applicable anywhere you need to quickly assess people: hiring, customer development, reference checks, partnership evaluation. The underlying skill is the same: paying close attention, noticing inconsistencies, and trusting calibrated pattern recognition over first impressions.
