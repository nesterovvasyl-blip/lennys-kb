# Local CEO Model

> Revolut's product ownership philosophy: product owners are "local CEOs" who own their product end-to-end, including hiring, roadmap, and full P&L accountability.

## Content

The Local CEO Model is Revolut's approach to product ownership, articulated by Dmitry Zlokazov ([[dmitry-zlokazov]]), Global Head of Product. It is an explicit rejection of the "PM as spec writer" model in favor of full cross-functional ownership that mirrors what a founder-CEO does for their company.

### The Core Idea

At Revolut, a product owner is not a product manager in the traditional sense. They:
- **Own the full product lifecycle**, not just definition or roadmap
- **Have line management** over engineers, designers, data analysts, and operations in their pod
- **Hire their own team** — sourcing, evaluation, and offer decisions
- **Define the roadmap** and are accountable for business metrics, not just shipped features
- **Own the launch** — code complete is not done; go-to-market, customer success integration, and marketing coordination are part of the product owner's job

The name "product owner" is intentional and not Scrum terminology. It's a statement about accountability: you own it, you're responsible for it.

### Why "Local CEO" Matters

The framing solves an accountability problem common in large product organizations: features get built but not launched, products ship but don't achieve business outcomes, and PMs blame engineering while engineering blames PMs. When you're the local CEO, there's no one else to blame.

Dmitry's formulation: "If something is 99% done, it's closer to 0% rather than 100%." A feature that's built but not integrated into go-to-market, customer success, and marketing is essentially zero value delivered.

### Three Types of Product Owners

Revolut recognizes that "local CEO" applies across different product archetypes:

1. **UX Product Owners**: Consumer-facing experiences where taste and design judgment are paramount
2. **Technical Product Owners**: Former engineers who go deep into systems — appropriate for infrastructure, platform, and API products
3. **Data Science Product Owners**: Former data scientists managing metrics-heavy products like recommendations, fraud, or pricing

But Zlokazov emphasizes: 85-90% of what makes a great product owner is *common* across all three types. The distinction is at the margins.

### Hiring Philosophy

The model requires a different hiring bar. Revolut explicitly looks for:
- **Raw intellect and unquenched hunger** over experience
- **Tech co-founders and startup veterans** — people who've had to sell, support, and ship simultaneously
- **Internal transfers** from engineering and operations who love building

What they avoid: experienced PMs from large companies who have learned to "rest on their laurels" and lack urgency to change the status quo. At Revolut, that urgency is non-negotiable.

### The Wow Bar

The local CEO model pairs with Revolut's "wow product" standard: ship nothing below the bar of genuinely impressive. Cut features, never cut quality. The reason is epistemic — if you launch a mediocre product and it gets no traction, you can't tell if the idea is wrong or the execution is poor. By forcing wow quality from the start, you eliminate that uncertainty. No traction = the idea is the problem.

### Scale Test

The model's results: a Revolut credit division of 300 people can ship credit products across 50 countries. An equivalent team at an incumbent bank would need 2,000-3,000 people for a single country. The efficiency gap comes primarily from ownership structure, not from technical talent differences.
