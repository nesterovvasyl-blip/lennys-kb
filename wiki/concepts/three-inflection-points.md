# Three Inflection Points

> Any big product bet should be anchored to three simultaneous inflection points: a technology shift, a consumer behavior shift, and a business model shift — their convergence defines a strategic window.

## Content

Introduced by [[aparna-chennapragada]] (CPO at Microsoft, former VP at Google), the Three Inflection Points framework is a structured answer to the "why now?" question in product strategy. It argues that significant product opportunities require three conditions to converge simultaneously:

1. **Technology inflection** — What capability just became possible that wasn't before? Not "AI is improving generally," but the specific technical unlock (e.g., long-context LLMs enabling document-scale reasoning, or multimodal models enabling image understanding in consumer apps).

2. **Consumer behavior inflection** — What shift in how people do something has just occurred or is accelerating? Behavior shifts are often driven by external forces (pandemic, mobile adoption, a platform change) and create new habits and expectations that products can now address.

3. **Business model inflection** — What new monetization structure does this enable? A technology unlock without a monetization path is a research project. The business model must also be newly viable — either because the technology makes unit economics work, or because customer behavior has shifted to support it.

Aparna uses this framework when evaluating whether a product bet is well-timed or premature. The convergence of all three defines a strategic window. "Why now?" is answered not by any single factor but by the specific moment when all three are simultaneously true.

This framework operationalizes the [[adjacent-possible]] concept: the right next step is one where all three inflections align.
