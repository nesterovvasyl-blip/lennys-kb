# Boring Business Thesis

> The most profitable businesses are often the least glamorous ones, because low competition matters more than high excitement.

## Content

Andrew Wilkinson's core investing and entrepreneurship principle: the businesses nobody dreams of starting are often the best businesses to start or buy. Competition is the primary destroyer of margin, and competition concentrates in "sexy" industries.

**Examples of boring businesses that print money:**
- Government form-filling software ($30M/year revenue)
- Pressure washing companies
- Grease trap cleaning for restaurants
- Exhaust vent cleaning for commercial kitchens
- Pest control
- Funeral homes

**Examples of "exciting" businesses that destroy capital:**
- Cafes and restaurants (everyone wants to start one, brutal margins)
- Bars (complex operations, many failure points)
- Project management apps (Wilkinson lost $10M on Flow competing with Asana)
- Local news media
- Cat furniture businesses
- Online DJ schools

The thesis connects to Warren Buffett's concept of moats: boring businesses often have natural moats (regulatory complexity, unpleasant work that deters competition, established customer relationships) that exciting businesses lack.

A key nuance: boring doesn't mean you have to do the boring work forever. At scale, the founder can focus on the parts they enjoy (sales, marketing, strategy) while employees handle operations. "There's a big difference between a business and a job."
