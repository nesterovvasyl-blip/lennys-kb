# Lazy Leadership

> Get away from the things you hate as quickly as possible and be Teflon for tasks.

## Content

Andrew Wilkinson's leadership philosophy: instead of the Protestant work ethic of doing everything yourself, focus relentlessly on removing yourself from tasks you hate and delegating to people who are better at them.

**Core principles:**
- **Be Teflon for tasks.** Don't let tasks stick to you. Build systems and hire people to handle what you don't enjoy or aren't great at.
- **A business is not a job.** If you're the only employee doing all the work, you have a job, not a business. Scale means you focus on what you love (sales, strategy, creative work) while others handle operations.
- **Leave companies alone.** When Tiny acquires a company, they generally change nothing. If the founder leaves, they install a CEO and step back. "No one should know that we've even bought them."
- **CEOs are elephants.** You're the rider. They go where they want to go. Listen carefully in interviews to what they say they'll do, because that's what they'll do regardless of your direction. Never try to coach a CEO into doing something they don't believe in -- they'll sandbag it.

The philosophy connects to Wilkinson's AI adoption: he has systematically replaced human assistants with AI agents for email management, calendar scheduling, meeting prep, and CRM updates -- not because the humans were bad, but because AI is "the world's most reliable employee."
