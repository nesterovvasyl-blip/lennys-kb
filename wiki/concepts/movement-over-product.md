# Movement Over Product

> The most effective companies pitch a movement — a shift in the world — rather than a product with features.

## Content

Andy Raskin identifies a fundamental difference between companies that achieve breakout growth and those that compete on features: the winners define a movement. Instead of leading with "here's our product and why it's better," they lead with "here's how the world is changing, and here's the new way to win."

This principle explains why Salesforce beat Siebel (cloud as movement, not CRM as category), why Drift broke away from dozens of chatbot competitors (conversational marketing as movement, not better chatbot), and why Gong transcended the sales operations tool category (reality-based selling as movement, not call recording as feature).

The practical implication: product becomes a "prop" for making the story come true — a very important prop, but not the lead. Marketing content shifts from product announcements to trend pieces about the movement (Zuora's blog was 80% about the subscription economy, not about Zuora). Sales conversations start with the shift, not with features. Product roadmap decisions are filtered through "does this advance the movement?"

The concept connects to category creation but is broader: a category is a label, a movement is a story. You can have a powerful movement without a formal category name.
