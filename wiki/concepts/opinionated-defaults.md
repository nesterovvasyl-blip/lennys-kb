# Opinionated Defaults

> A product philosophy where the tool makes deliberate, considered choices about *how* work should be done — rather than offering infinite configurability — so users can focus on their work rather than configuring their tools.

## Content

Karri Saarinen (co-founder and CEO of Linear) built Linear on the principle of opinionated software. Most productivity tools trend toward maximum configurability: every setting, every workflow, every layout can be customized. Opinionated defaults go the opposite direction: the software makes strong choices, and users adopt those choices rather than reconfiguring everything.

### Why Opinionated Defaults Win

**Cognitive load reduction**: When a tool has too many options, users spend mental energy on tool-configuration rather than on the actual work. The best tools "disappear" — the interface gets out of the way and users focus on what they're building. Linear's "cycles" (not "sprints"), auto-scheduling, and fixed workflow structure are all opinionated — they embody a specific philosophy of software development.

**Better collective outcomes**: When everyone on a team uses the same defaults, coordination becomes easier. Shared vocabulary, shared workflow structure, and shared visual language reduce context-switching and miscommunication. Linear's opinionated structure means that a developer joining a new team using Linear already knows the basics.

**Taste and quality signal**: Opinionated design communicates care. When a tool makes a strong choice and does it beautifully, users infer that the team behind it understands their problem deeply. This is part of why Linear won market share against Jira — not by adding features, but by having fewer, more considered ones.

### The Risk

Opinionated software can alienate users whose workflow doesn't match the tool's assumptions. Karri accepts this tradeoff: "We are not trying to be all things to all people." Linear explicitly targets teams that adopt its workflow philosophy; teams that need infinite customization are not the audience.

### The Complementary Principle

Opinionated defaults pair naturally with the [[linear-method]] approach of not setting per-feature metrics goals. Both reflect a trust that good judgment — applied consistently — produces better outcomes than optimizing any individual variable. The software expresses the team's values; users who share those values self-select in.
