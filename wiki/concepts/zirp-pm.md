# ZIRP PM (Zero Interest Rate Phenomenon)

> A product manager conditioned by an era of abundant capital and resources to follow processes rather than exercise judgment — ill-prepared for real startup conditions.

## Content

Casey Winters coined this term while managing PMs at Eventbrite and later observed the pattern persisting when interviewing for Whatnot. The diagnosis: the ZIRP era (roughly 2010–2022) allowed every startup to operate with the resources of a well-funded public company. PMs who came up in this environment confused the frameworks with the job.

**Symptoms**:
- When asked to solve a problem without existing data, says "I can't begin to answer this without research"
- Treats user research as a required step rather than a scarce resource
- Recommends solutions that take months to get signal from
- Generates a list of options competitors have chosen without analyzing *why* or what's most applicable
- Follows a process someone else designed rather than thinking from first principles

**Root cause**: The abundance of resources — analysts, researchers, designers, engineers — removed the forcing function of judgment. When everything goes through a process, there is no practice of applying real-world intuition. "When's the last time you used your brain versus followed a process someone else designed?"

**The correction**: The job is not to follow processes. The job is to "figure out how to add value to customers that translates into value to the business." Reorient the North Star. Use research as a scarce resource — reserve it for situations of extreme uncertainty with high leverage for resolving it. The fastest way to learn is often to ship.

**Interview signal**: Casey's test — give candidates real scenarios from the role without data, and observe how they reason. Red flag: inability to generate creative solutions, propose fast tests, or think holistically about what metrics to improve.
