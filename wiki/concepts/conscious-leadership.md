# Conscious Leadership

> A leadership framework centered on self-awareness and choosing to operate from openness and curiosity (above the line) rather than defensiveness and reactivity (below the line).

## Content

Conscious Leadership was developed by Jim Dethmer and Diana Chapman (The Conscious Leadership Group) and is articulated in their book *The 15 Commitments of Conscious Leadership*. Multiple Lenny's Podcast guests have cited this framework as transformative.

### The Core Distinction: Above / Below the Line

The foundational concept is a simple binary:
- **Above the line**: open, curious, committed to learning — even when challenged or wrong
- **Below the line**: closed, defensive, committed to being right — even when learning is available

The goal is not to be above the line always (impossible) but to:
1. Notice when you've gone below the line
2. Choose to return above the line as quickly as possible
3. Build a culture where this shift is normalized, not shameful

### Drama Triangle (Below the Line)
When a team or individual is below the line, they tend to cycle through three roles:
- **Victim**: "This is happening to me; I have no power"
- **Villain**: "This other person/team/circumstance is the problem"
- **Hero**: "I'll save everyone from this situation"

Conscious Leadership replaces the drama triangle with the empowerment triangle: Creator, Challenger, Coach.

### Emotions as Data
A core commitment of Conscious Leadership is treating emotions as information rather than noise to be suppressed. Each emotion carries a signal:
- **Fear** → something wants attention
- **Sadness** → something wants to be let go of
- **Anger** → something is not of service
- **Joy** → something wants to be celebrated
- **Creative energy** → something wants to be born

This emotional wisdom framework (also articulated by [[john-mark-nickels]]) reframes emotions as functional data in the workplace rather than unprofessional intrusions.

### The 15 Commitments
The framework includes 15 specific commitments, ranging from "take radical responsibility" to "express your authentic feelings" to "practice integrity." The thread running through all 15: move from fear-based to trust-based leadership.

## Application in Product/Tech

Both [[john-mark-nickels]] (CPO-level exec at Uber, Waymo, DoorDash) and [[jonny-miller]] (nervous system coach) describe Conscious Leadership as the conceptual foundation for working with emotions in high-stakes professional environments.

Nickels applies it to:
- Product reviews where fear creates defensive responses
- Leadership team dynamics where below-the-line patterns create organizational drag
- Personal decisions under pressure

Miller's nervous system work provides the physiological complement: Conscious Leadership gives the conceptual framework; body-based practices (breathwork, interoception) give the physiological tools to actually execute the shift.
