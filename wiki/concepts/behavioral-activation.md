# Behavioral Activation

> A CBT technique — applied to workplace leadership by Hilary Gridley — based on the principle that behavior changes emotion, not the reverse. Act your way into feeling better rather than waiting to feel better before acting.

## Content

Behavioral activation is a first-line intervention in cognitive behavioral therapy for depression and anxiety. The clinical insight: when people feel bad, they withdraw from activity, which makes them feel worse. The intervention is to schedule and execute behavioral activation regardless of mood — doing generates the positive feedback that eventually lifts the mood.

**[[hilary-gridley]]** adapted this for workplace leadership, specifically for the recurring experience of receiving negative feedback or spiraling into negative professional narratives.

### The Leadership Application

**The "take-a-punch" concept**: When you receive criticism or notice yourself trapped in a negative professional narrative — "people think I don't know what I'm talking about," "I'm not being heard," "my credibility is under attack" — the instinct is to:
1. Litigate the narrative mentally (is this true? why did they say that?)
2. Wait until you feel more confident before acting
3. Defend yourself by arguing against the narrative directly

The behavioral activation alternative: **take behavioral action first**, regardless of how you feel.
- Fear is "people don't trust my judgment" → ship something impressive
- Fear is "I'm not being heard" → produce a compelling artifact that forces attention
- Fear is "I lack technical credibility" → demonstrate technical depth in your next interaction

The behavior comes first. The changed emotion follows from the behavior, not the other way around.

### Counter-Programming Negative Narratives

A complementary technique: when a negative narrative is strong, explicitly hunt for counter-evidence.

- Notice the narrative ("I'm not being heard")
- Identify three specific recent moments that contradict it
- Write them down

The goal is not toxic positivity — it's accuracy. Negative narratives are rarely the full truth. Training yourself to look for counter-evidence weakens the narrative's grip without requiring you to believe it's false.

### Mental Model Transparency as Behavioral Tool

Hilary's weekly team note — sharing key conversations, biggest questions, things she's working through — is itself a behavioral activation tool at the team level. By making her mental model visible before it's fully formed, she:
1. Reduces the information asymmetry between leader and team
2. Models uncertainty and iterative thinking (making it safe for others to do the same)
3. Short-circuits narratives her team might form about her decisions ("why did she do that?")

### Connection to Product Design

Behavioral activation maps to a core product design principle: **do is better than show is better than tell.** (Hilary's formulation, echoing Hila Qu's "remove friction, give warm start.") Designing for users to *do something* immediately creates the positive experience that generates engagement — waiting for users to feel ready or learn first is the product equivalent of waiting to feel better before acting.
