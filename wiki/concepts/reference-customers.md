# Reference Customers

> The gold standard of product discovery: customers who have used your solution, love it enough to tell others, and will stake their reputation on recommending it.

## Content

Christian Idiodi calls the reference customer the "holy grail of product work." The concept integrates discovery, validation, and go-to-market into a single practice.

**Definition**: A reference customer has used your solution, loves it, and is willing to tell others about it. They put their reputation on the line as an endorser. This willingness is the ultimate proof of value — more meaningful than any NPS score or usability test.

**Why it matters**: Just because someone can use your product doesn't mean they will use it, choose it, or pay for it. People say things in research that differ from what they do. The reference customer is revealed by behavior, not stated preference.

**The steakhouse test**: You're unlikely to try a restaurant with one or two reviews (maybe it's the chef and their spouse). You need to see enough reviews from people who look like you to feel confident. The same dynamic governs enterprise software purchases — "if I see five or six people that look like me already have this, then I feel confident making the argument to my company."

**Target thresholds**:
- **B2B**: 6–8 reference customers before declaring early product-market fit
- **B2C**: 15–25 reference customers before scaling

If you cannot find even 6 people who have the problem and love your solution, the problem may not be worth solving.

**The discovery process**: Immerse yourself with real users who have the problem. Don't leave until you've solved it. Repeat until you reach the threshold. This is "pressure-cooked discovery" — when researchers were surrounded by COVID patients, vaccine development compressed dramatically because the research was immersed in the environment of the problem.

**Connection to sales**: Product teams lose influence when sales has to force-sell a product. Reference customers reverse this: salespeople stop selling and start curating social proof. The product team's job is to create the first customers; everyone else's job is to capture the value.
